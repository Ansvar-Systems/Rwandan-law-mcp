Article One: Purpose of this Law
This Law determines the organization, powers
and functioning of the High Council of
National Public Prosecution Authority.
Article 2: Mission of the High Council of the
National Public Prosecution Authority
The High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority has the mission
provide general policy guidelines and to ensure
smooth functioning of the prosecution services
throughout the country.

Article 3: Members of the High Council
the National Public Prosecution Authority
The High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority is composed of
following members:
1° the Minister in charge of justice;
2° the Prosecutor General;
3° the Deputy Prosecutor General;
4° the Ombudsman;
5° the Chairperson of the National
Commission for Human Rights;
6° the Commissioner General of Rwanda

7° the Military Prosecutor General;
8° Authority in charge of investigation

10° two (2) Prosecutors at the intermediate

the same level;
11° two (2) Prosecutors at the primary level
elected by their peers working at the
same level;
12° two (2) Assistants to the National
Public Prosecution Authority elected
by their peers;
13° the Rector of the Institute of Legal
Practice and Development;
14° the Dean of the Faculty of Law from
public universities and institutions
higher learning;
15° one (1) Dean of the Faculty of Law
from approved private universities and
institutions
of

learning

16° the President of the Bar Association.
At least thirty percent (30%) of members of
High Council of the National Prosecution
Authority shall be women.
The Minister in charge of justice is
Chairperson of the High Council of the National
Prosecution Authority while the Prosecutor
General is its Deputy Chairperson.
Article 4: Communication of posts subject
election and electoral timetable
The Executive Secretary shall inform in writing
all institutions where candidates to the High
Council of the National Public Prosecution
Authority come from that the posts subject
election fall vacant and the electoral timetable,
sixty (60) days before the election is held.
Article 5: Requirements for Prosecutor
Assistant to the National Public Prosecution
Authority running for a seat in the High
Council of the National Public Prosecution
Authority membership

Any Prosecutor or any Assistant to the National
Public Prosecution Authority who intends
submit his or her candidacy to be elected
member of the High Council of the National
Public Prosecution Authority must:
1° have at least three (3) years’ experience
at a post of Prosecutor or of Assistant
of Public Prosecution;
2° neither have been subjected to
institutional disciplinary sanction
the High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority nor convicted
any offence;
3° inform the Executive Secretariat of
High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority in writing
least thirty (30) days before
election.
Article 6: Voting procedure
Election of members of the High Council
the National Public Prosecution Authority
requires the quorum of three quarters (3/4)
the electoral college. Members are elected by

absolute majority of the members present and
through secret ballot.
In case candidates get equal votes, the candidate
who has accumulated the most experience
public prosecution functions or in National
Public Prosecution assistance service becomes
the member of the High Council of the National
Public Prosecution Authority.
Election of members of the High Council of
National Public Prosecution Authority referred
to under Article 3 of this Law is organized and
supervised by the Chairperson of the High
Council of the National Public Prosecution
Authority or by any other person he/she may
delegates to do so.
Article 7: Term of office of members of the
High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority
The term of office of elected members of
High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority is three (3) years
renewable once at that level.

the High Council of the National Public

Article 9: Replacement of members of the
High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority
If an elected member of the High Council of
National Public Prosecution Authority loses
membership when his/her term of office has
least one (1) year before it expires, new
elections to fill the vacancy is organised within
thirty (30) days from the date the Executive
Secretariat has notified the Chairperson of
High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority of cessation
membership.
The elected member completes the remaining
term of office of his/her predecessor.
Section 2: Organs of the High Council of the
National Public Prosecution Authority and
their responsibilities
Article 10: Organs of the High Council of the
National Public Prosecution Authority
The High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority has the following organs:
1° the General assembly;
2° the Bureau;
3° Permanent committees.

Subsection One: General Assembly
Article 11: Composition of the General
Assembly
The General Assembly comprises all
members of the High Council of the National
Public Prosecution Authority.
Article 12: Responsibilities of the General
Assembly
The General Assembly is the supreme organ
the High Council of the National Public

The General Assembly has the following

National Public Prosecution Authority

advice on how to address weaknesses;
6° to provide proposals on any matter

Public Prosecution Authority;

8° to advise the Government on justice

the National Public

Subsection 2: Bureau
Article 13: Composition of the Bureau
The Bureau of the High Council of the National
Public Prosecution Authority comprises:
1° the Minister in charge of justice who

3° Chairpersons

Article 14: Responsibilities of the Bureau
Responsibilities of the Bureau are:
1° to organize meetings of the High
Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority;
2° to ensure the smooth running
meetings of the High Council of
National Public Prosecution Authority;

3° to appoint members of Committees
the High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority;
4° to ensure the implementation
decisions of the High Council of
National Public Prosecution Authority;
5° to promote collaborative relationships
between the High Council of
National Public Prosecution Authority
and other Government institutions.
Subsection 3: Permanent Committees
Article 15: Permanent Committees
The High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority comprises three
permanent Committees:
1° Committee in charge of discipline
Prosecutors and Assistants of
National Public Prosecution Authority;
2° Committee in charge of career
Prosecutors and Assistants of
National Public Prosecution authority;

3° Committee for the functioning of
National Public Prosecution Authority.
The High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority may establish ad hoc
Committee, if deemed necessary and specify
responsibilities and mandate.
Responsibilities and functioning of
Committees of the High Council of the National
Public Prosecution Authority are determined
its internal rules and regulations.
Article 16: Election of heads of the
permanent Committees
Chairpersons and Deputy Chairpersons
permanent Committees are elected in the first
meeting of the High Council of the National
Public Prosecution Authority.
Candidates for the posts of Chairperson and
Deputy Chairperson of a Permanent Committee
submit their candidacy during that meeting.
They are elected by secret ballot by an absolute
majority of the members present. When
absolute majority is not obtained, the voting
immediately repeated in accordance with
simple majority from the first two (2) winning

candidates of the first round of voting. In case
there is a tie vote, they draw lots to know
winner.
Members of the Commission elect among
themselves a Rapporteur during its first
meeting.
Section 3: Executive Secretariat
Article 17: Executive Secretariat
The Executive Secretariat of the High Council
of the National Public Prosecution Authority
responsible for the day to day activities of
High Council of the National Public

Executive Secretary appointed by a Prime
Minister’s Order after competition.
In his/her duties, the Executive Secretary
governed by the General Statutes for Public

Other staff members of the Executive Secretariat
are appointed in accordance with the law.

Article 18: Duties of the Executive Secretary
The Executive Secretary has the following
duties:
1° to serve as the Rapporteur of the High
Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority and the Bureau;
2° to receive and examine applications
Prosecutors and Assistants to National
Public Prosecution Authority running
for seats in the High Council of
National Public Prosecution Authority;
3° to receive and examine applications
the posts of Prosecutors and Assistants
to the National Public Prosecution
Authority;
4° to determine modalities for
organization of staff recruitment;
5° to prepare documents to be considered
by the High Council of the National
public Prosecution Authority;

6° to prepare and send meeting notices
members of the High Council of
National Public Prosecution Authority;
7° to notify decisions of the High Council
of the National Public Prosecution
Authority to concerned persons;
8° to perform other duties as may
assigned by the High Council of
National Public Prosecution Authority.

POWERS

Section One: Powers of the High Council

Article 19: Powers of the High Council of the
National Public Prosecution Authority
The High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority has the following
powers:
1° to examine matters relating to
functioning of the National Public
Prosecution Authority;
2° to provide advice, either on its own
initiative or upon request, on all matters
pertaining to the functioning of
criminal justice system;
3° to make decisions relating to
exercise of the prosecution function and
the conduct of Prosecutors, Assistants
the National Public Prosecution
Authority and personnel of the National
Public Prosecution Authority falling
within its jurisdiction with the exception
of the Prosecutor General and
Deputy Prosecutor General;
4° to provide advice on the candidacy
the Prosecutor General and Deputy
Prosecutor General.

Section 2: Functioning of the High Council
the National Public Prosecution Authority
Article 20: Convening and holding meetings
of the High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority
A meeting of the High Council of the National
Public Prosecution Authority is convened and
presided over by its Chairperson and in his/her
absence, by the Deputy Chairperson.
The invitation letter addressed to members
the High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority specifies the agenda,
time and venue and is sent to its members fifteen
(15) days before the meeting.
Members of the High Council of the National
Public Prosecution Authority are not authorized
to be represented in its meetings.
The High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority holds its ordinary
meeting once a quarter if at least two thirds (2/3)
of its members are present.

However, when a meeting is convened for
second time, it takes place regardless of
members present.
The High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority may also hold
extraordinary session, whenever necessary,
either on the Chairperson’s initiative or upon
request by at least one third (1/3) of
members. In that case, it only considers items
on the agenda and the deadline for invitation
to the meeting provided under Paragraph 2
this Article may not apply.
Article 21: Making decisions
The High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority takes decisions
consensus; otherwise, the decisions are taken
an absolute majority of the members present.
However, a decision to remove a Prosecutor
from office is taken by a majority vote of two
thirds (2/3) of the members present through
secret ballot.
The decisions of the High Council of
National Public Prosecution Authority
signed by its Chairperson and the Rapporteur
and are immediately implemented unless they
are subject to the approval by another authority.

However, these decisions must first be notified
to the concerned party.
Article 22: Minutes
The meeting of the High Council of the National
Public Prosecution Authority is recorded
minutes which are signed by its Chairperson and
the Rapporteur.
Such minutes are submitted to all members
the High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority within thirty (30) days
from the date the meeting was held.
Article 23: Professional secrecy
Members of the High Council of the National
Public Prosecution Authority and any other
person attending its meetings are bound to
secrecy of matters discussed once in service and
after the term of their office.
Any person who discloses the secret on matters
discussed in the meeting of the High Council
the National Public Prosecution Authority
punished in accordance with the law.

Article 24: Funds allocated to functioning
the High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority
The funds budgeted for the functioning of
High Council of the National Public
Prosecution Authority are included in
budget of the National Public Prosecution
Authority.
Article 25: Sitting allowance
A Presidential Order determines the sitting
allowances for members of the High Council
the National Public Prosecution Authority
present in its meetings and those of
committees.
CHAPTER IV: FINAL PROVISIONS
Article 26: Drafting, consideration and
adoption of this Law
This Law was drafted, considered and adopted
in Ikinyarwanda.

Article 27: Repealing provision
All prior legal provisions contrary to this Law
are repealed.
Article 28: Commencement
This Law comes into force on the date of
publication in the Official Gazette of
Republic of Rwanda.

GENERAL
Article One: Purpose of this Law
This Law establishes taxes on income.
Article 2: Scope of this Law
This Law relates to the following taxes
income:
1° personal income tax;
2° corporate income tax;
3° withholding tax;
4° capital gain tax.

Article 3: Definitions
For the purposes of this Law, the following
terms shall have the following meanings:
1° long term contract: a contract
manufacture, installation or construction,
or for the performance of related services,
which is not completed in the tax period
which work under the contract
commenced, excluding contracts estimated
to be completed within the twelve (12)
months as of the date on which work under
the contract commenced;
2° small business: business activities which
result into a turnover ranging between
twelve million and one Rwandan francs
(Frw 12,000,001) and twenty million
Rwandan francs (Frw 20,000,000) per each
tax period;
3° micro-enterprise: business activities
which result into a turnover equal to or
less than twelve million Rwandan francs
(Frw 12,000,000) per each tax period;

4° permanent establishment: a known fixed
place of business through which
business which gives rise to income
wholly or partially carried on;
5° controlled transaction: any income-
generating transaction carried out between
related persons;
6° arm’s length principle: a principle
according to which the conditions of
income-generating transaction between
related persons do not differ from
conditions that would have been applied
between independent persons in comparable
transactions carried out under comparable
circumstances;
7° transfer pricing: conditions, including
prices of commercial or financial
transactions between related persons,
whether residing in the country or abroad;
8° qualified pension fund: any fund, with
exception of the public institution in charge
of social security, which fulfils
following conditions:
a. established according to Rwandan laws;

b. operated for the purpose of providing
pension payments to residents in
country or abroad;
c. has effective management in Rwanda
any time during the tax period;
9° foreign development financial institution:
institution carrying out financial activities
with public funds, for which administrative
decisions are made by Government
representatives and having public interest
missions, such as sustainable development
and poverty reduction;
10° Minister: the Minister in charge of taxes;
11° casual employee: a person who performs
labour that does not require special skills,
and who is employed by an employer for
aggregate period not exceeding thirty (30)
days during a tax period;
12° employer: any person who pays to a worker
income arising from the work he/she
performed or will perform for him/her;

13° first employer: an employer who pays
an employee an annual professional income
arising from a permanent work or from
longer contract than other employers may
offer;
14° employee: any person who undertakes
work for another person for payment, under
the supervision and in accordance with
directives of his/her employer in relation
with his/her work;
15° person: an individual, companies, de facto
companies, cooperatives, partnerships,
subsidiaries, permanent establishments and
any other association of persons regardless
of its status;
16° related person: any person who acts or
likely to act in accordance with
directives, opinion or wishes of another
person when such directives, opinion
wishes are communicated or not
communicated to them. In particular,
following persons are regarded as related
person:
a. an individual and his/her spouse and
their direct lineal ascendants or direct

lineal descendants, and their relatives
the collateral lineage until at least the
degree;
b. one of the persons who participates
directly or indirectly in the management,
control or capital of the other;
c. third person who participates directly
indirectly in the management, control
capital or both control and capital
another person;
d. such persons referred to under sub-items
a), b) and c) who participate directly
indirectly in the management, control
capital of an enterprise;
17° taxpayer: any person who pays the tax
accordance with this Law;
18° liberal profession: profession exercised
the basis of special skills, in an independent
manner, in offering services to the clients.

Article 4: Residence
An individual is considered to be a resident
Rwanda if he/she fulfils one of the following
conditions:
1° he/she has a permanent residence
Rwanda;
2° he/she has a habitual abode in Rwanda;
3° he/she is a Rwandan representing Rwanda
abroad.
An individual who stays in Rwanda for more
than one hundred eighty three (183) days
twelve (12) month period, either continuously
or intermittently, is considered to be a resident
in Rwanda for the tax period in which the twelve
(12) month period has ended.
A person other than a natural person
considered to be a resident in Rwanda during
tax period if:
1° it is a company or an association established
according to Rwandan laws;

2° he/she has a place of effective management
in Rwanda at any time during that
period;
3° it is a Rwanda Government company.
An order of the Minister determines
person’s permanent residence and the location
of the effective place of management.
Article 5: Source of taxable income
Income taxable in Rwanda includes
following activities performed in Rwanda
any person and activities performed abroad by
resident of Rwanda:
1° services and employment;
2° activities of a crafts person, singer, artist and
a player;
3° sports, cultural and leisure activities;
4° activities carried on by a non-resident
through a permanent establishment
Rwanda;

5° use, sale, lease and free transfer of business
movable assets;
6° sale, lease and free transfer of immovable
assets allocated to the business;
7° crop farming, animal farming, fishing and
forestry activities;
8° usufruct and other rights attached
immovable assets and their sale if such
rights are allocated to the business;
9° investments in shares of companies;
10° direct or indirect sale or transfer of shares
debentures;
11° change of profits into shares that increases
the capital of partners;
12° distribution of profits among partners;
13° lending, deposits and other similar income-
generating activities;

14° transfer, sale and lease of intellectual
property;
15° any other income generating activities.
However, all payments made by a resident
Rwanda on services performed abroad, other
than those consumed abroad, constitute
taxable income.
Article 6: Permanent establishment
For the purposes of this Law, one of
following areas is considered as a permanent
establishment:
1° a place of management;
2° a branch;
3° a factory or a workshop;
4° a mine, a quarry or any other place for
exploitation of natural resources;
5° a site set for construction, construction site
or a place where supervision or assembly
works are carried out;

6° place of provision of services, including
consulting services, carried on by a person,
with the support of employees or other
personnel, for more than ninety (90) days
a twelve (12) month period, either
continuously or intermittently.
A person is considered as not to be
permanent establishment if that person:
1° uses facilities solely for the purpose
storage of goods or merchandise belonging
to him/her;
2° maintains a stock of goods or merchandise
belonging to him/her solely for the purpose
of storage;
3° maintains a stock of goods or merchandise
belonging to him/her solely for the purpose
of processing by another person;
4° has a place of operation aimed purposely
purchasing goods or merchandise
collecting information related to his/her
business;

5° has a place of operation solely for the
purpose of performing, within the context
of his/her activities, any other activities
a preparatory nature or intended to make
them more effective.
Subject to the provisions of items 1° and 2°
Paragraph 2 of this Article, where a person
except an independent person, concerned with
item 5° of Paragraph 2 of this Article acts
behalf of a person and who has capacity to make
contracts in the name of that person, that person
is considered as if he/she owns a permanent
establishment in respect of activities his/her
agent undertakes for the person except if such
activities of the agent are limited to those
mentioned in Paragraph 2 of this Article.
A person is not considered as a permanent
establishment if he/she only carries out
activities through a broker, general commission
agent or any other private agent in accordance
with procedures of the ordinary course of
activities of such an agent.
The fact that a company controls or is controlled
by another company shall not of itself constitute
either company a permanent establishment
the other.

Article 7: Foreign tax credit refund
If during a tax period, a resident in Rwanda
generates income derived from taxable
activities performed abroad, in accordance with
Articles 4 and 5 of this Law, the income
payable by that resident in respect of that
income is reduced by the amount of foreign
payable on such income. The amount of foreign
tax payable shall be substantiated by appropriate
evidence such as tax declaration, a withholding
tax certificate or other similar acceptable
document.
However, the reduction of the income
provided for under Paragraph one of this Article
shall not exceed the tax payable in Rwanda
income from abroad.
Article 8: Tax period
The tax is calculated for the calendar year,
which starts on 1 January and ends on
December.
Upon written request, the Minister may allow
writing a taxpayer to apply any other twelve

(12) months period as a tax period, if
taxpayer fulfils the following conditions:
1° he/she is an entity subject to corporate
income tax;
2° he/she is required to keep books of accounts
according to generally accepted accounting
principles;
3° he/she presents sound reasons to change
his/her tax period.
Where a taxpayer’s tax period changes,
period from the start of the usual tax period
the date of such a change is considered as
usual tax period and is separately subject
taxation.

The personal income tax is calculated, reduced
by the following:
1° the tax withheld on taxable income; with
the exception of the tax withheld
employment income, as well as on income

applied;
2° the prepayments made every quarter.
The personal income tax referred to
Paragraph One of this Article must be paid

Any withholding or prepayment that exceeds

considered by the Tax Administration
liquidation of tax arrears or as the payment
any future tax obligations.
Upon a written request by the taxpayer and upon

discharged, the Tax Administration returns
the taxpayer the excess amount within thirty
(30) days from the date of receipt of the request.
Section 2: Employment income
Article 15: Components of employment
income
Employment income includes all payments paid
to an employee by his/her employer in cash
in kind in relation to the work performed. Those
payments are composed of the following:
1° wages, salary, leave pay, sick pay and
medical allowance, payment in lieu of leave
for an employee who stops working before
benefiting from his/her annual leave, sitting
allowances, commissions, bonuses and
gratuity;

2° allowances relating to the cost of living,
subsistence
allowances,
housing
allowances, and entertainment or travel
allowances;
3° any discharge or reimbursement of expenses
incurred by the employee or an associate;
4° payments to the employee working
exceptional conditions of employment;
5° payments for redundancy or loss
termination of contract;
6° pension payments;
7° other payments made in respect of previous,
current or future employment.
Article 16: Payments
employment income tax
exempted
from
The following payments are not included in
calculation of taxable employment income:

associate:
a. wholly for business activities of
employer;
b. those that are deducted or would
deductible
in

employee’s income from all his/her

2° contributions made by the employer for
employee to the public institution in charge
of social security;
3° pension payment from the public institution
in charge of social security or from
qualified pension fund;
4° employment income received by
employee who is not a Rwandan citizen
from a foreign Government or a non-
governmental organization under
agreement signed by the Government
Rwanda, when the income is received
the performance of aid services in Rwanda;

5° employment income received from
employer who is not a resident in Rwanda
by a non-resident individual for
performance of services in Rwanda, unless
such services are related to a permanent
establishment of the employer in Rwanda.
Article 17: Persons
employment income tax
exempted
from
Persons that are exempted from employment

Rwanda;
2° any other individual employed in any

is a national of that State and who owns

3° a non-citizen individual employed by

Benefits in kind received by an employee

consideration of market value as follows:

benefits on a loan including advance on

given to an employee valued at a difference
between:

been paid by the employee during

employee in that month;
3° there shall be added to the taxable income
amount meant for use or availability for use
of premises including or excluding any
household equipment of other contents
an employer for residential occupation by

twenty percent (20%) of the employment
income excluding benefits in kind.
Subject to provisions of Paragraph One of this
Article, following benefits in kind
considered in the same manner as benefits
employer gives to an employee:
1° benefits in kind provided by an employer
a person related to an employee when there
is no service rendered;
2° benefits in kind provided by a company
one of its members.

However, a rent of house or motor vehicle
directly paid by an employer for an employee,
is taxed as any allowance referred to
Article 15 of this Law.
Section 3: Business profits
Article 19: Computation of business profits
Business profits are determined as the income
from all business activities reduced by
business expenses. Business profit also includes
proceeds of sale of any business asset and
proceeds from asset sharing received during
tax period.
Business profits are determined per tax period
on the basis of the profit or loss account drawn
up in accordance with Generally Accepted
Accounting Principles, subject to the provisions
of this Law.
The Tax Administration may use any other
accounting method or other source

information in accordance with the law,
ensure the accuracy of the taxpayer’s profit.
Article 20: Tax exemption for income
accrued from savings and employees’ shares
scheme within a company
Income accruing from savings in collective
investment schemes and employees’ shares
scheme within a company are exempted from
income tax.
Article 21: Tax exemption for profit
agricultural and livestock activities
Income earned by an agriculturalist or
pastoralist on agricultural or livestock activities
is exempt if the turnover from agricultural
livestock activities do not exceed twelve million
Rwanda francs (Frw 12,000,000) in a tax period.
In case the turnover exceeds twelve million
Rwandan francs (Frw 12,000,000), the latter
amount is excluded from the taxable income.

Article 22: Accounting for small business
A taxpayer running a small business
mentioned under item 2° of Article 3, and
Paragraph 2 and 3 of Article 12 of this Law, may
decide to pay a tax on actual profit derived from
business activities in accordance with
simplified accounting method to be determined
by an Order of the Minister. Such an option may
be subject to change after three (3) years.
Article 23: Profit on assets in foreign
currency
During the conclusion of the tax period,
assets in foreign currency, including claims and
debts, are valued at the exchange rate on the last
day of the tax period. The profits or loss therein
are included in the assessment of business profit
for that period.
Article 24: Computation of profits of
business bound by long-term contract
Business profits relating to a long-term contract
are computed on the basis of the percentage
activities completed during any tax period.

The percentage of activities completed during
any tax period is determined by comparing
total expenses allocated to the contract and
incurred before the end of the tax period with
the estimated total contract expenses including
any variations of fluctuations.
A loss in a tax period in which a long-term
contract is completed may be carried back and
offset against previously taxed business profit
from that contract to the extent it cannot
absorbed by business profit in the tax period
completion.
Article 25: Deductions from taxable income
In determining profits on business activities,
expenses deducted from taxable income shall
fulfil the following:
1° they are incurred for the direct purpose
the business and they are directly chargeable
to the income;

2° they correspond to a real expense and can
substantiated with proper purchase receipts;
3° they lead to a decrease in the net assets
the business;
4° they are used for activities related to the
period in which they are incurred.
Article 26: Non-deductible expenses from
taxable income
The following expenses are not deductible from
taxable income:
1° dividends declared and profits paid-out
their beneficiaries;
2° reserve allowances, savings and other
special-purpose funds, unless otherwise
provided for by this Law;
3° fines and similar penalties;

4° donations, save for donations given to non-
profit making organisations the value
which does not exceed one percent (1%)
the turnover;
5° income tax paid in accordance with this Law
or paid abroad on business profit and
recoverable Value Added Tax (VAT);
6° personal consumption expenses;
7° entertainment expenses save for expenses
on general sporting activities for employees;
8° twenty per cent (20%) of expenses paid
business overheads as in the case
telephone, water, electricity and fuel whose
private and business use cannot
practically separable;
9° management, technical services and royalty
fees paid to a non-resident person exceeding
two percent (2%) of the turnover of
taxpayer;

10° interest arising from loans between related
persons either paid or due on a total loan
which is greater than four (4) times
amount of equity. This equity should not
include provisions or reserves according
the balance sheet, which is drawn up
accordance with the Generally Accepted
Accounting Principles.
The provisions under item 10° of this Article
not apply to commercial banks, financial
institutions and insurance companies.
Article 27: Trading stock value
The trading stock is valued at the lower price
between the cost price and the market price
the last day of the tax period.
Work in progress is valued at cost price.

Article 28: Depreciation
In the determination of business profit,
depreciation for business assets is deducted
from taxable income.
Buildings, heavy industrial equipment and
machineries are depreciated annually, each
its own, on the basis of the rate of depreciation
equivalent to five percent (5%) of the cost
acquisition,
construction,
refining,
rehabilitation or reconstruction.
Intangible assets including goodwill that
purchased from a third party are depreciated
annually, each on its own, on the basis of
rate of depreciation of ten percent (10%) of
cost of acquisition, refining, rehabilitation
reconstruction.
Information and communication systems whose
life is over ten (10) years are depreciated
annually on the basis of the rate of depreciation
of ten percent (10%) of the cost of acquisition.
The assets in the following two categories
depreciated in a pooling system on the basis
the following rates:

1 º computers and accessories, information and
communication systems whose life is under
ten (10) years: fifty percent (50%);
2 º any other business asset: twenty five percent
(25%).
Depreciation of leased assets shall be allowed
the lessee in case of finance lease and to
lessor in case of operating lease.
Land, fine arts, antiquities, jewellery and any
other assets that are not subject to wear and tear
or obsolescence are not depreciated.
Article 29: Depreciation basis
The depreciation basis for assets is
acquisition value.
However, for the assets depreciated in
pooling system, their depreciation basis is
book value in the balance sheet at the beginning
of the tax period:

1° increased by the cost of assets acquired
created and the cost of refining,
rehabilitation and reconstruction of
assets in the tax period;
2° decreased by the sale price of assets sold and
the compensation received for the loss
assets due to natural calamities or other
conversion during the tax period.
If the depreciation basis is less than zero (0), that
amount is added to the business profit and
depreciation basis becomes zero (0).
If the depreciation basis does not exceed five
hundred thousand (500,000) Rwanda francs,
entire depreciation basis is deemed to be
deductible expense.
Article 30: Training and research expenses
During a tax period, all training and research
expenses incurred by a taxpayer, which promote
business activities, are considered as deductible

from taxable income in accordance with
provisions of Article 25 of this Law, if they have
been declared and planned in the activity plan
that tax period.
Expenses on training and research for
promotion of business activities do not concern
the purchase of land, houses, buildings and
other immovable properties including refining,
rehabilitation and reconstruction as well
assets exploration expenses.
Article 31: Bad debts
In the determination of business profit,
deduction is allowed for bad debts if
following conditions are fulfilled:
1 º if an amount corresponding to the debt was
previously included in the income of
taxpayer;
2 º if the debt is written off in the books
accounts of the taxpayer;
3 º if the taxpayer has taken all possible steps
pursuing payment and has shown a court

decision declaring the insolvency of his/her
debtor.
However, for an individual whose debt is less
than three million Rwandan francs (Frw
3,000,000) in addition to the conditions referred
to in points 1° and 2° of Paragraph One of this
Article, the taxpayer must provide proof that
has taken all reasonable steps over a period
three (3) years to recover the debt.
Notwithstanding the provisions of Paragraph
One of this Article, commercial banks and
leasing entities duly licensed as such
allowed to deduct from taxable income, any
increase of the mandatory reserve for non-
performing loans as required by the directives
related to management of bank loans and similar
institutions of the National Bank of Rwanda.
The business profit is increased by the entire
amount recovered from bad debts deducted
from such reserves.

Article 32: Loss carried forward
If the computation of business profit results in
loss in a tax period, the loss may be deducted
from the business profit in the next five (5)
periods, earlier losses being deducted before
later losses.
However, the Tax Administration may
authorise the taxpayer who so applied for,
loss carried forward of more than five (5)
periods if s/he fulfils requirements determined
by an Order of the Minister.
During a tax period, foreign sourced losses
cannot be deducted from either present or future
domestic sourced business profits.
If during a tax period, the direct and indirect
ownership of the share capital or the voting
rights of a company, whose shares are not traded
on a recognized stock exchange changes more
than twenty five per cent (25%) by value or
number, the provisions of Paragraph one of this
Article cease to apply to losses incurred by that
company in the tax period and previous
periods.

Article 33: Transfer pricing between related
persons
Related persons involved in controlled
transactions must have documents justifying
that their prices are applied according to arm’s
length principle.
Failure to do so, the Tax Administration adjusts
transactions prices in accordance with general
rules on transfer pricing, issued by an Order
the Minister.
Article 34: Quarterly prepayment
During the current tax period, the taxpayer pays
to the account of the Tax Administration before
and not later than 30 June, 30 September and
December of the year of taxable business
activities, twenty five (25%) per cent of the
liability as calculated in the tax declaration
the previous tax period. This amount is reduced
by the tax withheld in that quarter, unless
taxable income is not included in the total
taxable income.

If the taxpayer uses a tax period that does not
coincide with the calendar year, the quarterly
prepayments as calculated according
Paragraph One of this Article shall be paid not
later than the last day of the sixth month (6),
ninth month (9) and the twelfth (12) month
the tax period of which he / she is allowed.
If the taxpayer started his/her business activities
during the previous tax period, the quarterly
prepayment is calculated as twenty five (25%)
percent of the amount of tax liability of
previous tax period divided by the number
months during which the taxpayer carried on
or her business activities and multiplied
twelve (12).
Section 4: Investment income
Article 35: Components of investment
income
Investment income includes any payments
cash or in kind to an individual in the form
financial interest, dividends, proceeds from sale
or transfer of shares, royalty, or rent which has
not been taxed as business income

accordance with the provisions of section 3
this chapter.
Article 36: Capital gain tax
Without prejudice to the provisions
Paragraph one of Article 19 of this Law, capital
gain tax is charged on the sale or transfer
shares.
The capital gain on sale or transfer of shares
the difference between the acquisition value
shares and their selling or transfer price.
Article 37:Tax rate on capital gain
The tax rate on capital gain is five percent (5%)
applicable on the gain.

Article 38: Withholding and declaration
capital gain tax
The capital gain tax on the sale or transfer
shares shall be withheld by the company within
which the transaction occurred.
The company within which the sale or transfer
of shares occurred shall declare and pay
capital gain tax to the Tax Administration
within fifteen (15) days following the month
which the sale or transfer of shares occurred.
Article 39: Exemption from capital gain tax
Capital gain from the sale or transfer of shares
on the capital market and capital gain from
sale or transfer of units of the collective
investment schemes, is exempted from capital
gain tax.
Article 40: Financial income
Financial income includes:
1° income from loans;

2° income from deposits;
3° income from guarantees;
4° income from government securities,
income from bonds, negotiable securities
issued by the Government, securities
issued by public and private companies,
well as income from cash negotiable
securities.
Article 41: Dividend income
Dividend income includes income from shares
in any societies, other similar income that may
be generated by all entities that pay corporate
income tax, as well as the outstanding balance
after the taxation of income from the correction
made by the Tax Administration in the transfer
pricing.
Article 42: Royalty income
Royalty income includes:

1° all payments of any kind received as a prize
for the use of, or the right to use, any
copyright of literary, craftsmanship
scientific work including cinematograph
films, films, or tapes used for radio
television broadcasting;
2° any payment received from using
trademark, design or model, computer
application and invention patent;
3° the price of using, or of the right to use
industrial, commercial or scientific
equipment or for using information
concerning industrial, commercial
scientific knowledge;
4° payments from natural resource use.
Article 43: Taxation of rental income
All revenues derived from rent of machinery
and other equipment including agriculture and

livestock equipment in Rwanda, are included
taxable income, reduced by:
1° ten percent (10%) of gross revenue
deemed expense;
2° interest paid on loans;
3° depreciation expenses calculated
provided for by Article 28 of this Law.
CHAPTER III: CORPORATE INCOME
TAX
Section one: General provisions
Article 44: Base for corporate income tax
The corporate income tax is levied on business
profits received by companies.
Article 45: Taxpayers
The following entities are subject to corporate
income tax:

1° companies established in accordance with
Rwandan or foreign law;
2° cooperative societies and their branches;
3° commercial public institutions;
4° partnerships;
5° entities established by Districts and the City
of Kigali, as long as these entities perform
an income generating activity;
6° de facto companies or associations and any
other type of entities that are established
realize profits.
Entities mentioned under items 1°, 2° and 3°
Paragraph One of this Article, are deemed
conduct business with their whole equity. This
means that all their revenues are received from
their business activities.

Article 46: Entities which are exempted from
corporate income tax
The Government of Rwanda and the following
entities are exempted from corporate income
tax:
1° the City of Kigali and Districts;
2° the National Bank of Rwanda;
3° entities that carry out only activities of
religious,
humanitarian,
charitable,
scientific or educational character, unless
the revenue received exceeds
corresponding expenses or if those entities
conduct a business;
4° international organizations, agencies
technical
cooperation
and
their
representatives, if such exemption
provided for by international agreements;
5° qualified pension funds;
6° public institution in charge of social
security;
7° Development Bank of Rwanda « BRD »;

8° Agaciro Development Fund Corporate
Trust;
9° Business Development Fund limited “BDF
Ltd”.
However, entities exempted from corporate
income tax are required to submit to the Tax
Administration their financial statements not
later than 31 March following the tax period.
Article 47: Zero-rated entities
Companies and cooperatives that carry out
micro finance activities approved by competent
authorities pay corporate income tax at the rate
of zero percent (0%) for a period of five
years from the time of their approval.
However, this period may be renewed where
entities fulfil the conditions prescribed by
Order of the Minister.
Entities referred to in Paragraph one of this
Article are required to submit to the Tax
Administration their financial statements not
later than 31 March following the tax period.

Article 48: Scope of tax liability to corporate
income tax
Resident entities are liable to corporate income
tax on business profit per tax period whether
from domestic or foreign operations.
However, dividends paid between resident
companies and which have been subject to
withholding tax are not included in corporate
taxable income.
Non-resident entities shall be liable to corporate
income tax on business profit per tax period,
which is equivalent to the income tax applicable
to resident entities, through their permanent
establishments in the country.
Article 49: Corporate income tax rate
Taxable Business profit is rounded down to
nearest one thousand Rwandan Francs
(Frw 1.000) and taxable at a rate of thirty
percent (30%).

Newly listed companies on capital market shall
be taxed for a period of five (5) years on
following rates:
1° twenty percent (20%) if those companies
sell at least forty percent (40%) of their
shares to the public;
2° twenty five percent (25%) if those
companies sell at least thirty percent (30%)
of their shares to the public;
3° twenty eight percent (28%) if those
companies sell at least twenty percent (20%)
of their shares to the public.
Article 50: Tax Declaration
A taxpayer who receives taxable business profit
prepares an annual tax declaration in accordance
with the procedure determined by the Tax
Administration and presents it, at the same time,
with the accounting balance sheet, profit and
loss statement for the tax period, the annexes
thereto, as well as any other relevant document
required by the Tax Administration, not later
than 31 st March of the following tax period.

The amount of tax to be paid is calculated on
basis of the annual basic declaration, reduced
by:
1° the tax withheld on taxable income; with
exception of the tax withheld
employment income, as well as on income
to which lump sum tax or flat tax
applied.
2° the prepayments made every quarter.
The tax is paid to the Tax Administration on
date the tax declaration is submitted.
If a withheld or prepaid tax exceeds the amount
of tax liability calculated on the basis
Paragraph One of this Article, it is considered
by the tax administration as liquidation of
arrears or as the payment of any future
obligations. Upon a written request by
taxpayer and upon satisfaction that prior
obligations have been discharged, the Tax
Administration returns to the taxpayer
excess amount within thirty (30) days from
date of receipt of the request.

Section 2: Corporate income
Article 51: Corporate income taxation
The corporate income is taxable in the same way
as personal income as provided for by
provisions of Chapter II of this Law dealing
with personal income tax, with the exception
the provisions of Article 12 as well as all other
provisions on employment income tax.
However the following companies shall pay
as follows:
1° small business companies pay a lump sum
tax of three per cent ( 3%) of the turnover;
2° micro-enterprise companies pay the flat
whose amount is indicated in accordance
with the table referred to under Article 12
this Law;
3° without prejudice to real profit tax regime,
companies operating in the Transport
persons and goods by road pay a flat
calculated as indicated in the annex of this
Law.

Article 52: Taxation of income from the rent
of movable and immovable assets
Income from the rent of movable and
immovable assets incorporated as assets
entities which are subject to corporate income
tax is consolidated in the total taxable income.
Article 53: Corporate restructuring
Companies are subject to restructuring
follows:
1° a merger of two or more resident
companies into a separate company;
2° the acquisition or a takeover of fifty percent
(50%) or more of shares or voting rights,
number or value in a resident company
exchange for shares of the purchasing
company;
3° the acquisition of fifty percent (50%)
more of the assets and liabilities of

resident company by another resident
company solely in exchange of shares
the purchasing company;
4° the acquisition of the entire company’s
assets so that its existence is replaced by
purchasing company;
5° splitting of a resident company into two
more resident companies.
Article 54:
companies
Taxation
of
restructured
In case of restructuring of companies,
transferring company is exempt from tax
respect of capital gains and losses realized
restructuring. The receiving company values
assets and liabilities involved at their book value
in the hands of the transferring company at
time of restructuring. The receiving company
depreciates the business assets according to
rules that would have applied to the transferring
company as if the restructuring did not take
place.

In case of restructuring, the receiving company
is entitled to carry over the reserves and
provisions created by the transferring company,
subject to the conditions that would have
applied to the transferring company as if
restructuring did not take place. The receiving
company assumes the rights and obligations
the transferring company in respect of such
reserves and provisions.
Article 55: Taxation in case of liquidation
For the purpose of liquidation of a company,
after payment of liabilities and distribution
dividends, the remainder, shall be considered
dividends on shares in the last tax period of
company’s existence.

Article 59: Tax on sitting allowance
Sitting allowance allocated to the members
the Board of Directors are taxable at a rate
thirty percent (30%).
Article 60: Withholding tax on payments
other methods of extinguishing an obligation
A withholding tax of fifteen percent (15%)
the total amount excluding Value Added Tax
(VAT) where applicable is levied on payments
or other methods of extinguishing an obligation
made by resident individuals including tax-
exempt entities, when such payments or other
methods of extinguishing an obligation
made to a person not registered in the Rwandan
tax administration or to a registered person who
does not have recent income tax declaration.
Payments or other methods of extinguishing
obligation subject to the withholding tax

fifteen percent (15%) are related to
following:
1° dividends, except income distributed to

Article 61: Withholding tax on goods
imported for commercial use
A withholding tax of five percent (5%) of
value of goods imported for commercial use
shall be paid at custom on the cost insurance and
freight (CIF) value before the goods are released
by customs.

The withholding tax referred to in Paragraph
one of this Article shall be deducted from
income tax when it is declared and paid.
Article 62: Withholding tax on public tenders
A withholding tax on public tenders of three
percent (3%) of the sum of invoice, excluding
the Value Added Tax (VAT), is retained when
public tenders are paid.
However, a tax of fifteen percent (15%) shall
withheld on public tenders if the recipient is not
registered with the Tax Administration or he/she
is registered but does not have his/her previous
income tax declaration.
A withholding tax on payment made to a person
registered with the Tax Administration shall
deducted from income tax during the income
declaration and its payment.

Article 63: Time
withholding taxes
for
declaration
The person who withholds taxes referred to
Articles 59, 60 and 62 of this Law is required
file a tax declaration and make payment
accordance with the procedures prescribed
the Tax Administration within a period
fifteen (15) days after the month in which
taxes were withheld.
Article 64: Persons
withholding taxes
exempted
from
The following taxpayers are exempted from
withholding taxes referred to in Articles 59,
and 62 of this Law:
1° those whose business profit is exempted
from taxation;
2° those who have tax clearance certificate
issued by the tax administration.
The Tax Administration may revoke a
clearance certificate at any time if the conditions
required by the tax administration are not
fulfilled.

Article 65: Failure to withhold tax
Any person who is required to withhold tax and
who fails to do so in accordance with this Law
is personally liable to pay to the Tax
Administration, the amount of tax which has not
been withheld including penalties and interest
on arrears.
However, the person who is liable to pay the
not withheld has the right to appeal for
amount of the tax imposed or to recover any
excess amount of tax paid.
Article 66: Records of payments and tax
withheld
Any person who pays a withholding tax keeps
and makes available to the Tax Administration,
for inspection and whenever necessary, records
in relation to each tax period showing
following:
1° payments made to taxpayer;
2° amount of tax withheld and paid.
A person who pays withholding tax shall keep
the records referred to in Paragraph One of this

Article for a period of ten (10) years after
end of the tax period to which the records relate.
The Tax Administration may require a person
who pays a withholding tax to provide a copy
records kept in accordance with Paragraph 2
this Article.

Article 67: Tax reduction or exemption for

Any tax reduction or exemption of income
for the purposes of promoting and facilitating
investment is carried out pursuant to the Law
investment promotion and facilitation.

This Law was drafted in English, considered
and adopted in Ikinyarwanda.

Law n o 16/2005 of 18/08/2005 on direct taxes
