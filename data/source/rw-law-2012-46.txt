The Chamber of Deputies, in its session of

Pursuant to the Constitution of the Republic

ADOPTS:
CHAPTER ONE: GENERAL PROVISIONS
Article One: Purpose of this Law
This Law determines organization, functioning
competence of the Council of pharmacists.
Article 2: Definitions of terms
Under this Law, the following terms shall have
following meanings:
1° Pharmacist: any person authorized to make
or dispense a medicine by virtue of his/her
academic qualification;
2° Council: Council of Pharmacists.
Article 3: Mission of the Council
The Council shall be responsible for ensuring
the rules, honor and dignity of the pharmacy
profession are complied with and ensure
protection of public health.

The Council shall ensure compliance with
principles of morality, integrity and dedication
essential to the practice of the pharmacy profession
and ensure that all its members comply with their
professional requirements and the laws
regulations governing pharmacists.
Article 4: Competence of the Council
The Council shall have the following competence:
1° to grant and revoke the authorization
practice the pharmacy profession;
2° to provide institutions of higher learning
advice with respect to pharmacy academic
programs;
3° to take disciplinary measures against
pharmacists.
Article 5: Autonomy of the Council
The Council shall have legal personality, financial
and administrative autonomy.
Article 6: Head Office of the Council
The head office of the Council shall be located
City of Kigali, the Capital of the Republic
Rwanda.
It may be transferred to any other location
Rwanda upon decision by the National Council
Board.

CHAPTER II: ORGANISATION OF THE
COUNCIL
Article 7: Organs of the Council
The Council shall have the following three
organs:
1° the National Council Board;
2° the Bureau of the National Council
Board;
3° Technical Committees.
Article 8: National Council Board
The National Council Board shall be the supreme
organ of the Council.
Article 9: Composition of the National Council
Board
Members of the National Council Board shall
elected by their peers in each Province and in
City of Kigali on the basis of the number
pharmacists registered in such an area.
The internal rules and regulations of the Council
shall provide for modalities for their election.
Article 10: Term of office of members of the
National Council Board
Members of the National Council Board shall serve
for a three (3) year term of office renewable only
once.

They may once again stand for election after five
years following the expiry of their previous term
office.
Article 11: Grounds for termination
membership in the National Council Board
A person shall cease to be a member of the National
Council Board if:
1° his/her term of office expires;
2° he/she resigns in writting;
3° he/she is no longer able to perform his/her
duties due to physical or mental disability
duly confirmed by an authorized medical
doctor;
4° he/she is definitively sentenced to a term
imprisonment equal to or exceeding six
months without suspension;
5° he/she misses three (3) consecutive
meetings of the National Council Board
year without justified reasons;
6° he/she jeopardises the interests of
Council;
7° he/she demonstrates behaviors inconsistent
with his/her duties;
8° he/she no longer meets the requirements
which were considered at the time of his/her
election in the National Council Board;
9° he/she confesses and pleads guilty of
crime of genocide;

10° he/she is convicted of the crime
genocide ideology;
11° he/she dies.
Article 12: Replacement of a member of
National Council Board
When a member of the National Council Board
ceases to be member, he/she shall be replaced
within three (3) months under the same conditions
as those under which he/she acquired membership.
When a member of the National Council Board
ceases to be member before the expiry of his/her
term, he/she shall be replaced for the remainder
his/her term if such a remainder is more than six
months.
Article 13: Bureau of the National Council Board
The Bureau of the National Council Board shall
comprised of nine (9) members including
Chairperson and a Deputy Chairperson, a Secretary,
a Treasurer and five (5) Advisors.
Modalities for their election shall be determined
the internal rules and regulations of the Council.
Article 14: Technical Committees
In carrying out its responsibilities, the National
Council Board shall be assisted by Technical
Committees.

The organization and functioning of such
Committees shall be determined by the internal
rules and regulations of the Council.
Article 15: Legal representation
The Council shall carry out its mission through
organs.
In legal proceedings and other acts in connection
with the fulfillment of its mission, the Council shall
be represented by the Chairperson of the National
Council Board and, in case of his/her absence,
the Deputy Chairperson.
CHAPTER
III :
COMPETENCE
AND
RESPONSIBILITIES OF THE ORGANS
THE COUNCIL
Section One: Competence and responsibilities
the organs of the Council
Article 16: Competence of the National Council
Board
The National Council Board shall serve as
interlocutor with public and private organs with
regard to all matters relating to the pharmacy
The National Council Board establishes internal
rules and regulations and general principles relating
to morality, honor, confidentiality, dignity
devotion essential to the practice of the profession
and which constitute the Code of pharmacy ethics.

profession.
The National Council Board shall collaborate with
all other organs involved in the practice of
medical profession.
Article 17: Responsibilities of the National
Council Board
In particular, the National Council Board shall
responsible for the following:
1° to participate in the process of defining
quality of potential procedures expected
from a pharmacist and to participate
determining
standards for pharmacy
education at the university level in Rwanda;
2° to set up regulations on the minimum
knowledge and skills required for
pharmacists in their respective categories;
3° to grant and revoke the authorization
practice the pharmacy profession;
4° to set subscription fee required for
member of the Council;
5° to
set
annual
contribution
to be paid by each member registered
the Register of members of the Council;
6° to determine the conditions for organizing

elections, the procedure of appeal
dispute settlement;
7° to serve as an appellate body for decisions
made by the Bureau of the National Council
Board;
8° to approve the budget, the long- term
planning and annual action plan;
9° to support all activities and initiatives
designed to promote the knowledge
Rwanda’s residents in the pharmacy
profession.
Article 18: Responsibilities of the Bureau of
National Council Board
The Bureau of the National Council Board shall
have the following responsibilities:
1° to do its utmost to ensure the smooth
running of activities of the Council
those of its Committees;
2° to update the Register of members of
Council;
3° to implement instructions, regulations
decisions of the National Council Board;
4° to put an end to any act, contract or statutes
to which a pharmacist may be a party
which include clauses that undermine

principles of pharmacy ethics ;
5° to report to relevant authorities acts
illegal practice of the pharmacy profession
that come to its knowledge and take
disciplinary measures accordingly;
6° to impose disciplinary sanctions
professional misconduct committed by
pharmacist as well as serious misconduct
committed
outside the
course
professional practice but that are likely
undermine the honor and dignity of
pharmacy profession;
7° to enforce any disciplinary measure taken
by the organs of the Council responsible
the ethics of pharmacists;
8° to hire and dismiss staff of the Council
determine their duties and set their
remuneration in accordance with
relevant laws;
9° to perform any other duty falling within
responsibilities
as may be deemed
necessary by the National Council Board.
Section 2: Training and knowledge evaluation
Article 19: Training
The National Council Board shall put in place
training system for each pharmacist to enhance
his/her knowledge on an ongoing basis.

Each pharmacist must enhance his/her knowledge
and be subject to a knowledge and performance
assessment.
Article 20: Knowledge assessment
The National Council Board shall put in place
knowledge assessment system, organs responsible
for conducting such assessment and monitor
conduct.
The internal rules and regulations of the Council
shall determine modalities for conducting
assessment.
Each pharmacist must obtain the minimum score
determined by the National Council Board
him/her to be duly authorized to practice
pharmacy profession.
Article 21: Post-knowledge assessment measures
When a pharmacist fails to obtain the required
score, the National Council Board shall take one
more of the following measures:
1° to subject him/her to a specific ongoing
training program;
2° to require him/her to sit for a written
examination;
3° to require him/her to work under
supervision of another pharmacist or
impose him/her some restrictions;
4° to remove his/her name from the Register
members of the Council.

CHAPITRE IV: FUNCTIONING OF THE
ORGANS OF THE COUNCIL
Article 22: Chairperson of the National Council
Board
The activities of the National Council Board shall
led by the Chairperson of the National Council
Board and, in his/her absence, by the Deputy
Chairperson.
Article 23: Meetings of the National Council
Board
Modalities for preparing the meetings of
National Council Board, procedures for taking
decisions and sanctions for absence from meetings
shall be determined by the Internal Rules
Regulations of the Council.
Article 24: Invitation of a resource person in
meetings of the National Council Board
In order to carry out its responsibilities, the National
Council Board may invite to its meetings any person
whose opinion may be useful for the consideration
of a particular item on the agenda. The invited
person cannot participate in vote or discussions
other items on the agenda.
Article 25: Transmission of the register
pharmacists members of the Council
Before 31 st July of each year, the Bureau of

National Council Board shall transmit to
Minister in charge of health the Register
members of the Council drawn up on 30 June of
same year, which register must be published.
Any deletion or removal of a pharmacist from
register shall be notified to the authority referred
above and must be published.
Article 26: Permanent Secretariat
For the Council to carry out its mission, the Bureau
of the National Council Board shall be assisted in
daily activities by a Permanent Secretariat.
Responsibilities and the functioning of
Permanent Secretariat of the Council shall
determined by the internal rules and regulations
the Council.
CHAPTER V: CODE OF ETHICS AND
DISCIPLINARY MEASURES
Article 27: Disciplinary measures
Disciplinary measures that may be taken by
organs of the Council against pharmacists shall
as follows:
1. warning ;
2. reprimand ;
3. temporary suspension of practice for
period not exceeding twelve (12) months

4. removal from the register of members of
Council.
Each disciplinary measure provided above shall
notified to the Minister in charge of health.
Before any disciplinary measure is taken against
pharmacist, he/she must be given the opportunity
be heard by the organ with the power to impose
such a measure against him/her.
Article 28:
suspension
Consequences
of
temporary
A pharmacist who is subject to a disciplinary
measure of temporary suspension of practice of
pharmacy profession shall be deprived of his/her
right to vote and be elected in the organs of
Council for a period of three (3) years.
Article 29: Automatic removal from office
A pharmacist elected on any organ of the Council
shall be subject to automatic removal from office
1° he/she is subject to a disciplinary measure
of temporary suspension;
2° he/she was removed from the Register
members of the Council;
3° he/she has been definitively sentenced to
term of imprisonment equal to or exceeding
six (6) months.

Article 30: Non-discrimination
No disciplinary measure shall be imposed against
pharmacist by the organs of the Council on
grounds of any form of discrimination.
Article 31: Professional secrecy
All pharmacists and members of the organs of
Council shall be bound by professional secrecy
any information gained from their duties or they
acquire in the course of performance of their duties
even after they cease to perform such duties.
The same shall apply to any person who,
whatever capacity, participates in the functioning
the Council.
The breach of professional secrecy shall
punishable in accordance with the provisions of
Penal Code.
Article 32: Prosecution of a pharmacist
practicing without being registered in
register of the Council or while on the suspension
Any pharmacist who practices the pharmacy
profession without being registered in the register
the Council as well as a pharmacist who practices
while he/she is subject to temporary or permanent
suspension shall be brought before competent courts
of law.

The same shall apply to any employer of
pharmacist referred to under Paragraph One of
Article.
Article 33: Disciplinary proceedings
Disciplinary proceedings relating to the profession
against a pharmacist shall not prevent :
1° legal proceedings in criminal courts ;
2° legal proceedings before civil courts;
3° disciplinary proceedings before the superior
in the administrative hierarchy.
CHAPTER
VI:
DISCIPLINARY
PROCEDURES AND APPEAL
Article 34: Disciplinary action by the National
Council Board
The National Council Board shall institute
disciplinary action against a pharmacist on its own
initiative or upon request by any other interested
person.
Article 35: Temporary suspension of
pharmacist by the Bureau of the National
Council Board
Pending the decision of the National Council Board

which must be taken within one month, the Bureau
of the National Council Board may temporarily
suspend a pharmacist suspected of having
committed a serious misconduct punishable
temporary suspension or removal from the Register
of the members of the Council.
The National Council Board which had taken such
measure shall reconsider it to make a final decision.
Without prejudice to the provisions of the Paragraph
One of this Article, a pharmacist who is subject
removal from the Register of members of
Council for gross misconduct may apply for
registration on the register after three (3) years.
Article 36: Measure taken against a pharmacist
in absentia
A pharmacist against whom a measure was taken
absentia may file an opposition to such an action
within fifteen (15) days of notification.
When the person who has filed an opposition fails
appear once again without good reason, he/she
no longer file a new opposition.
Article 37: Appeal
The appeal shall be made in writing and sent to
Permanent Secretariat of the National Council
Board. Such an appeal may be made within thirty
(30) days from the date of its notification either

the concerned pharmacist or by the Minister
charge of health or any other interested person.
The appeal against a measure shall lead to
suspension of its enforcement.
The decision of the National Council Board shall
notified to the concerned person by means of
registered mail or hand-delivered to him/her
through any other means of communication against
acknowledgment of receipt within thirty (30) days
from the date the decision was taken.

Article 38: Sources of the property and
modalities for its management
The property of the Council shall be comprised
movables and immovables.
It shall come from the following sources:
1° members’ contributions;
2° subsidies, donations and bequests;
3° investments of the Council;
4° proceeds from services rendered by
Council.
Article 39: Annual contribution
The National Council Board shall determine
annually the amount of contribution to be paid

each person practicing the pharmacy profession
the Provincial Council of the place of his/her
practice.
It also determines the quota to be paid to
National Council Board.
Article 40: Management of the property
The National Council Board shall manage
property of the Council and may organize
subsidy non-profit activities designed to promote
pharmacy profession.
CHAPTER VIII: TRANSITIONAL
FINAL PROVISIONS
AND
Article 41: General Assembly
Within ninety (90) days from the publication of
Law in the Official Gazette of the Republic
Rwanda, the Minister in charge of health shall
convene the first General Assembly which shall
elect a provisional Committee responsible
preparing the putting in place of the organs of
Council within three (3) months.
Article 42: Code of ethics
The Code of ethics of the pharmacy profession shall
be determined by an Order of the Minister in charge
of health.

Article 43: Drafting, consideration and adoption
of this Law
This Law was drafted in English, considered
adopted in Kinyarwanda.
Article 44: Repealing provision
All prior legal provisions contrary to this Law
hereby repealed.
Article 45: Commencement
Kigali, on 14/01/2013
This Law shall come into force on the date of
publication in the Official Gazette of the Republic
of Rwanda.

Council;
7° he/she demonstrates behaviors inconsistent
with his/her duties;
8° he/she no longer meets the requirements
which were considered at the time of his/her
election to the National Council Board;
9° he/she confesses and pleads guilty of the
crime of genocide;
10° he/she is convicted of the crime of genocide
ideology;
11° he/she dies.

Article 14: Replacement of a member of the
National Council Board
When a member of the National Council Board
ceases to be a member, he/she shall be replaced
within three (3) months under the same conditions
as those under which he/she acquired membership.
When a member of the National Council Board
ceases to be a member before the expiry of his/her
term, he/she shall be replaced for the remainder
his/her term if such a remainder is more than six
(6) months.
Section 2: Bureau of the National Council Board
Article 15: Bureau of the Council
The Bureau of the National Council Board shall
comprised of seven (7) members including
Chairperson and a Deputy Chairperson,
Secretary, a Treasurer and three (3) Advisors.
Modalities for their election shall be determined
the internal rules and regulations of the Council.
Section 3: Technical Committees
Article 16: Technical Committees
In carrying out its responsibilities, the National
Council Board shall be assisted by technical
Committees.
The Organization and functioning of such

Committees shall be determined by internal rules
and regulations of the Council.

The Bureau of the National Council Board shall

2° to update the register of members of the

3° to implement the decisions, regulations and

4° to put an end to all acts, contracts or Articles

6° to impose disciplinary sanctions for

as serious misconduct committed outside the

likely to undermine the honor and dignity
the profession;
7° to enforce any disciplinary measure taken
the organs of the Council responsible for the
professional ethics;
8° to recruit the staff of the Council and
determine their duties and their remunerations
in accordance with the relevant laws as well
as to dismiss them;
9° to perform any other duty as the National
Council Board may consider necessary.

Post-knowledge

3. to require him/her to work under the

4. to suspend his/her authorization to practice;
5. to remove his/her name from the register
members of the Council.

The conditions for registration and the contents

Application for registration shall be in writing and

Where the applicant is not registered, he/she may

The National Council Board shall examine and
make decision on the appeal in its following

Where he/she is not satisfied by the decision taken
on the appeal, the appellant may file a legal
proceeding with the competent court.
Article 26: Usurpation of titles
No person shall practice as a practitionner of allied
health profession in Rwanda or claiming to belong
to the profession unless he/she is registered on the
register of Council.
It is prohibited to employ a practitionner of allied
health profession who is not registered on the
register of members of the Council.

Article 32: Disciplinary measures and organs

Disciplinary measures that may be taken by the
organs of the Council against the practitioners
allied health professions shall be the following:
1°
warning;
2° reprimand;
3° temporary suspension of practice for a period
not exceeding twelve (12) months;
4° removal from the register.
Each disciplinary measure provided above shall
notified to the Minister in charge of health.
Before any disciplinary measure is taken,
concerned practitioner of an allied health
profession must be given the opportunity to
heard by the organ with the power to impose such
sanction against him/her.
Article 33:
suspension
Consequences
of
temporary
A practitioner of allied health profession who
subject to a disciplinary measure of temporary
suspension of practice of the allied health
profession shall be deprived of the right to vote and
to be elected in the organs of the Council for

period of three (3) years.
Article 34: Automatic removal from office
A practitioner of allied health profession elected
any organ of the Council shall be subject
automatic removal from office if:
1. he/she was subject to a disciplinary
measure of temporary suspension;
2. he/she was removed from the register
members of the Council;
3. he/she has been definitively sentenced to
leat six (6) months of imprisonment.
Article 3: Non-discrimination
No disciplinary measure shall be imposed against
practitionner of allied health profession by the
Council organs based on the grounds of any form
of discrimination.
Article 36: Professional secrecy
All practitioners of allied health profession and
members of the organs of the Council shall
bound by professional secrecy for any information
gained from their duties or they have acquired
the course of performance of their duties even after
they cease to perform such duties.

The same shall apply to any person who,
whatever capacity, participates in the functioning
of the Council.
The breach of professional secrecy shall
punishable in accordance with the provisions of the
Penal Code.
Article 37: Prosecution of a practitionner
allied health profession practicing without being
registered in the register of the Council or while
on the suspension
Any practitionner of allied health profession who
practices his/her profession without being
registered in the register of the Council as well
one who practices while on temporary
permanent suspension shall be brought before
competent courts of law.
The same shall apply to any employer of
practitionner of allied health profession referred
in Paragraph one (1) of this Article.
Article 38: Concommitance of proceedings
Disciplinary proceedings against a practitioner
allied health profession shall not prevent :
1. legal proceedings in criminal courts ;
2. legal proceedings before civil courts;
3. disciplinary proceedings before the
superior in the administrative hierarchy.

Article 39: Disciplinary action by the National
Council Board
The National Council Board shall institute the
disciplinary action against a practitioner of allied
health profession on its own initiative or upon
request by any other interested person.
Article 40: Investigations
In case of a decision to investigate into the case
a practitionner of allied health profession, such
practitionner shall be notified of the decision
through a registered letter within fifteen (15) days,
before the investigations start.
The National Council Board shall, basing on the
report it received and decision grounded for, decide
whether the issue is definitively settled, needs
further investigations or the concerned practitioner
of allied health profession has to be invited.
The decision of the National Council Board shall
be notified to the concerned practitioner through
registered letter. However, the decision to delete
names of a practitioner of allied health profession
from the register shall be communicated through
modalities determined by the internal rules and
regulations of the Council.

Article 41: Suspension of a practitioner of allied
health profession by the Bureau of the National
Council Board
Pending the National Council Board decision
which has to be taken within a period of one (1)
month, the Bureau of the National Council Board
may temporarily suspend a practitioner of allied
health profession in suspect of having committed
serious misconduct punishable of temporary
suspension from the practice of the allied health
profession or removal from the register of members
of the Council.
Without prejudice to the provisions of Paragraph
One of this Article, a practitioner of allied health
profession who is subject to removal from the
register of members of the Council for gross
misconduct may apply for re-registration on the
register after three (3) years.
The National Council Board which had taken such
an action shall reconsider it to make a final
decision.
Article 42: Measure taken against a practitioner
of allied health profession in absentia
Any practitioner of allied health profession against
whom a measure was taken in absentia may file
opposition to such a measure within fifteen (15)
days of notification.
When the person who has filed an opposition fails

to appear once again without good reason, he/she
can no longer file a new opposition.
Article 43: Appeal
The appeal shall be made in writing and sent to the
permanent Secretariat of the National Council
Board. Such an appeal is made by a practitioner
allied health profession himself/herself within
thirty (30) days from the date of the notification
the decision.
The appeal against an action shall lead to the
suspension of its enforcement.

This Law relates to the regulation and inspection
of food and pharmaceutical products.

Article 5: Regulation of food products
Food products authorized for use shall conform
quality standards set by the national competent
organs.
No food product shall be manufactured, sold,
donated, imported, stored or exhibited unless
conforms to the requirements provided under this

Law. Each food product must:
1° not contain toxic substances;
2° be safe for human or animal consumption;
3° not be rotten,
contaminated;
4° not contain additives that may cause
disease;
5° not be associated with effects on human
health;
6° conform to quality standards or the
provisions of food product regulations
applicable in the country;
7° not be counterfeited ;
8° not be derived from diseased or infected
animals;
9° be stored, manufactured or prepared in
place that meets hygiene standards.
spoiled,
expired
An Order of the Minister shall set out instructions
regulating food products and their composition.

Article 6: Hygiene for food products intended
for sale
Each food product must meet hygiene
requirements.
No person shall be authorized to manufacture,
prepare, store, package or keep food products for
sale unless he/she complies with hygiene
requirements.
Food products shall be stored in such conditions
that they retain their properties and originality
while at the same time ensuring that they do not
lose their nutrients due to the storage conditions.
An Order of the Minister shall set out instructions
regulating hygiene requirements for food products
intended for sale.

Article 7: Donation of food products
Food products donated shall not be sold and must
conform to the requirements provided under
Article 5 of this Law.

products
The advertisement and exhibition of food products
shall be carried out in accordance with this Law.

An Order of the Minister shall set out instructions
regulating the advertisement and exhibition
food products.
Article 9: Trade in food products that do not
meet required standards
It is prohibited to manufacture, store, supply, sell
or have in one’s possession for commercial
purposes food products that do not meet
requirements provided under this Law.
Article 10:
consumers
If it is evident that food products have caused
harmful effects on consumers’ health, the
competent organs shall carry out investigations
and take action in accordance with applicable
national laws.

pharmaceutical product or any other product, in
manner that causes erroneous impression of the
product, unless the product conforms to the
standards under these regulations.
Article 14: Sample pharmaceutical product
No person shall distribute or cause to distribute
any pharmaceutical product that is used as
sample.

However, the provisions of paragraph one of this
article do not apply to the distribution
pharmaceutical products by medical doctors,
dentists, veterinary surgeons or pharmacists.
CHAPTER IV: COSMETICS
Section One: Prohibitions
Article 15: List of cosmetics
Cosmetics shall mean any substances applied
the human body in order to enhance, change and
impart a pleasant smell onto the human body.
Cosmetics must be free of toxic substances and
meet quality standards applicable in the country
and be prepared in compliance with relevant
principles of good preparation practice.
An Order of the Minister shall set out the list
cosmetics whose use is prohibited within the
country.
Article 16: Prohibited acts in connection with
cosmetics
No person shall manufacture, import, store,
exhibit, sale or dispense cosmetics unless they
meet requirements under this Law.

Article 17: Prohibited cosmetics ingredients
For purposes of public health interest, any
cosmetic ingredient that does not meet quality
requirements is prohibited.
No person shall manufacture, import, store,
exhibit, sell or dispense cosmetics that:
1° contain or consist of substances likely
adversely affect health when used;
2° were manufactured, prepared, preserved
or stored under unsanitary conditions.
No person shall manufacture or store cosmetics
under inappropriate conditions, using non-
permitted colours or including toxic, putrid
decomposed substances.
Section 2: Regulations on counterfeit cosmetics
Article 18: Standards prescribed for cosmetics
Where standards exist for cosmetics, no person
shall label, sell, package, advertise or dispense
any cosmetic in such a manner that creates

erroneous impression.
Article 19: Prohibition of manufacturing,
storing sale and distribution of toxic cosmetics
No person shall manufacture, store, sell and
distribute toxic cosmetics or those that may cause
adverse effects to the users.
Article 20: Counterfeit cosmetics
No person shall manufacture, import, sell,
dispense or possess counterfeit cosmetics.
CHAPTER V: MEDICAL DEVICES
Article 21: List of medical devices
The list of pharmaceutical products approved
the country shall include the list of medical
devices. These medical devices must meet quality
standards applicable in the country and
manufactured in compliance with relevant
principles of their manufacture.

Article 22: Prohibitions in connection with
medical devices
No person shall manufacture, import, sell and
distribute unregistered medical device that, when
used according to directions, may cause adverse
effects or cause injury to the user.
No person shall manufacture, import, store,
exhibit, sell or distribute medical devices that
not meet requirements under this Law.
Article 23: Prohibited parts in medical devices
For purposes of public health interest, any part
a medical device that does not meet quality
requirements is prohibited.
No person shall manufacture, import, store,
exhibit, sell or distribute medical devices that
contain or consist of parts likely to be harmful
health when they are used.
No person shall manufacture or store medical
devices under unsanitary conditions.

Article 24: Regulations on medical devices
No person shall label, pack, treat, sell, distribute
or advertise any medical device in a manner that
is false, misleading or is likely to create
erroneous impression regarding its performance,
design, use, intended use, value or quality.
Article 25: Counterfeit medical devices
No person shall manufacture, import, sell,
distribute or possess counterfeit medical devices.

RELATED
TO
PHARMACEUTICAL
PRODUCTS AND MEDICAL DEVICES
Article 26: Requirements for registration
pharmaceutical products and medical devices
No person shall market a pharmaceutical product
or a medical device on the Rwandan market
unless such a product or device is registered.
An Order of the Minister shall set out
requirements for registration of pharmaceutical
products and medical devices.

An Order of the Minister in charge of finance
shall determine fees for registration
pharmaceutical products and medical devices.
Article 27: Publication of
registered
pharmaceutical products and medical devices
An updated list of registered pharmaceutical
products and medical devices shall be published
in an easily accessible manner.
Article 28: Certificate of registration of
pharmaceutical product and medical device
The certificate of registration of a pharmaceutical
product and a medical device shall be valid for
period of five (5) years renewable upon request
the applicant of registration of a pharmaceutical
product or a medical device.
No
person
shall
import
unregistered
pharmaceutical products and medical devices.
Article 29: Removal from the list of registered
pharmaceutical products and medical devices
For purposes of public health interest, registered
pharmaceutical products and medical devices may
be removed from the list due to their potential

adverse effects or unproven effectiveness.
Article 30: Pharmaceutical products and
medical devices clinical trials
Pharmaceutical products or medical devices
whether manufactured in the country or imported
must have undergone a thorough clinical trial
identify their effectiveness and potential adverse
effects related to their use.
Article 31: License to practice pharmacy
profession
A pharmacist licensed to practice pharmacy
profession shall be a registered person who holds
a bachelors’s degree in pharmacy.
Article 32: License for pharmacy ownership
and practice
A registered pharmacist may apply for and
granted a license for pharmacy ownership.
However, an individual or an association
persons may apply for and be granted a license
condition to employ a registered pharmacist
tasked with carrying out pharmaceutical activities.
A head pharmacist shall practice his/her
profession with due diligence and discretion

without interference or receiving instructions from
his/her employer.
No person shall open a pharmacy unless the
pharmacy is under the supervision of
pharmacist, or make use of any title, emblem
description reasonably intended to suggest that
he/she is a pharmacist.
No head pharmacist shall be entrusted with the
responsibility of managing any other pharmacy
the same time.
The certificate of registration of the pharmacist,
the licence for opening a pharmacy and the license
to operate a pharmacy shall be conspicuously
exhibited in the pharmarcy premises.
A pharmacy must be identified by a clearly
displayed sign post containing the name of the
pharmacy, name of the pharmacist and the
telephone number.
When a pharmacist resigns, is dismissed from
his/her job or dies, the pharmacy shall remain
closed until another pharmacist is recruited.
No pharmaceutical product written on the medical
prescription can be dispensed without the
supervision of an authorized person.

CHAPTER VII : MANUFACTURE, IMPORT,
EXPORT, STORAGE AND DISTRIBUTION
OF ITEMS REGULATED UNDER THIS
LAW
Article 33: Manufacture of items regulated
under this Law
Pharmaceutical products and medical devices
shall be manufactured in compliance with relevant
principles relating to their manufacture.
No individual or association of persons shall
manufacture pharmaceutical products and medical
devices unless they are granted a license to do so.
Article 34: Import and export
Import and export of pharmaceutical products and
medical devices shall be done in accordance with
the provisions of an Order of the Minister.
No person or association of persons shall import
and export pharmaceutical products and medical
devices unless they are granted a license to do so.
No pharmacy importing pharmaceutical products
and medical devices shall sell them on
wholesale basis to other pharmacies in the same

category.
No person shall import pharmaceutical products
and medical devices with shelf life less than two
thirds (2/3) of their total shelf life.
Article 35: Import and introduction
pharmaceutical products and medical devices
for special purposes
No person shall import and introduce into the
country pharmaceutical products and medical
devices for special purposes unless he/she has
license granted in accordance with the provisions
of this Law.
However, if there are justified reasons,
individual or a company that carries out activities
provided under this Law may be authorized
import pharmaceutical products and other medical
devices for special purposes.
Without prejudice to the provisions of this Law,
tourist, a visitor in the country or any other person
may, on medical grounds as certified by a medical
doctor, be authorized to introduce into the country
a pharmaceutical product or medical devices they
need within a renewable period of thirty (30)
days.
A resident in the country shall, on medical
grounds as certified by a medical doctor,
authorized to import a pharmaceutical product
such pharmaceutical product is not available

the country.
Article 36: Emergency situation
Emergency situation occurs when the country
faced with disasters, pandemics and war.
Article 37: Supply of pharmaceutical products
and medical devices
Retail pharmacy business consists of the retail
sale of pharmaceutical products, food products,
cosmetics, poisonous products, herbal medicines
and medical devices but does not include
professional practice carried on by a medical
practitioner.
A pharmacy that supplies pharmaceutical products
and medical devices may buy them on a wholesale
basis but shall not sell them on a wholesale basis
to other pharmacies in the same category.
The pharmacy shall not be allowed to bid for the
supply of pharmaceutical products and medical
devices.
No person shall supply pharmaceutical products
and medical devices which do not meet quality
standards or which are counterfeit.

Article 38: Unfit pharmaceutical products
No person shall:
1° add to or reduce any substance from
pharmaceutical product so as to change
composition;
2° dispense, exhibit or have in his/her
possession any pharmaceutical product
whose composition has been affected
the addition thereto or reduction there
from of any substance;
3° dispense any pharmaceutical product
which does not meet the composition and
quality required by the consumer;
4° dispense, buy, sell and administer to any
person
or
animal,
any
unfit
pharmaceutical product.
Unfit pharmaceutical products are kept in
separate
confinement
labelled
“unfit
pharmaceutical products” marked in red.
An Order of the Minister shall set out the nature
of the place of confinement of unfit
pharmaceutical products.

Article 39: Dispensation of a pharmaceutical
product
Dispensation of a pharmaceutical product is an act
of receiving and examining a medical
prescription, counselling a person and offering
pharmaceutical product intended for human
animal consumption through oral, intranasal,
ocular,
auricular,
vaginal
or
urethral,
intracutaneous, topical route or by injection
through any other route that may be used
administer a pharmaceutical product depending
on its existing state pursuant to a prescription
a medical doctor, a dentist or veterinary surgeon.

counterfeiting pharmaceutical product.

Article 41: Nature and use of medical

Pharmaceutical products shall be dispensed on the
basis of a medical prescription.
An Order of the Minister shall determine the

Article 42: Essential pharmaceutical products

The Minister shall publish in the Official Gazette

Without prejudice to the provisions of other laws

pharmaceutical products for medicinal
scientific purposes unless he/she meets
requirements provided under this Law.

Article 44: Inspection Department
The Inspection Department SHALL control the
manufacture, distribution and dispensing
pharmaceutical products. It shall at any time
verify whether the conditions that were required
continue to be met and whether medical devices

The Inspection Department shall verify whether

samples for analysis, if necessary; it shall also

assistance if considered necessary.

Inspection Department or local authorities of any
implications resulting from the implementation
the provisions of Paragraph Oe of this Article
take an appropriate decision.
District authorities may also take an appropriate
decision in relation to whatever can cause
serious harm to health.
