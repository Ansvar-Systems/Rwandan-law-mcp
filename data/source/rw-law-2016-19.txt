THE PRESERVATION OF AIR QUALITY AND
PREVENTION OF AIR POLLUTION

CHAPTER ONE: GENERAL PROVISIONS
Article One: Purpose of this Law
This Law determines modalities for preservation
air quality and prevention of air pollution in Rwanda.
Article 2: Definitions of terms
For the purpose of this Law, the following terms
have the following meanings:

1° air pollution: condition of the atmosphere
which pollutants are present in such a quantity
to be likely detrimental to public health, flora,
fauna, property or to interfere with materials
environment;
2° ambient air: outdoor air to which people, plants,
animals or material are in normal circumstances
exposed;
3° emission: release of a pollutant into the air;
4° emission limit: the highest permissible quantity
of pollutants released into the air from a pollution
source, expressed as the concentration
pollutants;
5° excessive emission: emission of an air pollutant
in excessive of an emission standard or emission
target;
6° permissible pollutant activity: activity
does not comply with minimum standards
prescribed under this Law, which is subject
prior authorization by the Authority in charge
environment protection;
7° Minister: Minister in charge of environment;

8° monitoring: any periodic or continuous testing
to determine the level of compliance
statutory requirements or pollutant levels
various materials, in humans, animals and other
living beings;
9° suspended particles: small airborne particles
with a diameter of ten (10) micrometres or less;
10° unexpected emission:
unexpectedly through
inappropriate ways;
pollutants released
unconventional
11° environmental inspector: government staff
charge of follow-up and monitoring of
enforcement of environmental preservation.
Article 3: Scope of this Law
This Law applies to all measures aimed at
preservation of air quality as well as all elements
activities likely to affect air quality or pollute
atmosphere.
However, this Law does not apply to air pollutants
arising from the following:
1° the use of radioactive substances or devices;

2° military operations and drills;
3° fire fighting operations;
4° natural disasters.
CHAPTER
II:
ESTABLISHMENT
QUALITY STANDARDS
Article 4: Establishment of air quality standards
The National Authority in charge of establishing
quality standards must:
1° prescribe criteria and procedure
measuring air quality and air pollutants;
2° establish ambient air quality standards
order to curb the impact of air pollutants;
3° establish occupational air quality standards
for various sources of air pollution which
cause harm to public health;
4° establish quality standards that regulate
emissions of air pollutants from different
sources contributing to air pollution;

5° establish specific quality standards
regulate industrial activities with a view
avoid or minimize environmental pollution
that may result from such industries;
6° determine stack heights of chimneys for
emissions;
7° prescribe any matter in relation with
affecting air emission quality standards.
An Order of the Minister establishes specific
quality standards.
CHAPTER
III:
COMPLIANCE
WITH
MINIMUM AIR QUALITY STANDARDS
Article 5: Compliance with minimum air quality
standards
Every person must comply with the minimum
quality standards established by the National
Authority in charge of setting up regulations
quality standards.
Article 6: Prohibition of emission of chemicals,
materials, gas or hazardous substances
The emission of chemicals, materials, gas
hazardous substances or mixture containing gaseous
and toxic substances is prohibited unless

emission is authorized by the Authority in charge
the protection of environment.
Any person producing, transporting, trading, using,
storing or possessing chemicals, toxic substances,
inflammable or explosive substances must comply
with this Law for the purpose of preserving safety
humans and other living beings and avoid causing
environmental degradation.
Article 7: Emission limit
A person engaging in any activity is required
comply with the highest permissible emission limits
of air pollutants from the atmosphere to the living
things in accordance with ambient air quality
standards.
Article 8: Specific tolerance limit of pollutants
from industries
Without prejudice to the provisions of this Law,
tolerance limit of air pollutants from industries
the air, must be the same as the tolerance limit in
specific quality standards in force determined by
national Authority in charge of setting up regulations
for quality standards.

Article 9: Inspection of air pollutants from
transport means
Any person owning any means of transport of people
and goods which is an emission source must control
production and emission of air pollutants.
Means of transport referred to under Paragraph
of this Article operating in Rwanda must undergo
inspection for emissions control.
An Order of the Minister determines modalities
requirements for compliance with permissible
emissions limits by the means of transport and other
machines using petroleum products.
Article 10: Air pollutants from construction
works
Any person transporting or storing construction
materials or materials from construction works,
carrying out demolition of a building or part
building must avoid air pollution by complying
relevant quality standards.
Article 11: Air pollutants from the storage
objects
Any person must avoid storing objects in such a
that they may interfere with the air quality.

Article 12: Air pollutants from waste incineration
Any person operating a waste incinerator must avoid
emission and production of air pollutants
complying with the relevant quality standards.
Article 13: Air pollutants from other sources
Any person owning emission sources including
power plants, gas extraction, plants, boilers,
generators, and furnace must avoid causing emission
of air pollutants by complying with the relevant
quality standards regulations.
An Order of the Minister may determine other
of specific activities that are considered as sources
air pollution.
CHAPTER IV: AIR POLLUTANT EMISSION
PERMIT
Article 14: Air pollutant emission permit
Any person whose activity is air polluting and
not comply with the air quality standards must apply
for a permit issued by the national Authority
charge of environment protection.

An Order of the Minister determines requirements
and modalities for applying, granting and using
permit.
CHAPTER V: AIR QUALITY COMPLIANCE
Article 15: Obligation to comply with air quality
Every person has the obligation to safeguard
preserve the air quality.
Article 16: Monitoring of air quality compliance
The inspection and monitoring of air quality
compliance are carried out by the Authority in charge
of environment protection. The Authority
establishes and maintains close collaboration with
institutions for the purpose of enforcement of
quality standards.
Article 17: Compliance order on air pollution
control and prevention
The National Authority in charge of environment
protection, the City of Kigali or the District may issue
protection or prevention orders against activities
likely to result in air pollution or have adverse effect
on the air and on public health.

Compliance order specifies activities that are subject
to prevention or protection and actions that the owner
of the activity is required to take and the timeframe
for implementation of such actions.
Article 18: Orders to stop activities
The National Authority in charge of environment
protection, the City of Kigali or the District must
stop, in writing, any activity which pollutes the
where they notice that pursuing such activity
cause serious harm to human health or environment.
Where such activity is stopped by the City of Kigali
or by the District, the National Authority in charge
environment protection is given a copy thereof.
stop order must indicate all the necessary information
as to enable the person to whom it is served
understand such an order and what it requires.
Where the person to whom a stop order is served
complied with the requirements, he/she makes
written request for its cancellation.
Article 19: Order for emergency prevention
emission of air pollutants
An environmental inspector who receives or notices
information on emission of air pollutants in a
that constitutes a risk to human health
environment, must issue an order for emergency
prevention of the emission of air pollutants.

Such an order requires the person who has
pollution emission activities to particularly indicate
the following:
1° adequate measures indicating how activities
of reduction and elimination of emission
air pollutants are conducted;
2° the necessary equipment, facilities
trained personnel for dealing with emission
of air pollutants;
3° any other measures necessary to respond
other issues that may arise.
Article 20: Reporting accidental or inadvertent
emissions of air pollutants
Whenever a person is involved in an incident
inadvertent or accidental emissions of air pollutants,
that person must report it to the nearest
administration or to the Authority in charge
environment protection within twenty-four
hours of the occurrence of the incident. He/she must
also indicate measures for dealing with
emissions.

Article 21: Powers of the City of Kigali and
District to notify and require polluters to
measures to remedy emissions of air pollutants
Where the City of Kigali or the District notices
activity that causes emissions of air pollutants,
an entity notifies it to the concerned person
requires him/her to find a remedy. If such a person
fails to find the remedy, the City of Kigali or
District works on it and the cost is borne by
owner.
An Order of the Minister determines notification
modalities.
CHAPTER
SANCTIONS
ADMINISTRATIVE
Article 22: Administrative sanctions
VI:
Without prejudice to provisions of the Penal Code,
any person who violates provisions of this Law
liable to administrative sanctions determined by
Order of the Minister.
CHAPTER VII: TRANSITIONAL AND FINAL
PROVISIONS
Article 23: Transitional period
Any person concerned with provisions of this
must comply with them within a period not exceeding

twelve (12) months from the date of publication
this Law in the Official Gazette of the Republic
Rwanda.
Article 24: Drafting, consideration and adoption
of this Law
This Law was drafted in English, considered
adopted in Kinyarwanda.
Article 25: Repealing provision
All prior legal provisions contrary to this Law
repealed.
Article 26: Commencement
This Law comes into force on the date of
publication in the Official Gazette of the Republic
Rwanda.

ADOPTS:
CHAPTER ONE: GENERAL PROVISIONS
Article One: Purpose of this Law
This Law relates to human reproductive health.
Article 2: Definitions of terms
For the purpose of this Law, the following
have the following meanings:
1° family planning: a way for people to
their desired number of children and to
unwanted pregnancies, taking into account
welfare of the child, mother and family;
2° human reproductive health services:
services provided by health facilities, varied
centers with staff that have the relevant
qualifications and other persons trained by
responsible Ministry;
3° human reproductive health: a state of human
physical, mental and social well-being

matters relating to the reproductive system
its functions and processes;
4° incapable person: a child or a person
mental disability;
5° child: any person who is under eighteen
years old.
Article 3: Key components
reproductive health
of
human
Key components of human reproductive health
the following:
1° safe delivery for the mother and the
born;
2° care of the new-born;
3° family planning;
4° prevention and treatment of sexually
transmitted
infections
including
HIV/AIDS;
5° prevention and treatment of other infections
that are harmful to human reproductive
health;
6° prevention and treatment of infertility;
7° prevention of gender-based violence
care for victims thereof;
8° raising awareness with the aim
attitudinal change.

Article 4: Authority to monitor and supervise
human reproductive health-related activities
The Ministry in charge of health has the authority
monitor human reproductive health-related
activities.
A Prime Minister’s Order determines the role
other institutions in activities related to human
reproductive health.
CHAPTER II: RIGHTS IN TERMS
HUMAN REPRODUCTIVE HEALTH
Article 5: Equal rights to human reproductive
health
All persons have equal rights in relation to human
reproductive health.
No person shall be denied such rights based on
form of discrimination.
Article 6: Right of a pregnant woman, a woman
who has given birth and the new-born
A pregnant woman, a woman who has given
and a new-born have the right to be cared for
to ensure their well being.

Article 7: Right to decide
Subject to provisions of other laws, every person
having attained the majority age has the right
decide for oneself in relation to human reproductive
health issues.
Article 8: Right of access to education
medical services
Every person has the right of access to education
medical services related to human reproductive
health.
Article 9: Right not to be subjected to any
likely to be harmful to human reproductive
health
Every person has the right not to be subjected to
experiment or any other act likely to be harmful
human reproductive health.
Article 10: Right in terms of HIV/AIDS testing
Every person has the right to voluntarily undergo
HIV/AIDS test and have his/her results in secret.
No person shall undergo unconsented HIV/AIDS
testing. However, mandatory testing may

required upon request by competent organs
accordance with law.
A medical doctor or any other health professional
may, if necessary, perform HIV/AIDS testing
incapable person subject to consent by that person’s
biological parent, guardian or caretaker.
Article 11: HIV/AIDS infected person’s right
services and medical care
An HIV/AIDS infected person has the right
services and necessary medical care consonant
his/her disease conditions and taking into account
the national financial capability.
CHAPTER III: OBLIGATIONS RELATING
TO HUMAN REPRODUCTIVE HEALTH
Section One: Obligations of individuals
Article 12: Avoidance of any harm against
human reproductive health and that of the others
Regarding human reproductive health, every person
has the obligation to:

1º avoid anything that can harm his/her
personal reproductive health, that of his/her
family and that of others;
2º take care of a pregnant woman, a woman
who has recently given birth and a
born;
3º protect and safeguard human reproductive
health of an elderly person, a person
disability or a person incapable of making
his/her own decision.
Article 13: Self-protection and protection
others against sexually transmitted diseases
Every person has the obligation to protect
himself/herself and protect others against sexually
transmitted diseases.
Any discrimination or stigmatization against
person infected with HIV/AIDS is prohibited.
Article 14: Discussion with children about
human reproductive health
Every biological parent or guardian has the duty
discuss with the children about human reproductive
health.

Section 2: Obligations of the Government, public
institutions, other institutions in charge
human reproductive health and partners
Article 15: Obligations of the Government
promote human reproductive health
In order to promote human reproductive health,
Government has the following obligations:
1º to establish a human reproductive health
policy;
2º to coordinate all activities relating to human
reproductive health;
3º to establish a specific unit in charge of human
reproductive health in each hospital
health centre with medicines, equipment
other means necessary to fulfil its mission;
4º to sensitize and assist pregnant women
deliver at health facilities and play their
in taking decisions on human reproductive
health;

5º to help destitute persons and incapable
persons have access to human reproductive
health services;
6º to train on a regular basis health
professionals, community health workers,
teachers, authorities of decentralized
administrative entities, the private sector
the youth on human reproductive health
7º to sensitize all employer institutions
encourage their staff to know about human
reproductive health;
8º to build partnerships with the private sector
in activities related to human reproductive
health and monitor their implementation
other partners;
9º to conduct and publish research on human
reproductive health;
10º to perform any other activity that
promote human reproductive health.

Article 16: Care for a pregnant woman, a woman
giving birth, a woman who has given birth and
new-born
The Government, public institutions and
institutions providing human reproductive health
services have the obligation to take care
pregnant woman, a woman giving birth, a woman
who has given birth and a new-born.
Article 17: Providing necessary medicines
health services closer to those in need thereof
The Government, public institutions and
institutions in charge of human reproductive health
have the obligation to provide necessary medicines
and health services close to any person in
thereof for him/her to have good reproductive
health.
Article 18: Provision of good services related
human reproductive health
Public servants, the staff of public institutions
other institutions providing services related
human reproductive health are required to provide
good customer care and give to clients fast and
quality service without any discrimination.

Article 19: Education in schools regarding
human reproductive health
The educational curricula of different categories
schools and literacy centres must provide courses
human reproductive health.
An Order of the Minister in charge of education
determines modalities for the implementation
provisions of this article.
Article 20: Public awareness about family
planning
The Government, public institutions, institutions
charge of human reproductive health and
partners have the following obligations:
1º raising public awareness on the adoption
family planning and bringing close to
people all the necessary materials enabling
them to make it for their welfare;
2º sensitizing spouses to have common
understanding for their cooperation
agreement in relation to family planning;

3º raising youth awareness on family planning.
CHAPTER IV: MISCELLANEOUS
FINAL PROVISIONS
Article 21: Repealing provision
All prior legal provisions contrary to this Law
repealed.
Article 22: Drafting, consideration and adoption
of this Law
This Law was drafted, considered and adopted
Kinyarwanda.
Article 23: Commencement
This Law comes into force on the date of
publication in the Official Gazette of the Republic
of Rwanda.
