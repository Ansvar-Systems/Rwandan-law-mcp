CHAPTER ONE: GENERAL PROVISIONS
Section One: Purpose and scope of this Law
Article One: Purpose of this Law
The aim of this Law is to establish a framework
Information and Communication Technologies
(ICT) policy and regulation, with emphasis on
following:
1° to promote national Information
Communication Technologies (ICT) policy
objectives;
2° to establish a licensing and regulatory
framework in support of national policy

objectives for the Information
Communication
Technologies
(ICT)
industry
taking
into
account
convergence of technologies;
3° to establish and strengthen the relevant
institutions by providing them with
powers and procedures that are necessary
for the implementation of this Law;
4° to establish Rwanda as a major global
centre and hub for communications
multimedia information;
5° to promote an information society for
enhancement of quality of both life
work;
6° to ensure an equitable provision
affordable services over ubiquitous national
infrastructure.
Article 2: Scope of this Law
This Law applies to the following:
1° electronic communications;
2° information society;

3° broadcasting sector;
4° postal sector.

Section 3: Organs, their responsibilities
fundamental principles
Article 8: Organs
This Law provides for two following organs :
1° Organ in charge of ICT policy making;
2° Regulatory Organ.
Article 9: Organ in charge of ICT policy making
The Ministry in charge of ICT is responsible
making ICT policy.
The Minister issues regulations on critical issues
national importance following developments in
sector.

Article 10: Regulatory Organ
The regulatory functions for the ICT sector
performed by the Regulatory Authority in
independent,
proportionate,
impartial
transparent manner with a view to achieving
following objectives:
1° adoption of the principle that regulation
should be technology-neutral, and therefore
a prohibition against granting unjustified
advantage to any particular technology;
2° creation of an open and competitive market.
Article 11: Regular review of ICT activities
Regular review of ICT activities avoids to maintain
activities that are or have become unnecessary
obsolete.
Article 12: Reviewing regulatory burden
The Regulatory Authority must keep its functions
under review in order to ensure that its regulatory
activities do not involve the imposition of burdens,
which are unnecessary or do not involve
maintenance of burdens, which have become
unnecessary.
When reviewing its duties, the Regulatory Authority

must consider whether or not the general duties
imposed by the law have effective regulatory impact
on the ICT sector and come up with consideration
and proposals for the appropriate removal
reduction of regulatory burdens created by
general duties imposed by the law.
Article 13: Provision of information
Licensed operators providing equipment, networks
and services in the ICT sector must provide
necessary information in period determined
Regulatory Authority, including financial, technical
and service information.
The information requested by the Regulatory
Authority must be proportionate to the performance
of that task.
The Regulatory Authority has to respect
confidentiality of all information received.
Article 14: Advisory committee on elderly
persons and persons with disabilities
The Regulatory Authority establishes a permanent
committee to provide advice on the ICT- related
interests of elderly persons and those with
disabilities.

Article 15: Powers in terms of monitoring
activities and imposing administrative sanctions
The Regulatory Authority has the power to monitor
the activities and impose administrative sanctions
the licensed operators.
In imposing administrative sanctions,
Regulatory Authority respects the principle of
right to defence for all parties, transparency,
impartiality and non-discriminatory procedures.
Article 16: Special Committee for the settlement
of disputes
The Regulatory Authority establishes a special
committee in charge of solving all disputes that
arise among operators themselves and those
between operators and their consumers.
Specifically, the committee must:
1° take timely and free of charge decisions;
2° respect the principle of cross-examination
and the right to defence giving each party
the opportunity to be heard;
3° sufficiently ground for the decisions
takes;

4° publicize the decisions taken in accordance
with the provisions and limits set by
country’s laws in force;
5° take any other action provided for by
law.
The Regulatory Authority determines
responsibilities, organization and functioning of
committee in charge of solving disputes.
Article 17: Right to refer matters to courts
Any person aggrieved by the decision of
Regulatory Authority may refer the matter to
competent court.
Section 4: Universal access fund
Article 18: Creation of Universal Access Fund
There is hereby established a Fund, known as
Universal Access Fund.
The main mission of this fund is to support
universal access to communication services
throughout the country so as to ensure timely
affordable services.
A Presidential Order determines its responsibilities,
organization and functioning.

Section 10: Computer misuse and cyber crime
Article 197: Unauthorised access to computer

Any access by a person to computer data

2° does not have consent from any person
is so entitled.

or her, to have such an access;

information is used and for a period of at least
(1) year thereafter, keep secret of the personal

and reason for the disclosure.

become obsolete.

CHAPTER V: POSTAL SERVICES
Section One: Preliminary provisions
Article 235: Postal correspondences
Items of postal correspondence may include: letters,
simple postcards, printed matter, newspapers, relief
printing for use by blind or braille letters and small
packages.
Article 236: General principles of postal services
The general principles of postal services are
following:
1° to ensure equal treatment of beneficiaries
the respect of the principles of adaptability
continuity of the public service;
2° to promote and protect the interests
beneficiaries;

3° to guarantee the secrecy of the correspondence;
4° to ensure the transparent management of
accounts of the postal operators;
5° to ensure the compliance with the rules
transparent competition;
6° to ensure the respect of international postal
instruments ratified by the Republic
Rwanda.
Section 2: Postal services
Article 237: Public postal services
The public postal service includes:
1° the universal postal service;
2° the postal financial services;
3° the missions of general interest.
Article 238: Consistency of universal postal
services
The consistency of the universal postal services
corresponds to the service offering a given
controlled quality, provided in a permanent
regular way, during every working day, except
exceptional circumstances, on the entire national
territory at affordable prices for all fixed by
competent organ.
This service is provided in respect of the principles

of equity, continuity and adaptability by seeking
best economic and social efficiency.
Article 239: Content of universal postal service
The universal postal service includes the national
and international services as follows:
1° collection, sorting, routing and distribution
of postal letters up to two (2) kilograms;
2° collection, sorting, routing and distribution
of the postal parcels up to twenty
kilograms;
3° services relating to registered postal items;
4° services relating to the postal items with
declared value;
5° the issuance and payment of money orders;
6° any other services as may be determined
the competent organ.
Article 240: Postal financial services
The postal financial services include:

1° services of postal cheques as well as
whole of the services and operations
opening and management of current
accounts where their holders can deposit
money by means of any legally approved
process;
2° service of the money orders and all services
and operations of emission and payment
negotiable instruments for the execution
funds transfers;
3° service of the items to be recovered and
cash on delivery items and the whole of
services and operations of recovering
receipts, invoices, bills of sight, banking
drafts and delivered items paid by
recipient;
4° service of postal savings and other services
and operations intending to receive deposits
from private individuals and companies.
Such services are carried out in accordance with
relevant legal provisions.
Article 241: Exceptions to the operators of postal
services
The following services are not considered as postal
services governed by this Law:
1° transport of letters or items by a staff

member of an institution to or from the head
office or the branches of the postal operator;
2° transport of correspondences by embassies
and
international
organizations
accordance with relevant international
conventions;
3° transport of correspondence between
individuals or institutions by their
employees;
transport
of
correspondence by an individual;
4° transport of consignment notes.
Article 242: Compensation of the inequitable
costs
Inequitable costs supported under the universal
postal service by the operator in charge
compensated by:
1º a fund in charge of compensation,
financed by the fees paid by the postal
operators;
2º services reserved for the postal operator
charge of the universal postal service.
Article 243: Services of general interest
The Government establishes an operator in charge

of the universal postal service entrusted with
missions of general interest in order to contribute
1° the provision of services and operations
be performed free of charge or
preferential financial conditions for certain
users or to support certain activities;
2° the development of new Information
Communication Technologies;
3° the provision of postal services at
national level, on time and at an affordable
price;
4° any other matter as may be determined
competent organs.
There are concluded agreements between
Government and the operator in charge of universal
postal service.
Without prejudice to provisions of Paragraph one
this Article, the operator in charge of universal
postal service and other postal service operators
must support the Government to fulfil its following
missions:
1° defence and safety of the national territory;
2° implementation of the national policy
regarding environmental protection;

3° implementation of the national policy
regarding land use planning.
Section 3: Reserved postal services
Article 244: Reserved services
In return for additional costs for the distribution
universal postal services, the operator in charge
the universal postal service benefits from reserved
postal services.
A Prime Minister’s Order determines the list
reserved services provided by the operator in charge
of the universal postal services.
Article 245: Reserved rights of issuing postage
stamps and other postal values
The right to issue postage stamps and other postal
values marked “Republic of Rwanda” or any other
sign seal or symbol of the republic is reserved
the postal operator in charge of the universal postal
service.
This Article does not preclude the printing by postal
operators of stickers, tape or postage vouchers
billing of their services in compliance with laws
regulations.

Article 246: Franking items of correspondence
The postal items must be fully paid for and franked
by the sender.
Notwithstanding provisions of international
conventions to which Rwanda is a signatory, postal
items without franking are banned.
Article 247: The power of the operator to reduce
the price of items including newspapers
printed paper
The operator in charge of the universal postal
services has the power to reduce the price in relation
to issuing newspapers and periodicals published
the country, but the reduction should not exceed
fifty percent (50%) of the printed paper rate and
should be notified to the Regulatory Authority.
Article 248: The sale of reply coupons
The international reply coupons are sold only by
operator in charge of the universal postal service
and are exchangeable in all member countries of
universal postal union, against one or more postage
stamps representing the franking of a letter usually
in the first category weight shipped overseas.

universal postal service
Article 249: Designation of the postal operator

A Prime Minister’s Order appoints the postal

The sender of postal items retains ownership until
their delivery to the recipient, unless otherwise

Section 6: Licensing
Article 255: Prerequisites
Any person who wishes to carry out postal
operations or services must request an authorization

The Regulatory Authority determines

licensing

The Regulatory Authority may revoke a license

Section 8: Liability of postal operators
Article 261: Liability
The postal operator is responsible for the amount
money and postal items entrusted to its services
case the money or items:
1º delay in distribution;
2º are lost;
3º are incomplete when they reach
recipient.
The Regulatory Authority determines the modalities
of compensation in the above cases as well as
maximum compensation on the basis
international laws and conventions.
Article 262: Period of claims
Any claim for the liability of the postal operator
lapses within the agreed period but not exceeding
six (6) months, from the day following
submission to the office of the operator of
claimed consignment.

Section 9: Control of the postal services
Article 263: Control of the postal sector
The control of the postal sector is carried out by
Regulatory Authority.
Article 264: Mission of Regulatory Authority
postal matters
The Regulatory Authority prepares and implement
regulations for postal services:
1° to monitor the provision of the universal
postal service, as well as the development
the postal sector;
2° to ensure the respect by the authorized
postal operators of their obligations;
3° to analyse and issue licenses to postal
operators;
4° to define the mechanisms of setting tariffs
of the reserved service of the postal operator
in charge of universal service;
5° check and advise where necessary on
rates charged by private postal operators;
6° to determine, on the basis of suitable
accounting held by the operator in charge
the universal postal service, the costs
ascribable with the obligations of

universal service;
7° to ensure the respect by the operator
charge of the universal postal service of
standards of geographical and tariff
accessibility, and the quality standards
by the multi-annual contract signed by
state and the postal operator in charge of
universal postal service;
8° to ensure that the operator in charge of
universal service uses the sums paid by
funds of access to the universal postal
service in accordance with their objective.
