CHAPTER
PROVISIONS
ONE:
GENERAL
Article One: Purpose of this Law
This Law establishes and organizes the real

property valuation profession in Rwanda.
Article 2:
Definitions of terms
Under this Law, the following terms shall
defined as follows:
1° Institute: an association of certified
property valuers;
2° certified valuer: a competent person
authorized, under this Law, to conduct
real property valuation as a profession
compliance
with
standards
regulations applicable in Rwanda;
3° market value: estimated amount
which a property should exchange on
date of valuation;
4° real property: immovable property
including land, buildings, fences,
foundations and all other improvements
made to the land;
5° Council: authority regulating
profession of real property valuers

Rwanda;
6° real property valuation: process
determining the value of a real property
based on market value.
CHAPTER II: ESTABLISHMENT
THE INSTITUTE OF REAL PROPERTY
VALUERS
Article 3:
Establishment
Institute
of
An Institute of Real Property Valuers
Rwanda referred to as “the Institute” in
Law is hereby established.
The first meeting to launch the Institute
Real Property Valuers shall be convened
chaired by the Minister in charge of lands.
The Institute has legal personality
autonomy.
Article 4: Requirements to exercise
profession
Any person wishing to practise as a
property valuer in Rwanda shall have to be
member of the Institute.

Article 5: Head office of the Institute
The head office of the Institute shall
located in Kigali, the Capital City of
Republic of Rwanda.
The head office may be relocated elsewhere
on the Rwandan territory, where deemed
necessary.
Article 6: Responsibilities of the Institute
The responsibilities of the Institute shall be:
1 º to analyse and find solutions to
problems related to the real property
valuation profession;
2 º to analyse and find solutions to
problems related to the conduct
real property valuers;
3 º to exchange information relating to
real property valuation profession;
4 º to promote the real property valuation
profession in Rwanda;
5 º to prepare regulations and guidelines
governing the real property valuation
profession,

6 º to prepare real property valuation
standards;
7 º to represent the interests of,
advocate for real property valuers
Rwanda and abroad.
Article 7: Organs of the Institute
The Institute of real property valuers shall
comprise the following organs:
1 º the General Assembly;
2 º the Board of Directors;
3 º the Executive Secretariat.
Article 8: General Assembly of
Institute
The General Assembly comprises experts
real property valuation who are members
the Institute. The General Assembly shall
elect from amongst itself members of
Board of Directors and establish
Executive Secretariat which reports to
Board of Directors.
The organization and functioning of organs
the Institute shall be determined by its internal
rules and regulations.

CHAPTER
III:
REGULATION
COUNCIL
Article 9: Establishment of the Council
A council of regulation of the real property
valuation profession in Rwanda referred to
“the Council” in this Law is hereby
established.
The Council shall commence its activities
within ninety (90) days from the publication
of this Law in the Official Gazette of
Republic of Rwanda.
Article 10: Members of the Council
The Council shall be composed of seven
members as follows:
1° a representative of the National Bank
Rwanda;
2° a representative of the Bankers‟
Association of Rwanda;
3° a representative of the National Land
Centre;
4° a representative of the Ministry in charge
of Infrastructure;

5° a representative of the Private Sector
Federation;
6° two (2) representatives of the Real
Property Valuers‟ Institute elected by their
colleagues.
Members of the Council shall be appointed
an Order of the Prime Minister which
determines the Chairperson, the Deputy
Chairperson of the Council and one of them
shall be a real property valuer.
Members of the Council should be at least
knowledgeable in real property valuation.
Article 11: Responsibilities of the Council
The Council shall
responsibilities:
have the following
1 º to approve regulations and guidelines
governing the real property valuation
profession;
2 º to approve standards for real property
valuation;
3 º to register and remove a real property
valuer from the register of certified
valuers;

4 º to assess and monitor valuers‟ conduct
and practicing;
5 º to advise Government
functioning of the Institute;
6 º to submit a quarterly activity report
the Minister in charge of lands.
on
All instructions issued by the Council shall
published.
Article 12: Term of office for members
the Council
Members of the Council shall serve a five
year term of office, renewable only once.
The term of a Council member shall come
an end if :
1 º he/she dies;
2 º his/her term of office expires;
3 º he/she resigns in writing;
4 º he/she can not perform his/her duties
due to disability approved by
recognized medical doctor;
5 º he/she is sentenced to a term
imprisonment equal to or more than
six (6) months without suspension;

6 º he/she is absent from three
consecutive Council meetings without
sound reasons;
7 º he/she misbehaves;
8 º he/she confesses and pleads guilty
the crime of genocide;
9 º he/she obstructs interests of
Council;
10 º
he/she is removed from his/her
duties by the appointing authority.
A member of the Council wishing to resign
shall do so in writing to the Chairperson
the Council and give a thirty (30) day notice.
If a member of the Council leaves office
before the end of his/her term, the competent
authority shall appoint a substitute. The newly
appointed member shall complete the term
office.
Article 13: Convening
members of the Council
the meeting
Members of the Council shall meet once
quarter and whenever necessary. The quorum
required for members of the Council is
thirds (2/3) of all members.

Decisions are taken by consensus of
participants. If no consensus can be reached
then majority vote will decide. In case of
tied vote, the chairperson shall have a casting
vote.
Members of the Council can invite anyone
with expertise to its meetings depending
the agenda. The invited person shall neither
vote nor attend deliberations on other items
on the agenda.
Article 14: Decisions and minutes
Council meetings
Resolutions of Council meetings shall
signed by all members in attendance at
end of each meeting, a copy of the resolutions
shall be submitted to the Minister in charge
lands within five (5) working days for his/her
comments which should be communicated
the Council within fifteen (15) days from
day he/she received it. If no comments from
the Minister are given within that period,
resolutions shall be deemed approved.
Minutes of Council meetings shall be signed
by the Chairperson together with
Rapporteur and adopted at the next meeting.
A copy of the minutes shall be submitted

the Minister in charge of lands within fifteen
(15) working days from the day of
approval.
The decisions of the Council shall be notified
to the Institute of real property valuers.
Article 15:
allowances
Council
members‟
sitting
Members of the Council attending its meeting
shall receive sitting allowances determined
a Presidential Order.
Article 16: Conflict of interests
A member of the Council is prohibited from
being among the members who take decisions
in case the meeting is examining issues
concerning him/her, those concerning his/her
parents, relatives up to the second degree
in- laws up to the second degree or any issue
in which he/she has an interest. The
concerned person shall inform the Council
and shall disqualify him/herself at any time
the issue is under examination.
Article 17: Powers of the Council with
regard to registration
The Council shall have the following powers:

1 º to examine and decide on
application for registration of a
property valuer;
2 º to determine the format of the registry
and certificates for real property
valuers;
3 º to issue certificates to certified
property valuers;
4 º to determine fees for registration
issuance of valuer‟s certifícate;
5 º to determine real property valuation
charges;
6 º to punish valuers in accordance with
their code of conduct.

Section One: Procedures for registration
Article 18: Conditions for registration as
valuer
For a Rwandan citizen to be registered in
registry of property valuers, he/she shall meet

the following conditions:
1 º to hold at least a bachelor‟s degree
real
property
valuation,
civil
engineering, pedology or any other
degree with evidence of sufficient
knowledge in valuation;
2 º to have never been condemned to
imprisonment of more than six
months;
3 º to have never been dismissed from
association of real property valuers
either in Rwanda or in another
country;
4 º shall not be in bankruptcy.
Article 19:
Registration of foreigners
A foreigner who intends to practice
property valuation in Rwanda shall:
1 º meet the conditions provided
under Article 18 of this Law;
2 º hold a certificate of real property
valuer or its equivalent from
his/her country.

Article 20: Association
collaboration
and
A certified real property valuer may practice
either individually, or as part of an association
or company or in collaboration with another
certified real property valuer or group
certified real property valuers.
Collaboration shall be formed when a
property valuer commits him/herself to giving
his/her services in another real property
valuer‟s office either full time or part time
remuneration.
An agreement of association of real property
valuers is one by which two or more
property valuers decide to practice
profession together either in the same office
or in different offices and commit themselves
to share profits and losses.
Section 2:
certificate
Register and valuer‟s
Register of certified valuers
Article 21:
The Council shall create and maintain

register of certified valuers which shall
include at least the following:
1 º identification of the valuer;
2 º the date of entry into the register
of valuers;
3 º the address of the valuer ;
4 º the valuer‟s degree;
5 º the registration number of
valuer.
Artice 22:
Valuer‟s certificate
When an applicant has been entered into
register of valuers, the Council shall issue
him/her a certificate of certified valuer.
Article 23: Cancellation
certificate
of
valuer‟s
The Council may cancel the registration of
valuer if :
1 º he/she has been erroneously
fraudulently registered;
2 º he/she manifests poor professional
conduct including bribery and breach
of confidentiality;
3 º any other
reason
considered
necessary by the Council.

Article 24: Procedures for appeal
If an applicant is of the opinion that
Council‟s refusal to issue him/her with
certificate or cancellation of his /her
certificate is unfair, he/she may refer his/her
case to the relevant court of justice if
satisfied after having exhausted all other
means.
Article 25: Request for restoration to
valuers‟ register
A valuer who has been removed from
register by procedures provided in this Law
may apply for restoration to the register
according to the procedures set up by
Council if the reasons of his/her removal
cease to exist.
Section 3:
methods
Real
property
valuation
Article 26: People authorized to conduct
real property valuation in Rwanda
Only registered valuers members of
Institute shall be authorized to exercise
real property valuation profession in Rwanda.
However, Government employees fulfilling
requirements of the Council shall also
authorized to conduct valuation when

mandated by their government institutions.
Article 27: Valuation methods
Valuation of any type requires that the valuer
apply one or more valuation methods
provided by this Law or any other method
accepted by the Council. The valuer shall
select the best valuation method that can
used to determine the fair market value of
real property.
Comparable prices methods
The proposed price for the real property shall
be close or equal to the market value. The
valuer shall compare prices by referring to
prices recently assigned to a real property
is similar or comparable to the real property
subject to valuation.
Article 28:
Article 29: Comparison of land values
countrywide as an alternative land
valuation method
Where comparable prices are not available
land in a particular area, the valuer may
comparable prices of similarly classified land
from other areas of the country. Prices shall

vary depending on the quality and location
the land. The valuer shall fulfill his/her
valuation duties in compliance with principles
and regulations governing the valuation
profession and the Council.
Article 30: Replacement cost approach
as an alternative valuation method
improvements
Where sufficient comparable prices are
available to determine the value of improved
land, the replacement cost approach shall
used to determine the value of improvements
to land by taking real property as a reference.
Article 31: Use of multiple valuation
methods
Where real property valuation requires special
skills, the valuer shall use whatever
combination of the methods he/she considers
best suited to determine the current market
value. The methods used shall be clearly
explained in the valuation report.
Upon approval by the Council, a valuer may
use any other relevant worldwide methods

provided in this Law in order to carry out
assigned work.
Article 32:
Valuation report
The real property valuation report shall
written in a format set by the Council.
Article 33: Confidentiality
A valuer shall observe the requirements
confidentiality in collaboration with clients.
However, he/she may disclose
information if required by the Council or
Law.
Article 34:
Conflict of interests
A valuer shall consider and examine whether
there is any present or future conflict
interests when carrying out his/her
professional duties. If there is any, he /she
refrain from doing so, unless all parties
informed of the situation and consent to
real property valuer to continue the task.

Article 35:
Valuation without Certificate
Practicing real property valuation without
certificate shall be punished by the Law.
Article 36: Disputes
property valuation
related
to
real
Where a party does not agree with a
property valuation, he/she shall refer
matter to the Council.
In such case, the Council shall select other
certified valuers who shall decide other
valuation methods to be used. In case
dispute is not settled, it shall be submitted
competent court of Law.

Until the Council regulating the profession

Any person who has been practicing in

certification within six (6) months from

Article 39: Drafting, consideration and

This Law was drafted in English, considered
and adopted in Kinyarwanda.

All prior legal provisions contrary to this Law
are hereby repealed.

Article One: Scope of this Law
This Law governs electronic transactions,
electronic messages and prevention of misuse
of computers in electronic transactions,
electronic signature and all other applications
relating to information technology.
This Law shall not apply to use of hard copies
and which require signature of the issurer
these are:
1°
a will;
2°
negotiable instruments;
3°
a power of attorney;
4° a commercial agreement or other
document related to immovable property
or any interest in such property;

5° a certificate of transfer of immovable
property or any other interest attached
such property;
6° an authentic document or any document
deemed authentic.
Article 2: Definitions of terms
Under this Law, the following terms shall
defined as follows:
1° “addressee of an electronic message”:
person who is intended by the originator
to receive the electronic message,
does not include a person acting as
intermediary with respect to that data
message;
2° “authorised officer”: a person who
given competence by the controller
specified in article 66 of this law;
3° “certificate”: electronic message or
other message confirming the
between a signatory and the contents
the document;
4° “certification authority”: a natural
person or legal entity that issues
certificate;

5° “certification practice statement”:
statement issued by a certification
authority specifying the practices used
issuing certificates;
6° “communication network services”:
provision of connections, the operation
facilities for communication systems,
provision of access to communication
systems, transmission or routing of data
messages between or among points
specified by a user and the processing
and storage of data;
7° “computer program”: set of
instructions, codes, schemes or in
other form, which is capable, when
incorporated in a machine-readable
medium, of causing a computer
perform or achieve a particular task
result;
8° “controller” : Regulatory Authority
specified in item 25 of this article;
9° “corresponding”: a private key and

public key which belong to the same
pair;
10° “digital
signature”:
electronic
signature consisting of a transformation
of an electronic message using
asymmetric cryptosystem and a hash
function such that a person having
initial untransformed electronic message
and the signer's public key can accurately
determine:
a) whether the transformation
created using the private key
corresponds to the signer's public key;
b) whether the initial electronic message
is as it was after the transformation
was made;
11°
“digital signature certificate”:
electronic message issued for the purpose
of supporting digital signatures which
purports to confirm the identity of
person or other significant characteristics
certifying that the person holds the private
and the public keys;

12° “electronic message”: information
received, issued, sent or stored
electronic means or any other similar
means
including
electronic
data
interchange (EDI), such as electronic mail,
telegram, telex or telecopy;
13° “electronic payment order”: an order
