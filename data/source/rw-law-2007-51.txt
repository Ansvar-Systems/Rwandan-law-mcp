PROVISIONS
Article One: Scope of this Law
This Law determines the responsibilities,
organisation and the functioning of the Gender
Monitoring Office in national development,
referred to as the “Office” in the following
provisions.
Article 2: Definitions of terms
In this Law, these words have the following
definitions:
gender:
a
concept
that
refers
responsibilities, rights, opportunities, respect
and value a person receives from society
accordance with his/her sex, and which
likely to change in relation to time, culture
other criteria;
disparities: inequalities between
women and men in terms of their role in
activities, opportunities and rights in general;

gender equality: a situation where women
and men fully enjoy the same opportunities
fulfilling their human rights and utilising
potential, and gain access to
participation in all developmental activities
from a political, economic, social and cultural
perspective, and benefit equally from related
results;
gender based violence: any behaviour aimed
at sexual relations or any other sexual
behaviour which affects the dignity of a
or a female victim, whether such behaviour
may be from a superior at the work place,
school or whether from families as well
from elsewhere.
gender and development: the development
planning process that is based on analysis
integration of various situations and needs
women and men aimed at creating conditions
for the development of gender equality
complementarity.

gender mainstreaming in development
programmes: a process which aims
sensitising all organisations to recognise
gender and restore delays in gender
development programmes.
Article 3: Autonomy of the Office and
supervising Authority
The Office has a legal personality and
administrative and financial autonomy.
In fulfilment of its functions, the Office
be independent with no instructions from
other organ.
The Office shall be supervised by the Office
of the Prime Minister.
Article 4: The headquarters of the Office
The headquarters of the Office is situate
Kigali City, the Capital of the Republic
Rwanda. It may be transferred to elsewhere
Rwanda if considered necessary.
The Office operates in the whole country.

CHAPTER II: RESPONSIBILITIES
POWERS OF THE OFFICE
Section One: Responsibilities
Article 5: General responsibilities of
Office
In general, the Office has the following
responsibilities:
1° monitoring and carrying out evaluation
on a permanent basis of compliance
with gender indicators intended
respect gender in the context of
vision
of
sustainable
national
development and serving as
reference point on matters relating
gender equality and equity;
2° submitting to various institutions
recommendations relating to
program of gender promotion
national development;
3° monitoring the respect of the principle
of gender in national development
submit to the Cabinet its annual
programme of action and the activity
reports and reserve copies to
State organs mentioned in Article
of this Law.

Article 6: Specific responsibilities of
Office
In particular, the Office has the following
responsibilities:
1° monitoring on how the fundamental
principles of gender are respected
all organs at governmental, private,
non governmental and religious levels;
2° examining and monitoring the national
policy and programs intended
ensuring the promotion of gender
equality;
3° monitoring the existence of the policy,
programs as well as different projects
aimed at promoting gender equality,
their implementation and the system
of their budget allocation;
4° ensuring the implementation of
international
agreements
programmes relating to the respect
the principles of gender;
5° fighting against gender based injustice
and violence;
6° advocating for the respect of gender
equality at all levels;

7° raising awareness for all institutions
and the population to build a nation
which respects principles of gender;
8° disseminating national Laws
international Conventions aimed
promoting gender;
9° providing, upon request or at
initiative, opinion on the draft
policy and strategy documents or
other decisions relating to gender
equality;
10° encouraging all institutions
mainstream and to respect gender
equality in all their programs;
11° carrying out research based
statistics on specific issues in
framework of mainstreaming
respecting the principles of gender
disseminating the results
analysis;
12° developing
gender
awareness
indicators in all sectors;
13° identifying where there is gender
based inequalities in all national
bodies and providing a way to rectify
them;
14° proposing
to
the
relevant
administrative
institutions
strategies to be taken in order to avoid
violations of gender equality;

15° advising all institutions to respect
principles of gender equality;
16° building and increasing the capacity
the office and its staff.

under Articles 5 and 6 of this law, the Office

may be included in draft laws;

the relevant bodies for action
follow up ;

and
ensuring
settlement;

of gender inequality and to report

respect a “gender” sensitive budget;

them at source or requesting for
copies as well as any other document
which may help the Office
examining and collecting evidence
the violation of the principles
gender;

experts and other support staff of

CHAPTER III: ORGANISATION
FUNCTIONING OF THE OFFICE
Article 8: Organs
The organs of the Office are as follows:
1° the High Monitoring Council of
Office;
2° the Executive Secretariat;
3° the Consultative Committee.
Section One: The High Monitoring Council
of the Office
Article 9: High Monitoring Council of
Office
The High Monitoring Council is the supreme
organ of the Office in the overall management
and making decisions which help in
fulfilment of its responsibilities.
Article 10: Members of the
Monitoring Council of the Office and
appointment
The High Monitoring Council of the Office
shall be composed of the Chief Gender
Monitor and two (2) deputy Chief Gender
Monitors. They shall be Rwandans with
necessary integrity, discernment and ability
accomplish their responsibilities.

Among the High Monitoring Council
members, there shall be included at least
per cent (30%) of women.
At each post of the High Monitoring Council
of the Office, the Cabinet shall submit to
Senate the names of the candidate agreed
in the Cabinet meeting for approval.
The persons approved shall be approved
Presidential Order.
Article 11:
Monitors
Mandate of Chief Gender
The Chief Gender Monitor and the Deputy
Chief Gender Monitors shall be appointed
a term of five (5) and four (4)
respectively.
The term of office of the members of the
Monitoring Council of the Office may
renewed only once through procedures applied
in the first term.
Article 12:Taking oath by Chief Gender
Monitors
Before taking office, the Chief Gender
Monitor and the Deputy Chief Gender
Monitors shall take oath as provided for
Article 61 of the Constitution before
Supreme Court.

Article 13: Reasons for removal
member of the High Monitoring Council
The Chief Gender Monitor or the Deputy
Chief Gender Monitor shall leave such
office if:
1° his or her term of office expires;
2° he or she resigns through writing;
3° he or she is no longer able to perform
or her duties due to physical or mental
disability certified by an authorized
medical doctor;
4° definitively sentenced to a term
imprisonment equal to or exceeding six
months;
5° it is clear that he or she no longer fulfils
the requirements considered at the time
his or her appointment in such a post;
6° he or she jeopardises the interests of
Office;
7° he or she confesses and pleads guilty
the crime of genocide;
8° he or she is characterised by the ideology
of genocide;
9° he or she demonstrates behaviours
contrary to his or her responsibilities;
10° he or she demonstrates behaviours
contrary to the principles of gender;
11° he or she dies.

Article 14: Procedure for removal of
members of the High monitoring Council
If considered necessary, the decision
dismiss the Chief Gender Monitor or
Deputy Chief Gender Monitor from duties
shall be taken by the Senate upon request
the Cabinet meeting or by a third (1/3) of
Senators.
Resignation from duty by the Chief Gender
Monitor or the Deputy Chief Gender Monitor
shall be submitted via a registered mail or
requiring official receipt to the Prime Minister.
In default of any response within a period
thirty (30) days, the resignation shall
considered approved.
The Chief Gender Monitor or the Deputy
Chief Gender Monitor who leaves office
to any reason shall be replaced within a period
not exceeding three (3) months from the
he or she leaves office. The substitute
complete the remaining term of office.
Provisions of the preceding Paragraph of
Article shall not apply if the remaining period
of the term for the person to be replaced
not exceed six (6) months.

Article 15: Responsibilities of the Chief
Gender Monitor
In addition to the functions of the Office
she is required to implement, the Chief Gender
Monitor is particularly responsible for:
1° coordinating and directing the activities
the Office such that all the functions of
Office are fulfilled;
2° representing and advocating for the Office
in all national institutions and abroad;
3° convening and chairing the meeting of
Office;
4° submitting the report to the institutions
provided for by this Law;
5° informing all those concerned of
decisions taken;
6° ensuring the implementation of
decisions taken;
7° submitting to the relevant bodies views
relating to respect for gender, policy
documents, draft laws and to draft budget
laws at all stages;
8° convening and chairing the Consultative
Committee meetings.

Article 16: Responsibilities of the Deputy
Chief Gender Monitor in charge of gender
The Deputy Chief Gender Monitor in charge
of respect of gender in policies,
programs and projects is particularly
responsible for:
2° putting in place basic indicators in respect
of gender and to submitting them
various bodies ;
3° monitoring and coordinating the research
activities basing on statistics showing
general picture of gender in all bodies
the national life;
4° organising and providing debates
public, private and non governmental
religious organisations in order to respect
gender principles in all programmes;
5° organising debates for the public
national legislation in order for
principles of gender to be respected
programmes;
1° replacing the Chief Gender Monitor
case of his or her absence;

6° monitoring the respect of international
agreements and programs ratified
Rwanda to promote gender and preparing
a report thereof;
7° making the activity report and submitting
it to the Chief Gender Monitor;
8° attending the Consultative Committee
meetings;
9° performing any other activities which
in the responsibilities of the Office as
be assigned by the Chief Gender Monitor.
Article 17: Responsibilities of the Deputy
Chief Gender Monitor in charge of fighting
against injustice and gender based violence
The Deputy Chief Gender Monitor in charge
of fighting against injustice and gender based
violence is particularly responsible for:
1° receiving and examining complaints
relating to injustice and gender based
violence and forwarding them to the Chief
Gender Monitor in order for the Office
take decisions ;
2° collecting information relating to injustice
and “gender” based violence;

3° ensuring matters relating to respect
gender are included in budget of
institution;
4° preparing an activity report
transmitting it to the Chief Gender
monitor;
5° attending
Consultative
Committee
meetings.
6° performing any other activity which
the functions of the Office as may
assigned by the Chief Gender monitor.
Article 18: Powers of the High Monitoring
Council of the Office
The High Monitoring Council of the Office
shall have in particular power for:
1° adopting the draft internal rules
regulations of the Office which shall
established by an order of the Prime
Minister ;
2° approving the draft organisational
structure of the Office and the procedures
of the appointment of the personnel;
3° approving the draft budget and the use
finance;
4° approving the reports of the Office before
transmitting them to the concerned
authorities;

5° approving the annual activity report
submitted to the executive;
6° approving the necessary action plan
strategies in order for the office to fulfil
responsibilities;
7° taking decisions on the personnel
property management of the Office.
Article 19: Procedures on the conduct
meetings of the High monitoring Council
and the procedure of making decisions
The procedures through which the meetings
the High Monitoring Council of the Office
convene and the procedure of making
decisions shall be determined by the internal
rules and regulations of the Office.
Article 20: Benefits of the members of
monitoring Council
Salaries and other fringe benefits due to Chief
Gender Monitors shall be determined
Presidential Order.
Article 21: Organs that are given reports
The Office shall, each year, submit its plan
action and the activity reports to the Cabinet
and reserve copies for both Chambers of
Parliament and the Supreme Court.

Article 22: Reporting schedule
The Chief Gender Monitor shall submit to
Prime Minister the activity report of the Office
of the previous year and the plan of action
the year that has begun not later than March
of that year.
Section 2: The Executive Secretariat
Article 23: The Executive Secretariat
The Office has an Executive Secretariat
headed by an Executive Secretary appointed
by an Order of the Prime Minister.
Article 24: Staff recruitment
The Office shall recruit its support staff
daily activities in accordance with the general
statutes for Rwanda Public Service.
Article 25: Responsibilities of the Executive
Secretary
The Executive Secretary shall be in charge

1° monitoring the daily activities of
Executive Secretariat of the Office;
2° directing the staff of the Executive
Secretariat;
3° managing the use of the budget and
property of the Office;
4° preparing the draft of the organisational
structure of the Office and transmitting
to the High Monitoring Council of
Office;
5° preparing the draft of the plan of action
and the draft budget of each year
transmitting them to the High Council
the Office;
6° submitting to the High Monitoring Council
of the Office the activity report and
report relating to the use of finance and
management of property of the previous
year of the Office;
7° attending the meetings of the
Monitoring Council of the Office
serving as the rapporteur without the
to vote;
8° implementing the decisions of the
Monitoring Council of the Office;
9° gathering and keeping documents relating
to the activities of the Office;
10° attending
Consultative
Committee
meetings and acting as their rapporteur;

11° performing any other activity relating
the functions of the Office as may
assigned by the High Monitoring Council;
Article 26: Organisational structure
The organisational structure of the Office
be determined by a Prime Minister’s Order.
Section 3: Consultative Committee.
Article 27: Consultative Committee
The High Monitoring Council of the Office
shall be assisted by the Consultative
Committee composed of persons
different institutions. They shall be Rwandans
characterised by integrity, discernment
ability to fulfil their responsibilities.
Members of the Consultative committee
serve for a term of three (3) years which
be renewable only once.
At least thirty per cent (30%) of the members
of the Consultative Committee shall
women.

Article 28: Appointment of the members
the Consultative Committee
An Order of the Prime Minister shall appoint
and determine the number of the members
the Consultative Committee.
The members of the Consultative Committee
meeting shall be entitled to a sitting allowance
determined by a Presidential Order.
Article 29: Responsibilities
Consultative Committee
The Consultative Committee shall have
responsibilities for:
1° advising the High Monitoring Council
the Office in order to fulfil
responsibilities;
2° providing advice and suggestions on issues
the High Monitoring Office submits to
members of the Consultative Committee
or those they themselves identify;
3°
of
providing suggestions on the planning
report on the activity of the Office.

Article 30: Invitation and chairing of
Consultative Committee meeting
The meeting of the members of
Consultative Committee shall be convened
chaired by the Chief Gender Monitor and
the relevant Deputy Chief Gender Monitor
case of his or her absence.
The meeting of the Consultative Committee
shall be held once in six (6) months and at
time it is considered necessary in presence
at least a half (1/2) of its members.
The invitation of the Consultative Committee
meeting shall be in writing and indicate
on the agenda at least fifteen (15) days before
it is held.
Article 31: Reasons for removal
member of the Consultative Committee
A member of the Consultative Committee
shall leave such an office if:
1° his or her term of office expires;
2° he or she resigns in writing;
3° he or she is no longer able to perform
or her duties due to physical or mental
disability certified by an authorized
medical doctor;

4° definitively sentenced to a term
imprisonment equal to or exceeding six
months without any suspension ;
5° he or she is absent in meetings for two
consecutive times in a year with
justified reasons;
6° he or she no longer fulfils the requirements
considered at the time of his or
appointment in such an office;
7° he or she jeopardises the interests of
Office;
8° he or she confesses and pleads guilty
the crime of genocide;
9° he or she is characterised by genocide
ideology;
10° he or
she demonstrates behaviours
contrary to his or her responsibilities;
11° he or she demonstrates behaviours
contrary to the principles of gender;
12° he or she dies.
Article 32: Replacement of a member of
Consultative Committee
The Chief Gender Monitor shall report to
relevant body if any member of
Consultative Committee is not worth
membership or if he or she is no longer in
or her position in accordance with
provisions of Article 31 of this Law.
In case one of the members of the Consultative
Committee leaves his or her duties before
or her term of office expires, the relevant

authorities shall appoint his or her substitute
complete the remaining term of office.
Resignation of a member of the Consultative
Committee shall be tendered in writing to
Prime Minister via registered mail or one
requiring official receipt. In default of any
response within a period of thirty (30) days,
the resignation shall be considered approved.
A member of the Consultative Committee
leaves office due to any reason shall be
replaced within a period not exceeding three
(3) months from the period he or she leaves
office. The substitute shall complete the
remaining term of office of the person
replaced.
Provisions of paragraph 4 of this article shall
not apply if the remaining term of office of
person to be replaced does not exceed six
months.
CHAPTER IV: PROPERTY OF THE
OFFICE
Article 33: The property of the Office
The property of the Office shall comprise
movables and immovables..
It shall come from the following:

1° State budget;
2° subsidies, donations and bequest.
Article 34: Use and audit of budget
The budget of the Office shall be used
accordance with the laws governing public
finance,
The Auditor General of the State Finances
shall control the use of the budget of the
Office.
CHAPTER V: FINAL PROVISIONS
Article 35: Abrogating provisions
All previous legal provisions contrary to
Law are hereby abrogated.
Article 36: Commencement
This Law shall come into force on the date
its publication in the Official Gazette of
Republic of Rwanda.
