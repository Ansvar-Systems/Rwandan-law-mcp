CHAPTER
PROVISIONS
ONE:
GENERAL
Article One: Purpose of this Law
This Law governs the tax procedures.
Article 2: Taxes prescribed by this Law
Taxes prescribed by the tax procedures
provided for by this law are as follows:
1 º personal income tax;
2 º value added tax;
3 º property tax on vehicles and boats;
4 º tax on gaming activities;
5 º tax on precious metal
6 º tax on purchased goods;
7 º such other tax as may be prescribed
law.

Article 3: Definitions
For the purpose of this Law, the following
terms have the following meanings:
1 º controlled transaction: any transaction
between related persons;
2 º permanent establishment: a fixed
place of business through which
income generating business wholly
partially conducted;
3 º tax period: operating period of time
the end of which the tax is due;
4 º agent: a person appointed to act
behalf of another person or group
persons;
5 º Commissioner
General:
Commissioner General of the Rwanda
Revenue Authority;
6 º tax evasion: any deliberate act aiming
at evading whole or part of taxes
violation of tax laws;

7 º Minister: Minister in charge of taxes;
8 º Tax administration: Rwanda Revenue
Authority;
9 º authorized officer: an officer of the
Tax administration empowered by the
competent organ to conduct audit,
investigations, negotiate with the
taxpayer, make adjustments, prepares
and issues tax assessment notices
assessment, drafts affidavits and who
responsible for any other act necessary
to ensure the enforcement of the law
tax procedures and other laws
relation to collection of tax, and who
issued with means of identification
possess such powers;
10 º person: an individual, a company, a
facto company, a cooperative,
partnership, a subsidiary, a permanent
establishment, an association of persons
and any entity;

11 º substitute: a person who has the
quality to replace another in his or her
work during his or her absence;
12 º taxpayer: any person who is subject
tax according to the tax laws
Rwanda;
13 º large taxpayer: a taxpayer who has
been notified by the Tax Administration
that he or she is registered among large
taxpayers following the directives of the
Commissioner General;
14 º Fiscal Year: a period of twelve (12) months
that begins on January 1 and ends on December
31;
15 º budget year: a period of twelve (12)
months that begins on July 1 and ends on June
30 of the following year;
16 º Registrar General: a Government
Official whose duties are to provide
legal personality to companies and
make follow of their functioning.

Article 4: Authority responsible for
levying, collecting and managing taxes
The Tax administration is responsible
levying and collecting taxes.
The Tax administration deposits the tax
collected to the account of the Public
Treasury opened with the National Bank
Rwanda after deducting the amount
refund of extra taxes received save
charges deposited on the account of the tax
administration for services provided by the
tax administration in accordance with the
provisions of this law.
The amount referred to under Paragraph
of this Article is deposited to the account
the public treasury opened by the Minister
with the National Bank of Rwanda, based
on the percentage agreed upon between the
Tax Administration and the Minister.
The National Bank of Rwanda transmits
the Tax administration and the State
Treasurer a daily schedule of financial
situation showing the amount of taxes
deposited on the accounts mentioned
Paragraphs 2 and 3 of this Article.
The National Bank of Rwanda also
transmits to the Tax administration and the

State Treasurer a daily schedule of financial
situation showing the taxes collected under
financial instruments.
Article 5: Taxpayer address
The taxpayer addresses are the following:
1 º official head office;
2 º known residence;
3 º electronic address;
4 º other address communicated to the Tax
administration.
A taxpayer who changes address must
notify the Tax administration. Whenever
taxpayer has not notified the Tax
administration of his or her change
address, all his or her information
delivered to his or her last known address.
When a taxpayer has no known address, the
Tax administration delivers all his or her
relevant information through media.

Article 6: Means of communication
A communication between the Minister, the
Tax administration, the Commissioner
General and the taxpayer is delivered
through any of the following means:
1 º a registered letter;
2 º a written act or communication
delivered with acknowledgement
receipt;
3 º an official notification by the court
bailiff;
4 º electronic message.
When the recipient rejects the registered
letter or the notification by the bailiff, the
letter or the notification is considered
have been delivered. The post office or the
bailiff makes a statement to show the
rejection of the communication.
Where the electronic message has been duly
sent to the email address given by the
receiver, upon it’s accessible into the
network, it is deemed received and the non-
compliance of the content of the message

the receiver is considered to have voluntary
ignored it.
Article 7: Language of communication
All the information between the Minister,
the Tax administration, the Commissioner
General and the taxpayer is transmitted
one of the official languages.
Books, records, information and documents
required in this Law are written in one
the official languages.
Any information requested by the Tax
administration that is not in one of the
official languages is translated into one
those languages by the taxpayer, if the Tax
administration so requests.
When the taxpayer refuses to provide the
Tax administration with documents in one
of the official languages, the Tax
administration, at the expense of the
taxpayer, order such a translation with
invoice stating the amount due.

Article 8: Methods for calculating time
Where the tax legislation sets time within
which a certain act, confirmation
communication has to be made or delivered,
the time is calculated from the day
following the date shown by one of the
following:
1 º the postmark put on the registered
posted mail;
2 º a written act or communication
delivered with acknowledgement
receipt;
3 º an official notification by the court
bailiff;
4 º the date on which the electronic
message was delivered on the electronic
address provided by the receiver.
Days provided by this Law are calendar
days, unless the law provides otherwise.
A taxpayer or the Tax administration who
communicates to each other by post are
deemed to have discharged their respective
obligations on the date the postmark was
put on the a registered posted mail.

If the last day to respect provisions of the
tax legislation is an official holiday or a day
of weekend, provisions of the law are
respected on the following working day.
However, when the last day for declaration
and payment of tax at the end of budget year
coincides with a public holiday or a day
weekend the obligation for declaration and
payment of tax is executed on the working
day prior to the holiday or a day
weekend.
Article 9: Tax rulings
Subject to the provisions of other laws,
request or by his or her own initiative, the
Commissioner General issues an advance
tax ruling. In case this ruling is for the
public, it is published through a nationwide
media.
The rules of the Commissioner General
determine modalities for establishment
an advance tax ruling.

Article 10: Representative of a taxpayer
and his or her obligations
A taxpayer is allowed to be represented
one of the following persons:
1 º the guardian or any other person
responsible for custody of a minor or
person declared incapable;
2 º a legal or judicial administrator of
property or a legacy, and the heirs
such a property;
3 º the legal representative of a company;
4 º the partners in a partnership;
5 º the manager, or any other person with
mandate to represent the taxpayer;
6 º the curator of a company or any other
legal person in liquidation;

7 º any other person with mandate
represent the taxpayer.
A person mentioned under items 1°, 2º,
6º and 7º of paragraph one of this article
having the mandate to represent the
taxpayer must communicate the new
capacity or appointment to the tax
administration within a period of seven
days after obtaining the capacity or the
appointment.
Any person allowed to represent a taxpayer
is liable for tax obligations in the limit of
or her mandate.

Article 11: Registration duty
A person who sets up a business must
register with the Register General.
However, any other person who carries
non-commercial activities but which can
generate tax, has the responsibility
register to the Tax Administration.

A person who carries out any taxable
activity whose turnover is equivalent
twenty million Rwandan Francs (FRW
20,000,000) in the previous fiscal year,
five million Rwandan Francs (FRW
5,000,000) in the preceding calendar
quarter is required to register for Value
Added Tax within a period not exceeding
seven (7) days from the end of the
concerned year or quarter.
A person who is not required to register
Value Added Tax according to provisions
of Paragraphs 2 of this Article may
voluntarily register with the Tax
administration for Value Added Tax.
The taxpayer, who takes the initiative
register for the value-added tax at any time
of his commercial activity, registers with
the Registrar General. However, the
registration is done by the Tax
Administration if the taxpayer meets the
required registration turnover but fails
register.
Any changes related to the taxpayer or
or her activities is notified in writing to the
Tax administration within seven (7) days
from the day of the notice of the change.

The Registrar General issues rules
determining modalities for registration and
deregistration for persons who no longer
carry out business activities after
consultation with the Commissioner
General.
Article 12:
number
Taxpayer
identification
The Registrar General assigns a taxpayer
identification number to a taxpayer to
used for payment of any tax.
Every taxpayer must put a taxpayer
identification number on tax declaration
documents and on other correspondences
provided for by this Law and on any other
commercial documents or other proofs
or she files with the Tax administration.

CHAPTER III: ACCOUNTING AND
TAX DECLARATION
Section One: Books of accounts and
records and other requirements
Article 13: Books of accounts and
records
A person who carries out taxable activities
in Rwanda and has an annual turnover
exceeding twelve million Rwandan francs
(FRW 12,000,000) but not exceeding
twenty million Rwandan francs (FRW
20,000,000) must keep books of accounts
and records including the following
information:
1 º tax liability;
2 º withheld tax;
3 º a declaration of a tax withheld;
4 º sales record.
A person who carries out taxable activities
in Rwanda and has an annual turnover
exceeding two million Rwandan francs
(FRW 2,000,000) but not exceeding twelve

million Rwandan francs (FRW 12,000.000)
must keep only records of sales.
Article 14: Additional documents
A person who has an annual turnover
exceeding twenty million Rwandan Francs
(RWF 20,000,000) per year must keep the
following additional documents:
1 º a record of assets and liabilities;
2 º records of daily income and expenses
related to the business activity;
3 º records of purchases and sales of goods
and services related to his or her
business;
4 º records of stock inventory at the end
the accounting period;
5 º information related
transactions.

Article 15: Books of accounts and
records keeping
Books of accounts and records referred to
Articles 13 and 14 must be preserved for
period of five (5) years starting from
January of the year following the fiscal year
to which it relates.
Books of accounts and records must be kept
in the premises of the taxpayer or in any
other place located within Rwanda.
Article 16: Additional requirements for
company
In addition to the books of accounts and
records as provided for in Articles 13,
and 15 of this Law, any company must
follow an accounting system indicating
income and expenditure in accordance with
the accounting procedure in force
Rwanda.
A Company must also present an annual tax
declaration accompanied by a balance sheet
and full accounts, established on the day
closure of the tax period.

Article 17: Electronic invoice
A person who carries out any taxable
activity must issue an invoice generated
an electronic invoicing system certified
the Tax administration.
An Order of the Minister determines the
modalities and conditions to use
electronic invoicing system. It also
determines persons not required to use such
a system.
Article 18: Obligations of the user
electronic invoicing system
Any user of electronic invoicing system
bound by the following obligations:
1 º to possess an electronic invoicing system;
2 º to issue an electronic invoice to every
buyer regardless request thereof;
3 º to indicate real name of goods and related
tax rates on the electronic invoice

VAT registered persons;
4 º to indicate real name of goods and
related prices on the electronic invoice
for VAT unregistered persons;
5 º to notify the Tax Administration
failure of electronic invoicing system
within a time not exceeding six (6) hours;
6 º to refrain from deleting invoice except
sound grounds.
Section 2: Tax declaration
Article 19: Tax declaration
When the period for declaration of tax
arrives as provided for by law, the taxpayer
must make a declaration in Tax
administration.
Article 20: Request for extension of tax
declaration deadline
A taxpayer may apply to the Commissioner
General for an extension of the deadline

tax declaration if he or she provides valid
reasons for not declaring tax on time.
The taxpayer applies in writing to the
Commissioner General at least fifteen (15)
days before the last filing date of the
declaration.
The Commissioner General may,
writing, grant to the taxpayer an extension
of tax declaration deadline within ten (10)
days from the date the request was received.
Extension of tax declaration does not
suspend the accrual of interest of the
principal tax.
Section 3: Declaration and payment
withholding tax
Article 21: Time for declaration of Pay
As You Earn
A taxpayer whose annual turnover is equal
or less than two hundred million Rwandan
francs (FRW 200,000,000) declares and
pays quarterly the pay as you earn within
fifteen (15) days after the end of the quarter
to which the pay as you earn is related.

The taxpayer referred to in Paragraph One
of this Article may opt for a monthly
declaration of the Pay as You Earn.
The Commissioner General’s instructions
determine modalities of declaration and
payment of pay as you earn on a quarterly
basis.
Article 22: Records kept by withholding
agent
The taxpayer who withheld tax keeps and
makes available for audit by the Tax
Administration, records in relation to each
tax period which provide the following
information:
1 º payments made to a payee;
2 º tax withheld from those payments.
The taxpayer who withheld tax sends a copy
of records provided for in Paragraph One
this Article in a manner and form prescribed
by the Commissioner General.

AUDIT
AND
Section One: Audit
Article 28: Audit notice
The Tax administration informs the
taxpayer in writing, at least seven
working days before conducting an audit,
about the following:
1 º the audit to be conducted;
2 º the place where the audit is to
conducted and the possible duration
the audit;
3 º any document required to be audited
any information required.

If the taxpayer is not ready for audit, he
she writes to the Tax administration
requesting for a postponement which
should not exceed thirty (30) days and can
only be allowed once.
Article 29: Obligations of the taxpayer
during audit
During audit, the taxpayer must:
1 º provide tax auditors with appropriate
working environment;
2 º provide tax auditors with books and
records prescribed by this Law and
other related documents and provide
them with their copies.
Article 30: Unique audit principle
The Tax administration audits a taxpayer
only once on a type of tax and for a tax
period.
However, the Tax administration may
conduct a new audit only once in case
one of the following circumstances:

1 º complicity of the taxpayer and the tax
auditor to evade taxes or commit any
other act intending to non-payment
required tax;
2 º if the first audit was based on forged
documents;
3 º if the first audit was issue-oriented and
the Tax administration wants to conduct
a comprehensive audit,
4 º when the Commissioner General
cancels the first audit based on appeal.
Article 31: Additional information
In addition to books of accounts and records
provided for by this Law, the Tax
administration may request in writing
additional information or explanations from
the taxpayer. The taxpayer must provide the
requested information or explanations
writing within seven (7) working days, from
the time of receipt except in case of force
majeure.

The Tax administration may use any
information found from any source before
audit and investigation.
Article 32: Other sources of information
In case of audit by the Tax administration,
the following must provide all requested
information in relation with the taxpayer:
1 º all public or private institutions;
2 º other persons in case the Tax
administration needs to know the
structure and use of taxpayer’s property.
The institution or person requested
provide such information must do so within
fifteen (15) days, from receipt of the
request.
The provisions of Paragraph One of this
Article also apply to a person or
institution bound by secrecy.

Article 33: Use of available information
and evidence
The Tax administration uses all available
information and evidences, when:
1 º no tax declaration was carried out;
2 º the tax declaration was not certified
a competent person;
3 º the
tax
declaration
accompanied
by
all
documents;
was
not
necessary
4 º the taxpayer was unwilling to cooperate
with a tax audit officer or did not
provide the explanations requested;
5 º books and records were not filled
prescribed by law;
6 º there are serious indications of tax fraud
beyond reasonable doubt;
7 º the taxpayer did not provide sufficient
information on controlled transactions.

Article 34: Use of experts
The Tax administration, when considered
necessary, can seek the assistance
external experts in all tax audit related
activities.
Article 35: Audit procedure
Tax audit is conducted in one of the
following procedures:
1° contradictory procedure;
2° issue-oriented audit;
3° desk audit;
4° audit without notice.
Article 36: Contradictory procedure
Where the Tax Administration discovers
miscalculation,
an
omission,
understatement, a misrepresentation of a tax
or any other error in the tax declaration
assessment, the Tax Administration sends
draft note for rectification to the taxpayer
containing all the elements taken into

account for rectification to provide his
her opinion.
The draft note for rectification referred to
Paragraph One of this Article contains also
an administrative fine in case of non-
compliance with the tax laws.
Article
37:
procedure
Issue-oriented
audit
The Tax administration may proceed to
issue oriented audit when a comprehensive
audit is not necessary.
Disregarding the audit notice period
referred to in Article 28 of this Law, in case
of an issue-oriented audit, the Tax
administration must inform the taxpayer
writing, at least three (3) working days
before the audit is conducted.
In case of an issue-oriented audit, the
postponement of audit referred to in Article
28 of this Law cannot exceed seven
working days.

Article 38: Desk audit procedure
Desk audit is conducted by an auditor in
or her office on any of the following
grounds:
1 º if the turnover of Value Added Tax
not corresponding to the turnover
income tax without justification;
2 º if the tax declarations are
corresponding to paid taxes;
not
3 º if the taxpayer deducted from taxable
income non-deductible expenses;
4 º if one or more invoices were not
declared;
5 º in any other situation where the Tax
administration has sufficient documents
that can be used to assess taxes.
In case of the desk audit, it is not necessary
to inform in writing the taxpayer that he
she is audited. However, the taxpayer
invited for explanations before the tax
notice is issued to him or her.

Article 39: Giving opinion on draft note
rectifying the statement of contradictory
procedure
The taxpayer has the right to give his or her
written opinion on the draft rectification
note within thirty (30) days in case
contradictory procedure.
He or she may also submit to Tax
Administration additional evidence
explanations to indicate that the
rectification is incorrect. In responding
the draft note for rectification, the taxpayer
may request to provide additional
explanations orally.
When explanations provided by the
taxpayer reveal new information that leads
to an increase of the tax assessed or to a new
tax previously not assessed, the Tax
Administration issues a new draft note
rectification. In such case, the taxpayer

The Tax Administration issues to the

2 º after a period of five (5) working days,

any reaction of the taxpayer to the draft
note for rectification;
3 º after the Tax Administration has
considered the explanations of the
taxpayer on the draft note
rectification.
The final note for rectification provides
reasons of rejection of explanations
provided by the taxpayer and how the new
tax was calculated.

A taxpayer who is dissatisfied with the

the day of receipt of the tax assessment
notice or notification of administrative fine
Article 49: Conditions for admissibility

To be admissible, the file of appeal must:
1 º be in writing;

2 º identify the taxpayer and the taxpayer

3 º identify the tax period;
4 º specify the tax assessment
administrative fine imposed and the

5 º be signed by the taxpayer, his or her

Article 50: Decision on appeal
The Commissioner General makes
decision on the appeal in a period not
exceeding thirty (30) days from the date
receipt of the appeal and communicates it
the taxpayer in writing.
The Commissioner General or a competent
staff of the Tax Administration designated
by the Commissioner General may extend

this period for other thirty (30) days and
informs the taxpayer. In case no decision
made by the Commissioner General within
this period, the appeal is assumed to
valid.
In case the appeal is fully or partially
accepted, the Commissioner General
discharges the taxpayer from the respective
tax liability, interest and administrative
fine.
Article 51: New audit based on appeal
After consideration of the appeal, the
Commissioner General may cancel the
audit conducted and decide that a new audit
be conducted, if there are faults committed
by the taxpayer which were not disclosed
the audit and which resulted into reduction
of the tax.
The new audit is conducted in accordance
with the provisions of this Law.

Section 2: Amicable settlement and filing

Article 52: Right to request for amicable

A taxpayer who is dissatisfied with the
decision of the Commissioner General, may
request for an amicable settlement.
Rules of the Commissioner General
determine the modalities for amicable
settlement of tax related disputes.
Article 53: Filing a case to the court
If the two parties cannot resolve the dispute
amicably, the taxpayer may file a case to the
court within a period not exceeding thirty
(30) days from the date both parties fail
reach an amicable solution.
A taxpayer who is dissatisfied with the
decision of the Commissioner General may
also file a case to the competent court
within thirty (30) days from the receipt
the decision of the Commissioner General.
During the period in which the claim is filed
with the court, the obligation to pay tax

remains. Prior to the final decision on the
case, the Tax administration may recover
the tax assessed, the penalties and late
payment interests thereto.
However, the Commissioner General may,
upon request by the taxpayer, suspend the
tax recovery.
Article 54: Interest
administration
paid
by
Tax
If the taxpayer is discharged from tax,
interest or penalties by an administrative
judicial decision, or if no refund of excess

this Law, the Tax Administration must pay
interest on the due refund.
The interest rate provided for in Paragraph
One of this Article is calculated
accordance with the inter-bank offered rate
of the National Bank of Rwanda and is
on 1 st January of the fiscal year for the

day of payment until the day of refund

personally witnessed;
officer

CHAPTER VIII: TAX RECOVERY
Section One: Security and mortgage

Article 59: Security on movable property
The Tax administration has security
income and movable property of the

recovery of tax, interest, administrative fine
and the cost of recovery.
The security concerns also income and
movable property of representatives of the

Article 60: Mortgage
The Tax administration has a mortgage

taxpayer wherever it may be located
recovery of tax, interest, administrative fine
and the cost of enforcement.
The mortgage applies to immovable
property of representative of the taxpayer

Security on movable property and mortgage

legal recourse, the security and the

The Commissioner General requests
registration of the security and mortgage.
He or she also requests for its cancellation.
Section 2: Warning and seizure of the

Article 62: Warning
If tax, interest, administrative fine and

The period of fifteen (15) days referred

If the taxpayer does not pay within the

the Tax administration may seize any

person.
Subject to provisions of Articles 60 of this
Law, in case of seizure of moveable
immovable property, the security
mortgage preserves until seized property
sold or accorded a waiver of payment.

taxpayer

Subject to provisions of Articles 27 and 63,

the

CHAPTER IX: INTEREST ON LATE
PAYMENT AND PENALTIES
Section One: Interest for late payment
Article 76: Interest for late payment
If the taxpayer fails to pay tax within the
period provided for by this Law, he or she
must pay late payment interest on the
amount of principal tax.
The interest rate is fixed at one point five
percent (1.5%). Interests for late payment
are calculated on a monthly basis, non-
compounding, counting from the first day
after the tax should have been paid until
day of payment inclusive. Every month that
begins is considered as a complete month.
Interests for late payment accrue shall not
exceed one hundred percent (100%) of the
amount of tax.
Interests for late payment are always
payable, even when the taxpayer has lodged
an administrative or judicial appeal against
the assessment.
Interests for late payment do not apply on
taxpayer who discloses himself or herself

and pays the due taxes before he or she
notified of imminent audit. The disclosure
leads to exemption from interests only if
is done by a taxpayer who is not registered
with the Tax administration and who
discloses himself/herself in a period not
exceeding one (1) year starting from the
date the tax was due.
When the taxpayer pays, the payment
used to pay tax liability in the following
order:
1° principal tax;
2° administrative fine;
3° interests for late payment.

(50%) of the amount of value added tax
output for the entire period of operation
without value added tax registration,
where Value Added Tax registration
required;
2° an administrative fine of one hundred
percent (100%) of the value added tax
indicated in the invoice and payment
that tax as indicated on that invoice,
a person who issued a value added tax
invoice when he or she is not registered
for value added tax.
A public institution which fails to withhold
the value added tax or which withheld value
added tax and failed to pay the tax withheld
to the Tax Administration, must pay the Tax

not withheld or not paid, fines and default
interests as provided for by this Law.
Article 85: Failure to use electronic
invoicing system by a person registered
for the Value Added Tax
A person registered for the Value Added
Tax who sells goods or services without
issuing an electronic invoice is liable to
administrative fine of ten (10) times the
value of the evaded Value Added Tax.
In case of the fault is repeated, the defaulter
is liable to an administrative fine of twenty
(20) times the value of the evaded Value
Added Tax.
Article 86: Non-compliance with
obligations of the user of electronic
invoicing system
A person who fails to comply with
obligations of the user of the electronic
invoicing system provided for under Article
18 of this law is liable to an administrative
fine of two hundred thousand Rwandan
francs (FRW 200,000).

In case of the fault is repeated, the
administrative fine is increased to four
hundred thousand Rwandan francs (FRW
400,000).
A person registered to the Value Added Tax
who carries out a taxable transaction and
delivers an electronic invoice with under-
valued price or quantity of goods or services
is liable to an administrative fine of ten (10)
times the value of the evaded value added
tax.
In case of the fault is repeated, the
administrative fine is increased to twenty
(20) times the value of the Value Added
Tax evaded.
Section 3: Penal provisions
Article 87: Offense of tax evasion
A person who, while intending tax evasion,
commits one of the following acts:

1º use of forged documents in his or her
accounts;
2º counterfeit and use of documents
materials of the tax administration used
for taxation;
3º hiding taxable goods or assets related
to business;
4º making a declaration indicating that
the taxpayer has not made sales;
5º changing the trade name by a person
prosecuted in relation to tax;
6º fraudulent registration of trade under
the name of another person;
7º hiding accounting documents from the
tax administration or damaging them;
8º use of forged accounting records;
commits an offence of tax evasion.

Upon conviction, he or she is liable
imprisonment for a term of not less than two
(2) years and not more than five (5) years.
Article 88: Waiver of penalties for
taxpayer who makes self-disclosure
The default interests and administrative fine
related to the non-payment and non-
declaration of tax referred to in this Law
not apply to the taxpayer who makes self-
disclosure to the Tax Administration and
pays due taxes that he or she didn’t pay
before being notified that he or she
notified of imminent audit.
The self-disclosure leads to exemption from
the payment of default interests and
administrative fine only if it is done by
taxpayer not registered with the Tax
Administration or by another registered
taxpayer who is no longer audited
accordance with the provisions of this Law.
Article 89: Additional sanctions
Any person who commits a fault or
offence provided for by this Law may
liable to the following additional sanctions:

1 º closure of business activities for
period of thirty (30) days;
2 º to be barred from bidding for public
tenders;
3 º withdrawal of a business register;
4 º to be published in the media.
Except the sanctions provided for in items
1° and 4° of Paragraph One of this Article
imposed by the Commissioner General,
other sanctions provided for by that
Paragraph are ordered by the court
depending on the gravity of the offence
committed.
Article 90: Non-obstruction of the
payment of the tax due and criminal
proceedings against a taxpayer
Administrative sanctions provided for
this Law do not cancel either the payment
of the tax due or the criminal proceedings
against the concerned taxpayer.

CHAPTER X: MISCELLANEOUS
AND FINAL PROVISIONS
Article 91: Confidentiality
A person with the capacity or who
employed in tax collection is prohibited
from disclosing any information about the
tax status or use of the property of
taxpayer.
However, there is no duty of confidentiality
if:
1 º the authorized officer provides
information obtained in the course of
tax
proceeding
to
the
Tax
administration or law enforcement
officers for use in tax matters or
criminal prosecution for a tax-related
offence;
2 º the Minister receives and uses,
statistical or analytical purposes, the
information collected in the tax
proceeding in a form that does not
reveal the identity of taxpayers;

3 º the taxpayer releases other participants
in a tax proceeding from the duty
confidentiality.
The authorization provided for in item 3 o
Paragraph 2 of this Article must be
writing and may be limited to specific
information, a specific purpose or a specific
person.
Article 92: Counsel and representation
The taxpayer has the right to be represented
by a qualified professional during any
communication
with
the
Tax
administration. The professional holds
power of attorney with a signature of the
taxpayer and the date on which it was given.
However, an advocate is not required
hold such a power of attorney.
Rules of the Commissioner General
determine the conditions and functioning
qualified professionals who represent
taxpayers.
Except for an advocate, a qualified
professional referred to in Paragraph One
this Article may be suspended by the
Commissioner General for a period not
exceeding three (3) years, if the qualified

professional contravenes provisions of this
Law or related rules.
The suspended professional may appeal
against this suspension to the Minister in
period not exceeding fifteen (15) days from
the day of receipt of suspension letter. The
Minister makes a decision on such
appeal in a period not exceeding sixty (60)
days.
Article 93: Payment of Value Added Tax
on public tenders
The Value Added Tax on public tenders
withheld by the procuring public entity.
The public procuring entity has the
responsibility to submit a tax declaration
a manner and a form prescribed by the
Commissioner General and pay the tax
withheld within fifteen (15) days following
the end of the month in which the successful
bidder has submitted the invoice.
A Value Added Tax registered taxpayer
must notify the Tax administration
information regarding the tax withheld.

Article 94: Tax policy committee
There is established a Tax Policy
Committee in charge of advising the
Minister on all policies and issues related
taxation.
An Order of the Minister determines the
composition, organization and functioning
of the Tax Policy Committee.
Article 95: Procedure for granting
benefits under agreements for avoidance
of double taxation and prevention of tax
evasion
The benefits provided by the agreements
avoidance of double taxation and
prevention of tax evasion are granted after
validation of the residence certificate for tax
purposes in the manner determined by the
Tax administration.
The Tax administration establishes
guidelines for procedure, mutual agreement
and assistance in collection of taxes
provided for in the agreements
avoidance of double taxation and
prevention of tax evasion.

Article 96: Award for denunciation of tax
evasion
An Order of the Minister determines the
award given to a person for denouncing
tax evasion.
Article 97: Drafting, consideration and
adoption of this Law
This Law was drafted, considered and
adopted in Ikinyarwanda.
Article 98: Repealing provision
Law nº 25/2005 of 04/12/2005 on Tax
Procedures as modified and complemented
to date and all prior legal provisions
contrary to this Law are repealed.
Article 99: Commencement
This Law comes into force on the date of
publication in the Official Gazette of the
Republic of Rwanda.
