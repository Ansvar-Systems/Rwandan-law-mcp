CHAPTER ONE: GENERAL PROVISIONS
Article One: Purpose of this Law
This Law determines the mission, organisation
and functioning of the Rwanda Academy
Language and Culture hereinafter referred to
the ‘Academy’.
Article 2: Definition of terms
In this Law, the following terms shall be defined
as follows:
1° academy: a national institution in charge
development of sovereignty of language
culture;
2° academician: an expert in matters relating
to Rwandan language and culture;
3° chairperson: a head of the academy;
4°
expert:
a
person
who
has
a
particular
knowledge in Rwandan language
culture;
5° art: ability to create an innovative work
matters relating to language and culture;

6° cultural: quality of any object serving as
basis for a culture or its components;
7° culture: a set of patterns peculiar to a certain
society of human beings expressed through their
thinking, deeds or actions, behaviour in
know-how and speech that are specific to
society;
8° cultural vehicle: anything allowing
ensure respect for fundamental cultural values;
9° probity: behaviour, habits and customs
beliefs characteristic of a person;
10° artistic creation: ability to create
innovative work that seeks to express an idea
an aesthetical manner;
11° literature: creation based on ideas
thoughts expressed either in oral or written form
;
12° talent: innate gift;
13° experienced person: a person
possesses knowledge based on experience.

Article 3: Legal personality
The Academy has a legal personality and
administrative and financial autonomy.
It shall last for an indeterminate period.
Article 4: Head office and supervising
authority of the Academy
The head office of the Academy is established
in Kigali City, the Capital city of the Republic
of Rwanda. It may be transferred elsewhere
Rwanda if deemed necessary.
The Academy may have branches elsewhere
the country if deemed necessary in order
accomplish its mission upon approval by
Order of the Prime Minister.
The Academy is supervised by the Ministry
charge of culture.
CHAPTER II: MISSION AND POWERS
Article 5: Mission of the Academy
The main mission of the Academy is to ensure
implementation of provisions of Articles 50
51 of the Constitution of the Republic

Rwanda of 4 June 2003 as amended to date.
It has also the following specific responsibilities
:
1° to safeguard and promote language
culture;
2° to analyse Rwandan language and culture
all their forms;
3° to commend excellent works in the domain
of language and culture;
4° to advise, upon request or on own initiative,
on matters relating to language and culture;
5° to take decisions in matters relating
language and culture;
6° to identify the structure of the Rwandan
language, ensure and promote its integrity
its oral and writing aspects and other various
usages;
7° to enrich the Rwandan language for its
in all areas of knowledge;
8° to approve the appropriate use of Rwandan
language at all levels;
9° to prevent the disappearance of other
dialects of the Rwandan language used
the country;
10° to ensure the value of the Rwandan language

in competition with other languages used
the country;
11° to promote the use of Kinyarwanda
Rwanda and abroad;
12° to participate in designing the policy of
use of the languages used in the country
to ensure their implementation;
13° to approve fundamental principles of
Rwandan culture as a source and pillar
sustainable development;
14° to approve Rwandan cultural standards
values and show their role in general
knowledge;
15° to ensure that the Rwandan culture
maintains its true value in education of
Rwandan society in general and the youth
particular;
16° to ensure the integrity of the Rwandan
culture in comparison with other foreign
cultures;
17° to determine the standards relating
research in all art and artistic creation
levels;
18° to promote cultural industry in particular;
19° to determine appropriate methods of storing
heritage and cultural symbols;

20° to determine appropriate mechanisms
protect art and literature;
21° to collaborate with other local, regional
international institutions with similar
responsibilities.
CHAPTER III: ORGANISATION AND
FUNCTIONING OF THE ACADEMY
Section One: Organs of the Academy
Article 6: Organs
The Academy is composed of the following
organs:
1° the General Assembly of the Academy;
2° the Bureau of the Academy;
3° the Executive Secretariat.
Sub section One: General Assembly
Article 7: Supreme organ of the Academy
and its members
The General Assembly is the supreme organ
the Academy. It is made up of all academicians
of the Academy.

Article 8: Responsibilities of the General
Assembly
The General Assembly of the Academy shall
have the following main responsibilities:
1°
to consider and approve all activities
may facilitate the Academy in achieving
mission;
2°
to adopt the draft internal rules
regulations of the Academy;
3°
to consider and approve the annual
of action and strategic plan of the Academy;
4°
to consider and adopt the draft annual
budget of the Academy;
5°
to consider and approve the annual
activity report and budget execution report;
6°
to take decisions based on the evaluation
and financial audit reports of the Academy
and to take related decisions;
7°
to approve donations, grants
bequests to the Academy;
8°
to monitor performance of
administration of the Academy and
personnel.

Article 9: Convening and holding
General Assembly of the Academy
The General Assembly of the Academy shall
held once quarterly and whenever so required
at the invitation of the chairperson of
Academy, in case of his / her absence at
invitation of the Vice Chairperson in charge
Culture, on their own initiative or upon request
by a third (1/3) of the academicians of
Academy. The invitation shall be in writing
submitted to Academicians at least fifteen
days before the meeting is held.
However, the extraordinary meeting shall
convened in writing at least five (5) days before
it is held.
In order for the Academy General Assembly
be held, there shall be at least eight
academicians.
Items to be examined in the first quarter of
year include approval of the activity report
the previous budget execution report. In
third quarter, items on the agenda shall include
examination of the draft annual budget
action plan of the following year.

The General Assembly of the Academy shall
also quarterly consider the financial and activity
report relating to the previous quarter
transmit it to the Minister supervising
Academy.
Article 10: Mode of taking decisions
Decisions shall be taken by consensus. If
possible, decisions shall be taken by absolute
majority vote of members present.
Article 11: Invitation of any other resourceful
person in the Academy General Assembly
The General Assembly of the Academy
invite to its meeting any person from whom
may seek advice on a certain item on
agenda. The invited person shall not be allowed
to vote and to follow debates on other items
the agenda.
Article 12: Resolutions of the General
Assembly of the Academy and their
notification
Resolutions of the General Assembly of
Academy shall be signed by its members
soon as the meeting ends and a copy thereof
shall be transmitted to the Minister supervising
the Academy in a period not exceeding five

days for his/her views and opinions within
fifteen (15) days from its receipt. If this period
expires with no reaction from the Minister,
resolutions shall be deemed definitively
approved.
Resolutions of the Academy relating to
responsibilities referred to under Article 5
this Law that must be made public to Rwandans
shall be published in the Official Gazette of
Republic of Rwanda.
Article 13: Minutes of the Academy General
Assembly
Minutes of the meeting of the General
Assembly of the Academy shall be signed
the Chairperson of the Academy and
rapporteur and be approved in the following
meeting. The copy of the minutes shall
submitted to the Minister supervising
Academy in a period not exceeding fifteen
days from the day of their approval.
Article 14: Sitting
academicians
allowances
for
A Presidential Order shall determine sitting
allowances to be given to the academicians
duties of the Academy.

Sub section 2: Bureau of the Academy
Article 15: Members of the Bureau of
Academy
The Bureau of the Academy is composed of:
1° the Chairperson of the Academy;
2° the Vice Chairperson of the Academy
charge of culture;
3° the Vice Chairperson of the Academy
charge of language.
Article 16: Meetings of the Bureau of
Academy
An ordinary meeting of the Bureau shall be
at least once a month and whenever so required.
Article 17: Responsibilities of the Bureau
the Academy
The Bureau of the Academy is responsible for:
1° preparing meetings of the General Assembly
of the Academy;
2° taking urgent decisions when the General
Assembly is unable to meet, notifying
the decisions taken in the following
meeting;

3° monitoring activities of the Executive
Secretariat.
Article
18:
Responsibilities
Chairperson of the Academy
The Chairperson of the Academy is responsible
for:
of
1° convening and chairing meetings of
General Assembly and of the Bureau of
Academy;
2° representing the Academy before the Law;
3° promoting relations of the Academy with
other local and foreign organs;
4° performing any other duties as may
assigned by the Academy General Assembly
with regard to his/her responsibilities;
5° submitting activity and financial report
the Minister in charge of culture.
Article 19: Responsibilities of the Vice
Chairperson of the Academy in charge
culture
The Vice-Chairperson of the Academy in charge
of culture is responsible for :

1° deputizing for the Chairperson of
Academy and replacing him/her in case
his/her absence;
2° monitoring the activities of the Academy
matters relating to culture as mentioned
under Article 5 of this Law;
3° performing any other duties as may
assigned by the Academy General Assembly
with regard to his/her responsibilities.
Article 20: Responsibilities of the Vice
Chairperson of the Academy in charge
language
The Vice Chairperson of the Academy
charge of language is responsible for :
1° monitoring the activities of the Academy
relating to language as mentioned under
Article 5 of this Law ;
2° performing any other duties as may
assigned by the Academy General
Assembly with regard to his/her
responsibilities.

Sub section 3: Executive Secretariat
Article 21: Composition, responsibilities
functioning of the Executive Secretariat
The Executive Secretariat shall be composed
the Executive Secretary and other necessary
staff members.
The Executive Secretariat is responsible for
accomplishment of the missions of the Academy
referred to under Article 5 of this Law
coordination of activities of the Academy.
The structure and job classification of
Executive Secretariat shall be determined by
Prime Minister’s Order on request by
Minister supervising the Academy after
consultation with the Academicians.
The personnel of the Executive Secretariat shall
be appointed and managed in accordance with
the General Statute for Rwanda Public Service.

Article 22: Appointment and dismissal of
Executive Secretary of the Academy
The Executive Secretary of the Academy shall
be appointed and dismissed by a Prime
Minister’s Order.
Article 23: Responsibilities of the Executive
Secretary
The Executive Secretary shall be responsible
the daily management and administration of
Academy. He / she shall coordinate the daily
activities of the Academy and shall
accountable to the General Assembly for
implementation of its decisions.
He/she shall particularly be responsible for:
1° coordinating all activities of the Academy;
2° serving as the rapporteur of the meetings
the General Assembly and of the Bureau
the Academy;
3° implementing resolutions of the General
Assembly and of the Bureau of
Academy;
4° directing and coordinating the activities
departments;
5° preparing the draft internal rules
regulations of the Academy;
6° preparing the draft development plan of

Academy;
7° preparing the draft budget proposal of
Academy;
8° preparing the draft plan of action
strategic plan of the Academy;
9° preparing the draft proposal of the annual
report on activity and execution of
budget of the Academy;
10° preparing and following up the training
programmes for the staff and evaluating
their performance;
11° performing any other duties as may
assigned by the Bureau of the Academy with
regard to the mission of the Academy.
There shall be concluded, between the General
Assembly of the Academy and the Executive
Secretariat, a contract of performance indicating
the obligations of each party and the conditions
required in order for the Academy to fulfil
mission.
Section 2: Members of the Academy
Article 24: Members of the Academy
their term of office
The Academy shall be composed of fifteen
persons characterised by integrity
experience in the field of culture and language.

They are referred to as academicians. At least
thirty per cent (30%) of them shall be females.
Members of the Academy shall serve
renewable term of office of five (5) years.
Article 25: Appointment of members of
Academy
Academicians shall be appointed by
Presidential Order after approval by the Senate.
Article 26: Conditions for being a member
the Academy
For a person to be an academician, he/she shall
be quired to:
1° be a Rwandan by nationality;
2° be characterised by integrity;
3° be an expert in matters relating to culture
language;
4° not to have been deprived of civil
political rights by court decision;
5° not to have been definitively sentenced
term of imprisonment equal to or exceeding
six (6) months without suspension
sentence;
6° be free from prosecution for the crime
genocide ideology;

7° free from being prosecuted for the crime
genocide.
Article 27: Oath
Before taking office, Academicians shall take
oath before the Prime Minister in these words:
« I,……………………; solemnly swear
the Nation that I shall:
1° remain loyal to the Republic of Rwanda
2°
3°
4°
5°
uphold the Constitution and other laws;
safeguard the basic individual human rights
and the interests of the Rwandan people;
work for the consolidation of national
unity;
diligently fulfil
the responsibilities
entrusted to me;
6° never use the powers conferred on me
personal ends;
Should I fail to honour this oath, may I face
rigours of the law.
So help me God».

Article 28: Restrictions to Academicians and
companies in which they are shareholders
Academicians or companies in which they hold
shares shall not be allowed to carry out any duty
remunerated in the Rwanda Academy
Language and Culture nor compete for
tenders.
Article 29: Cessation
Academician
of
the
duty
The duty of an Academician shall cease in case
1° his/her term of office expires;
2° he/she resigns in writing;
3° he/she no longer able to perform his/her
duties due to physical or mental disability
certified by an authorized medical doctor;
4° he/she is definitively sentenced to a term
imprisonment equal to or exceeding six
months without suspension of sentence;
5° he/she is absent in meetings for three
consecutive times in a year with no sound
reasons;
6° he/she no longer fulfils the requirements
considered at the time of his/her
appointment to the Academy;
7° he/she demonstrates behaviour contrary
his/her responsibilities;
8° he/she jeopardises the interests of

Academy;
9° he/she confesses and pleads guilty to
crime of genocide;
10° he/she is characterised by signs of ideology
of genocide;
11° he/she dies.
The Academy shall indicate, in accordance with
provisions of the preceding paragraph, that
of its members is no longer in his/her position
that his/ her post is vacant and the appointing
organ shall approve it.
In case one of the members of the Academy
leaves office before completion of the term
office, he/she shall be replaced in accordance
with provisions of Article 26 of this Law.
person appointed shall complete the term
his/her predecessor.
CHAPTER IV: PROPERTY OF THE
ACADEMY
Article 30: Source of property of the
Academy
The property of the Academy shall be composed
of movable and immovable property.
It shall come from the following sources:
1° State budget allocation;

2° income from services rendered by
Academy;
3° grants, donations and legacy.
Article 30: Use, management and audit of
property of the Academy
Use, management and audit of the property
the Academy shall be carried out in accordance
with relevant Laws.
The internal audit unit shall submit a report
the General Assembly of the Academy with
copy to the Executive Secretariat.
Article 32: Adoption and management of the
Budget of the Academy
The budget of the Academy shall be adopted
and managed in accordance with relevant laws.
CHAPTER V: TRANSITIONAL
FINAL PROVISIONS
AND
Article 33: Drafting, consideration
adoption of this Law
This Law was drafted, considered and adopted
in Kinyarwanda.

Article 34: Repealing provision
All prior legal provisions inconsistent with
Law are hereby repealed.
Article 35: Commencement
This Law shall come into force on the date of
publication in the Official Gazette of
Republic of Rwanda.
