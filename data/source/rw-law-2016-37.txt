CHAPTER
PROVISIONS
ONE:
GENERAL
Article One: Purpose of this Law
This Law determines the organisation,
jurisdiction, competence and functioning
of Abunzi Committee.
Article 2: Establishment of an Abunzi
Committee at the Cell and Sector levels
There is established an Abunzi Committee
at the Cell level with jurisdiction over
Cell. An Abunzi Committee of appeal
the Sector level with jurisdiction over
Sector is also established.
Article 3: Definitions of terms
For purposes of this Law, the following
terms have the following meanings:
1º decision: a decision made
deliberations by a Panel of Abunzi,
which is notified to the disputing
parties immediately after

conciliation session and recorded
minutes;
2º minutes: a written record of details
about the conciliation process;
3º verdict: a document describing
how a dispute referred to Abunzi
Committee has been settled.
Article 4: Conciliation organ
Abunzi Committee is an organ responsible
for conciliating parties involved in disputes
under its jurisdiction.
ni The service of members of Abunzi
Committee is performed on a voluntary
and non-remunerative basis.
Article 5: Conditions for admissibility
a dispute referred to the Abunzi
Committee
A dispute referred to the Abunzi
Committee is admissible only if
applicant or the respondent has
standing, interest and capacity to refer
dispute.
Where the applicant or the respondent
lacks legal capacity, he/she is represented

by his/her guardian or by the person
exercising parental authority over him/her.
Where the dispute referred by a person
lacking legal capacity involves his/her
guardian or the person exercising parental
authority over him/her, the guardianship
council designates a person to represent
him/her.
CHAPTER II: ORGANISATION
THE ABUNZI COMMITTEE
Article 6: Composition of the Abunzi
Committee and term of office of
members
At the Cell and Sector levels, the Abunzi
Committee is comprised of seven
persons of integrity who must all
residents of the Cell and the Sector
respectively and well-known for their
conciliation skills.
Members of Abunzi Committee are elected
for a renewable term of five (5) years.
At least thirty percent (30%) of members
of Abunzi Committee must be females.

Article 7: Elections
Committee members
of
Abunzi
Members of Abunzi Committee are elected
by the Cell Council or the Sector Council
respectively, from among people other
than high authorities, Cabinet members,
Parliamentarians, staff of judicial organs,
security services, local administrative
entities and others whose duties
incompatible in accordance with specific
laws.
Elections are prepared, organized
supervised by the National Electoral
Commission.
A Presidential Order specifies modalities
for electing Abunzi Committee members.
Article 8: Oath of office of Abunzi
In a period not exceeding seven (7) days
after the elections, elected members
oath before the population and
Chairperson of the Council of
concerned entity or its Vice Chairperson
in case of absence of the Chairperson.
Their oath of office is as follows:

u “I, ………………………, solemnly swear
to the Nation that I will:
1º remain loyal to the Republic
Rwanda;
2º abide by the Constitution and other
laws;
3º safeguard human rights and
interests of the Rwandan people;
4º strive for national unity;
5º diligently fulfil the responsibilities
entrusted to me;
6º never use the powers conferred on
for personal interests.
Should I fail to honour this oath, may
subjected to the rigours of the law.
So help me God.”
The oath of office is received by
Primary Court of the area of operation
the Abunzi Committee.
Article 9:
Committee
Bureau
of
the
Abunzi
The Abunzi Committee at the Cell
Sector level is headed by a Bureau

composed of a Chairperson and a Vice
Chairperson elected by their peers.
The Bureau of the Abunzi Committee,
general, ensures coordination of activities
of the Abunzi Committee. It supervises
performance and conduct of members
the Abunzi Committee.
The Bureau gives to the Abunzi Committee
all instructions and necessary advice
the smooth performance of duties but
does not allow such a Bureau to instruct
Abunzi Panel on how to settle a dispute
referred to it.
The Bureau also prepares all
documents relating to the functioning
the Abunzi Committee.
CHAPTER III: COMPETENCE
THE ABUNZI COMMITTEE
Article 10: Subject-matter competence
of the Abunzi Committee in civil matters
The Abunzi Committee at the Cell level
has jurisdiction to decide any civil matter
relating to:

1º movable and immovable assets
and succession thereto where their
value does not exceed three million
Rwanda francs (Frw 3,000,000);
2º a breach of contract between
individuals, if its value does
exceed
three million Rwanda
francs (Frw 3,000,000);
3º family issues other than those
requiring rendering a decision
civil status.
Article
11:
Abunzi
Committee
competent to examine a dispute
For civil disputes provided for under
Article 10 of this Law, the competent
Abunzi Committee is determined with
regard to any of the following:
1º the Abunzi Committee in
territorial jurisdiction of
subject-matter;
2º the Abunzi Committee of
respondent’s place of residence
the subject-matter is movable

property or the Abunzi Committee
of the applicant’s place
residence
through
mutual
agreement with the respondent.
However, where the summoned person
no known identification or place
domicile or residence in Rwanda,
dispute is referred to the relevant court.
In no way, may disputes involving
State, its organs or associations
companies with legal personality whether
private or public be referred to an Abunzi
Committee.
Article
12:
Abunzi
Committee
competent to determine a dispute
involving land property located
different territorial jurisdictions
Subject to provisions of Articles 2 and
of this Law, when land property is located
in different jurisdictions of Abunzi
Committees, the competent Abunzi
Committee is determined in the following
way:
1° the Abunzi Committee having
jurisdiction over the place
location of the portion of land
in which both parties reside,
competent to settle the dispute;

2° when both parties do not reside
the same jurisdiction, the Abunzi
Committee having jurisdiction over
the place of location of the portion
of land and in which one of
parties resides is competent to settle
the dispute.
3° when both parties do not reside
the same jurisdiction where the
is located, Abunzi Committee
having jurisdiction over the place
location of the bigger portion of
land is competent to settle
dispute.
CHAPTER IV: FUNCTIONING
THE ABUNZI COMMITTEE
Section One: Consideration of
dispute referred to the Abunzi
Committee
Article 13: Referring a dispute to
Abunzi Committee
The Executive Secretary of the
receives disputes to be submitted to
Abunzi Committee at the Cell level.
Disputes are submitted verbally or
writing and are registered on the list
disputes of the Abunzi Committee.

On the day of receipt of a dispute,
Executive Secretary fills out forms which
contain a brief description of the dispute
and submits them to the Abunzi Committee
to allow it to summon the respondent
decide on the venue, the day and the time
of consideration of the dispute.
The Executive Secretary of the Sector
receives disputes to be submitted to
Abunzi Committee at the Sector level.
In case of absence of the Executive
Secretary or when Abunzi are involved
interested in the dispute referred to them,
the dispute is received by a person
deputizes for him/her in his/her daily
duties.
In no case, shall the Executive Secretary
the Cell or Sector take part in the hearing
panel.
Article 14: Summoning the respondent
The summons is delivered, with
acknowledgment of receipt, to the office
the Cell or Sector of the respondent’s place

of residence or to the secretariat of
institution where the respondent works.
The summons briefly describes the dispute,
the venue, date and time set for
respondent’s appearance before the Abunzi
Committee, with notification to
applicant.
The summoned person is given a period
at least seven (7) days to appear before
Abunzi Committee from the date
summons is served on him/her.
Article 15: Choosing Abunzi
On the day of appearance referred to
Article 14 of this Law, the parties agree
on three (3) Abunzi to whom they refer
their dispute.
Where parties fail to agree on the abunzi,
each party chooses one and the two
choose the third one. Where parties agree
on the same umwunzi, the latter chooses
two (2) others from Abunzi Committee
assist him/her. Parties have no right
refuse umwunzi or abunzi chosen
following this procedure.

The Abunzi Panel chooses from among
members a Chairperson and a rapporteur
who must be literate.
Article 16: The Panel to examine
dispute involving Abunzi Committee
the majority of its members
Umwunzi shall not sit on a panel examining
a dispute involving him/her or in which
he/she has an interest. Umwunzi
recuse him/herself from the panel
his/her own motion or in response
motion by the applicant.
When the dispute considered involves
or the majority of members of Abunzi
Committee at the Cell or Sector level,
thereby making it impossible to form
panel, the chairperson of Abunzi
Committee notifies the coordinator
activities of Abunzi Committee at
District level of the issue in writing within
fifteen (15) days of the day when
problem is noticed.
The Coordinator of activities of the Abunzi
Committee in collaboration with
Executive Secretary of the Cell or Sector
which the problem is noticed seeks support
from Abunzi of the nearest Cell or Sector
to determine the dispute.

Article 17: Conciliation procedure
The conciliation session is public, save
a session behind closed doors which
decided by Abunzi on their own initiative
or upon request, following the nature
the dispute.
Members of the Abunzi Committee that
not chosen to examine the dispute
participate in any conciliation session
without the right to vote.
During the conciliation, Abunzi hear from
each party and from witnesses when they
are presented by the parties. They may
seek advice from any other person as long
as he/she can provide clarification on
dispute.
While considering the dispute, Abunzi
both parties to reach a compromise.
Where parties fail to do so, Abunzi render
a decision in accordance with the
culture of the place where the dispute
being settled or their own conscience,
provided the decision is not contrary
written law.

Before the Panel of Abunzi, a lawyer
assist a party having sought his/her
assistance, but the lawyer cannot represent
or plead for him/her.
The applicant, the respondent or
witness may use an interpreter who is given
the floor before Abunzi Panel. Interpreter
fees are borne by the person having sought
his/her services.
Abunzi must settle the dispute within
month from the day the dispute
registered on the list of disputes of Abunzi
Committee.
Article 18: Decision rendered by default
If a summoned person fails to appear on
date indicated in the summons, he/she
summoned again and informed that Abunzi
render their decision on the scheduled
irrespective of whether he/she appears
not.
If the party summoned fails to appear again
on the date indicated, the applicant chooses
umwunzi and Abunzi Committee chooses
another and the two select a third one

decide on the dispute in the summoned
party’s absence.
However, if the summoned party fails
appear on reasonable grounds, Abunzi
reschedule the conciliation session
another date and notify him/her thereof
the manner provided for in Paragraph
of this Article.
2 Provisions of Paragraphs One and 2 of
Article also apply in the case of non-
appearance of the applicant.
Article 19: Right to intervene in
dispute under examination by Abunzi
Panel
Every person has the right to intervene
dispute under consideration by the Abunzi
Panel, where he/she is neither an applicant
nor a respondent, if the verdict in
dispute is likely to affect his/her interests.
He/she may intervene voluntarily or
forced to do so by Abunzi Panel. However,
forced intervention that could seek
obtain a conviction of the intervener cannot
be made for the first time in appeal.
The person intending to intervene in
dispute before Abunzi Committee shall
submit his/her request to the Executive

Secretary of the concerned entity, who
turn submits it to Abunzi Committee.
summoning procedure is made
accordance with the provisions of Article
14 of this Law.
If the intervener is not satisfied with
verdict of Abunzi Committee, he/she
the right to use remedies provided for
this Law.
Section 2: Rendering a verdict
Article 20: Deliberations and decision-
making
After the dispute consideration, Abunzi
withdraw themselves to make a decision.
The Abunzi’s decision is taken
consensus or, where there is no such
consensus, by an absolute majority
votes.
The decision is recorded in minutes signed
on each page by all members of Abunzi
Panel and the concerned parties right after
the procedure of conciliation.
The verdict must be written and signed
Abunzi on every page and available within
a period not exceeding ten (10) days from

the day on which the decision was made;
otherwise the concerned Abunzi may
disciplinary sanctions relating to Abunzi
profession as provided for by the order
the Minister in charge of justice.
Article 21: Content of the verdict
Abunzi Committee
The verdict of Abunzi Committee indicates
the following:
1º the parties’ identification;
2º the summary of the dispute;
3º the arguments put forward by
involved parties;
4º the decision with which all parties
agree;
5º the decision to which one of
parties does not agree, if any;
6º the date and place of the dispute
settlement;
7º signatures or fingerprints of parties
conflict;

8º Abunzi names as well as their
signatures or their fingerprints;
9º the rapporteur’s names as well
his/her signature or fingerprint.
The verdict of Abunzi Committee is sealed
with the seal of the Abunzi Committee
is kept by the Executive Secretary
submits it to the concerned parties.
Where one of Abunzi holds a dissenting
opinion, the issue and the reason thereof
are stated in the verdict.
The Chairperson of Abunzi Panel
examined the dispute notifies the parties
of the written verdict of the Abunzi
Committee, within five (5) days from
day on which the verdict was available.
Article 22: Force of Abunzi verdict
The Abunzi verdict with which all parties
agree serves as an agreement between
those parties, but shall not be enforceable
against third parties.

Article 23: Request for clarification
correction of the verdict of the Abunzi
Committee
A request for clarification or correction
the Abunzi verdict is submitted to
Executive Secretary of the concerned
entity, who submits it to the Abunzi
Committee.
The request is considered by the Abunzi
Panel that rendered the verdict. In case
not available, the Abunzi Committee
chooses among its members a panel
examine this request in accordance with
Article 15 of this Law.
The request is considered without
summoning of parties in conflict.
verdict complements the one for which
correction or clarification was required
is not subject to appeal.
Section 3: Appeal procedures
Article 24: Opposition against
verdict rendered by Abunzi Committee
Abunzi Committee’s verdict rendered
the absence of the respondent or
applicant may be subject to opposition

within ten (10) days from the day
notification of the Abunzi verdict to
party that applied for opposition.
The request for opposition against
Abunzi Committee’s verdict is submitted
a written or oral statement to the Executive
Secretary of the entity where the dispute
The entire Abunzi Panel which rendered
the verdict examines the dispute. In case
absence of such a panel, the dispute
examined by other members of the same
Abunzi Committee who are selected
accordance with provisions of Article 15
this Law.
Abunzi Committee’s verdict rendered
the absence of the respondent or
applicant shall not be appealed against
before the expiry of the period
opposition.
Article 25: Appealing against a verdict
rendered by Abunzi Committee at
Cell level
Any party that is not satisfied with
verdict of Abunzi Committee at the
level, may, within thirty (30) days from
day on which he/she was notified of

written verdict, appeal to
Committee at the Sector level.
Abunzi
At the appeal level, Abunzi Committee
the Sector level examines only points
the verdict rendered at Cell level
challenged by the applicant and to which
he/she never accepted before Abunzi
Committee at the Cell level.
Appealing against the verdict of Abunzi
Committee rendered at the Cell level shall
not be subject to payment of court fees.
Article 26: Opposition by a third party
against Abunzi Committee’s verdict
Any person who is not a party to
dispute examined by Abunzi Committee
but has an interest in it, may file a third
party opposition against the verdict which
is prejudicial to his/ her interests if neither
him/her nor the person he/she represents
were invited to attend conciliation session.
Notice of third party opposition is filed,
with supporting evidence, within ten
days from the day when the applicant
notified of Abunzi Committee’s verdict.

such period expires, the third party
opposition against a verdict rendered
Abunzi Committee is inadmissible.
Notice of third party opposition against
verdict is filed in a written or oral statement
to the Executive Secretary of the entity
where the dispute is.
The entire Abunzi Committee Panel
rendered the verdict examines the third
party opposition. In case of absence
members of the panel, the third party
opposition is examined by other members
of the same Abunzi Committee selected
accordance with provisions of Article 15
this Law.
If the third party opposition against Abunzi
Committee’s verdict is admissible,
dispute is reconsidered.
Section 4: Filing a case with the court
Article 27: Filing a case with
Primary Court
Any party that is not satisfied with
verdict of Abunzi Committee at
Sector level may, within thirty (30) days
from the day on which he/she was notified

of the written verdict of the Abunzi
Committee, refer the matter to
competent Primary Court.
Filing a case with the court is subject
payment of court fees.
Article 28: Points to be examined by
court referred to
The points agreed upon by the parties
before the Abunzi Committee shall not
included in the case filed with the Primary
Court.
The applicant must attach the copy of
verdict of the Abunzi Committee to his/her
application lodged with the Primary Court.
Failure to do so, the Court Registrar does
not receive the application.
The court to which the case is referred
considers only those points of the verdict
challenged by the applicant and to which
he/she raised objections before the Abunzi
Committee. The court may request a copy
of the minutes drafted by Abunzi,
considered necessary.

Section 5: Execution
Committee’s verdict
of
Abunzi
Article 29: Execution
Committee’s verdict
of
Abunzi
The verdict of Abunzi is executed with
consent of parties to the dispute.
However, if one of the parties refuses
comply with the Abunzi Committee’s
verdict, the prejudiced party may apply
enforcement of the decision in accordance
with laws relating to enforcement
proceedings. In that case, he/she requests,
either in writing or orally, the President
the Primary Court with jurisdiction over
the place where the verdict was rendered
append to the verdict an enforcement
formula.
Application for appending an enforcement
formula to a verdict of Abunzi Committee
is done without payment of court fees.
The President of the Primary Court
appends to the verdict an order for
enforcement after receiving a written
statement from the Executive Secretary
the Sector where the verdict was rendered
certifying that the verdict was rendered
the Abunzi Committee and that it is

longer subject to appeal or to be referred
any court.
The President of the Primary Court cannot
refuse to append to the verdict of
Abunzi Committee an enforcement formula
unless the verdict or its execution
contrary to public order. In that case,
he/she informs the Abunzi Committee
his/her decision in writing in order for
Abunzi Panel who rendered the verdict
correct it in relation to violated rules
public order.
Article 30: Dispute arising from
execution of Abunzi Committee verdict
A dispute arising from the execution of
Abunzi Committee’s verdict is submitted
the Abunzi Committee that in turn, submits
it to the Abunzi Panel that rendered
verdict in the last instance for more
clarification on that verdict in order
facilitate its execution. In case of absence
of Abunzi who composed the panel which
examined the incident arising from
execution of the verdict is submitted
Abunzi Committee and examined
another panel selected through procedures
specified in Articles 15 and 16 of this Law.

Section 6: Abunzi Committee code
conduct
Article 31: Abunzi code of conduct
The Bureau of Abunzi Committee provided
for in Article 9 of this Law ensures
conduct of Abunzi.
An Order of the Minister in charge
Justice determines the code of conduct
for Abunzi.
u Article 32: Suspension of a member
Abunzi Committee
At its own initiative or upon a request
the population, the Abunzi Committee
suspend one of its members for a period
not exceeding one (1) month on the ground
of partiality or any other misconduct.
decision is taken by at least two thirds (2/3)
of Abunzi Committee members, after
hearing the concerned umwunzi.
A member of the Abunzi Committee
be relieved from office by the electoral
college if it is evident that he/she is
longer able to fulfil his/her duties.

An Order of the Minister in charge
justice determines modalities for relieving
from office a member of the Abunzi
Committee.
CHAPTER V: MISCELLANEOUS,
TRANSITIONAL
AND
FINAL
PROVISIONS
Section One: Miscellaneous provisions
Article 33:
supervising
Committees
Organ in charge
activities
of
Abunzi
The Ministry in charge of justice
supervises
activities
of
Abunzi
Committees. It supports them in
performance of their duties through
trainings and supply of equipment. It
collaborates with the Ministry in charge
local government in order to monitor
activities of Abunzi Committees.
Article 34: Termination of the term
office of incumbent Abunzi
Subject to Articles 2 and 6 of this Law,
members of Abunzi Committee that
incumbent on the day this Law comes
force, keep their functions until their term
of office comes to an end.

Article 35: Computation of time limits
provided for under this Law
The time limits set in days, are computed
day by day up to the last day of the period.
However, the day the event took place shall
not be counted but rather the last day
event is counted.
In case of coincidence of the last day of
event with an official public holiday or
day of the weekend, it is extended to
next working day.
Section 2: Transitional provisions
Article 36: Civil disputes pending before
Abunzi Committee
Civil matters pending before Abunzi
Committee at the Cell level or Sector level,
which are not under their jurisdiction
accordance with this Law and whose final
verdict is yet to be rendered on the date
entry into force of this Law, are referred
a competent court.

Article 37: Cases filed to Abunzi
Committee
Criminal cases pending before Abunzi
Committee at the Cell level or Sector level
and whose final verdict is not yet rendered
on the date of entry into force of this Law,
are referred to the competent Judicial
Police organs.
Article 38: Validity of actions taken
nº
The verdict that Abunzi Committee
rendered in accordance with Organic Law
nº 02/2010/OL of 09/06/2010 on
organisation, jurisdiction, competence
functioning of the Mediation Committee
amended and complemented to date,
which became final remains valid.
Article 39: Procedure and period of
handover of files
The Chairperson of Abunzi Committee
the Cell and Sector levels submits to
Executive Secretary of the Sector
report of disputes and cases provided for
Articles 36 and 37 of this Law.

The Executive Secretary submits disputes
and cases to the following organs:
1º criminal actions are submitted to
judicial police organs ;
2º civil actions are brought before
Primary Court except for disputes
related to labour contract that
brought before a competent labour
inspectorate.
Provisions of Paragraphs One and 2 of
Article must be implemented within three
(3) months from the publication of
Law in the Official Gazette of the Republic
of Rwanda.
Section 3: Final provisions
Article 40: Repealing provision
All prior legal provisions contrary to
Law are hereby repealed.
Article 41: Drafting, consideration
adoption of this Law
This Law was drafted, considered
adopted in Kinyarwanda.

Article 42: Commencement
This Law comes into force on the date
its publication in the Official Gazette of
Republic of Rwanda.
