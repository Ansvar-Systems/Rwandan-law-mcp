GENERAL
Article One: Purpose of this Law
The purpose of this Law is to conserve,
manage, protect and promote biological
diversity.
Article 2: Scope of this Law
This Law governs:
1° conservation,
management,
protection and promotion of
biological diversity;
2° conservation,
management,
protection and promotion of
wildlife, their parts and derivatives;
3° trade in specimens of species.

Article 3: Definitions
For the purposes of this Law, the following
terms have the following meanings:
1° captivity: a place where animals
are confined out of their habitats;
2° protected area: clearly defined
geographical space, dedicated and
managed
for
long
term
conservation of nature with
associated ecosystem services and
cultural values;
3° animal capture: act aiming at
capturing a live wildlife animal or
taking a nest, egg or young animal;
4° transhipment:
transfer
of
specimens by various means of
transport in couple or moored by
intermediary inland-depot;

5° polluting: pollution and serious
natural environment depletion or
depletion of property or airspace,
with diverse negative impact on
biological diversity;
6° poaching: illegal hunting, fishing,
capturing or killing an animal;
7° introduction of a specimen from
the sea: introduction of a specimen
directly
from
the
marine
environment under no state
jurisdiction, including the airspace
above it, the sea-bed and subsoil
beneath the sea;
8° import of a specimen of species:
bring specimen of species into the
country other than through transit
or transhipment;
9° strict nature reserve: land or
marine protected area, set aside,
comprised
of
ecosystems,
geological or geomorphological

features, unique or representative
species, where human visitation,
use and impacts are strictly
controlled and limited to ensure
protection of the conservation
values or because it may serve as
indispensable reference area for
scientific research and monitoring;
10° bred in captivity: offspring or egg,
new born either born or laid by
parents that mated in a controlled
environment or whose gametes
were transmitted in a controlled
environment;
11° feral animal: an animal which has
escaped from a domestic or captive
status, to the wild and may
contribute to extinction of
indigenous species;
12° trophy: dead wild animal, bone,
horn, ivory, tooth, tusk, claw, hoof,
hair, hide, skin, feather, egg, shell
or another durable portion or
derivative of an animal;

13° derivative: any part, tissue or
extract of an animal, plant or other
organism, whether fresh, preserved
or processed and any chemical
compound derived from such part,
tissue or extract;
14° Authority: authority or organ in
charge of biological diversity and
wildlife
conservation
and
management;
15° plant: any plant growing naturally,
cultivated or bred;
16° artificially propagated plant:
plant grown under controlled
conditions from seeds, cuttings,
divisions, callus tissues or other
plant tissues, spores or other
propagules that either are exempt
or have been derived from
cultivated parental stock;

17° species: group of living organisms
capable of interbreeding and
producing
fertile
offspring
including any species, subspecies,
or
geographically
separate
population;
18° endangered species: indigenous
species that are at risk of extinction;
19° critically endangered species:
indigenous species facing an
extremely high risk of extinction;
20° invasive species: species which
multiply or spread causing damage
to environment;
21° alien species: non-indigenous
species trans-located to a place out
of its habitat, intentionally or
accidentally;
22° wildlife: wild and indigenous
animal, plant, their parts or

derivatives within their constituent
habitat or ecosystem on land and in
water and species introduced into
the country in accordance with
relevant laws;
23° vulnerable
species:
any
indigenous species likely to
become endangered;
24° specimen:
a. any animal or any other living
organisms or plant, whether
alive or dead;
b. any part or derivative of an
animal, living organism or
plant species;
25° aircraft: any type of aeroplane,
airship, balloon, drone, whether
free, looped, controlled or directed
by human agency;

26° habitat: ecological place or site
where a particular species or
organism naturally lives;
27° ecosystem: particular place where
biological diversity is found,
comprising a dynamic complex;
28° vermin: animal that is destructive
to a community or injurious to
health which, in due regard to its
proliferation status, is declared as
such;
29° animal:
any vertebrate
or
invertebrate, or its young one or
egg;
30° problem animal: any animal that
poses threat to human life or
property out of protected areas;
31° export of a specimen: taking any
specimen out of the country;

32° transit: transport of specimens
through a country toward another
country during which
any
interruption arises only from the
arrangements necessitated by the
form of traffic;
33° trespass: entering or camping in
protected area without permission,
entering a protected area through
unauthorized entry point or
overstaying permission;
34° degrading: throwing, dumping or
pouring toxic waste likely to cause
destruction to the ecosystem;
35° culling: selective killing of animals
based on ecological and scientific
principles
for
management
purposes;
36° Minister: Minister in charge of
biological diversity;

37° national park: natural, semi-
natural or developed areas, fresh or
sea waters protected by the State to
conserve large-scale ecological
processes,
along
with
the
complement of species and
ecosystems;
38° trade: exportation, re-exportation,
importation and introduction of
specimens of species;
39° wildlife user rights: various rights
granted to a stakeholder, to have
access to direct and indirect use of
wildlife resources;
40° low altitude: minimum altitude of
zero point six (0.6) kilometres,
above a protected area;

41° game
ranching:
extensive
breeding of wildlife species for
wildlife conservation, trade and
recreation purposes;
42° authorised officer: officer in
charge
of
protection
and
management of wildlife, park
ranger, protected area guide,
investigation officer or any other
person so designated by the Law;
43° person: a natural person,
company, a cooperative, a national
or international non-governmental
organisation,
a
faith-based
organisation or any other entity
with legal personality;
44° community: group of individuals
who share a heritage, an interest or
stake in land, land-based resources
or such other related benefits;

45° drug: pharmaceutical or traditional
medicine;
46° permit: official document granting
right to trade or use species or
specimens of species;
47° biological diversity: variety of
living organisms of all species on
land, underground, in water as well
as in the atmosphere and their
interactions;

Article 5: Establishment

A Ministerial Order establishes

Article 6: Establishment of a protected

A protected area is established
accordance with relevant laws.

Article 7: National park and strict
nature reserve
A national park and a strict nature reserve
are controlled by the State and their
boundaries cannot be altered or any portion
alienated, except in accordance with
relevant laws.
Article 8: Land use in a protected area
Protected area land use permit holder uses
it in conformity with the provisions of
relevant laws.
Article 9: Activities prohibited in
protected area
The following activities are prohibited in
protected area, except when authorized:
1° mining and quarrying;
2° farming;
3° excavation or prospecting, drilling,
or construction;

4° carrying out scientific research;
5° camping and flying an aircraft over
at low altitude;
6° any other activity that may be
found destructive to a protected
area.

Article 10: Organs in charge
management of biological diversity
of
The organs in charge of biological
diversity management are the following:
1. The Authority;
2. Science Advisory committee for
biological diversity;
3. any other organ as may be
determined by relevant laws;

Article 11: Responsibilities of the
Authority for biological diversity
conservation
Without prejudice to the provisions of
other laws, the Authority has the following
responsibilities:
1° to manage and control biological
diversity and wildlife resources;
2° to implement biological diversity
and wildlife policies;
3° to regulate and control trade in
wildlife;
4° to coordinate implementation of
treaties and agreements on
biological diversity and wildlife;
5° to issue permits and certificates for
authorised activities;

6° to ensure security in protected areas
and ecosystems;
7° to collect revenue generated from
biological diversity and wildlife
resources;
8° to develop mechanisms for
community
involvement
in
conservation and benefit sharing
from biological diversity and
wildlife resources;
9° to advise the Government on
matters pertaining to biological
diversity and wildlife policy,
strategy and legislation;
10° to designate one or more rescue
centres for seized and confiscated
living specimens;
11° to impose administrative fines;

Article 12: Science Advisory Committee
for biological diversity
This Law establishes the Science Advisory
Committee for biological diversity.
An Order of the Minister determines the
composition,
responsibilities
and
functioning of the Committee.
CHAPTER IV: PROTECTION AND
CONSERVATION OF BIOLOGICAL
DIVERSITY
Section
one:
Protection
and
conservation of biological diversity and
its habitats
Article 13: Protection and conservation
of biological diversity
Every person has the duty to defend,
protect, conserve and promote the
biological diversity.

Article 14: Introduction of alien species
into the country
Introducing an alien species into the
country is prohibited.
However, the Authority may issue a permit
for introduction of alien species into the
country for purposes of research,
conservation or for such other reasons
upon prior impact assessment on biological
diversity.
An Order of the Minister determines the
requirements of issuance of the permit
referred in Paragraph 2 of this Article.
Article 15: List of invasive species
A Ministerial Order establishes the list of
invasive species. This list is reviewed
every five (5) years and whenever
necessary.

Article 16: Activities involving listed
invasive species
Any activity involving listed invasive
species or their specimens is prohibited,
unless it is authorised.
The permit referred to in Paragraph One of
this Article is only issued upon wildlife
impact assessment of the intended activity.
Article 17: Obligation
invasive species
to
combat
A person holding the permit provided for
in Article 16 of this Law complies with
requirements provided in the permit.
The owner of land on which an invasive
species is found, informs the Authority in
writing or by any other possible means
immediately.
The Authority puts in place measures to
contain, eradicate and prevent invasive
species from spreading while minimizing
possible harm to biological diversity.

Article 18: Modalities for controlling,
containing or eradicating invasive
species
Controlling, containing or eradicating an
invasive species is carried out by means
appropriate to the species concerned and
the environment on which it is found.
The methods employed to control, contain
or eradicate invasive species must prevent
their future propagation.
All actions taken to control, contain or
eradicate invasive species is executed with
caution.
Article 19: Invasive species intervention
plan
A person with a permit referred to in article
16 of this Law prepares an intervention
plan to control, prevent or eradicate

invasive species and incorporates
strategic, monitoring and evaluation plans.
Article 20: Content of invasive species
intervention plan
The invasive species intervention plan
includes:
1° a detailed list of invasive species
found on the concerned area and
description of each one of them;
2° a description of parts of the area
infested with invasive species;
3° the extent of damage caused by the
invasive species;
4° methods used to control, contain or
eradicate invasive species;
5° indicators used to measure the
progress of the intervention.

The Authority produces on an annual basis
and whenever necessary a report on
control, containing or eradication of
invasive species.
Article 21: Ecosystems in need of
protection
An Order of the Minister establishes a list
of ecosystems that are threatened into the
country and in need of protection and their
location. It also determines activities
prohibited in the ecosystems.
Article 22: Permit to access genetic
resources or associated traditional
knowledge
A person who intends to have access to any
genetic resource or associated traditional
knowledge applies for it.
A permit to access genetic resources or
traditional knowledge is exclusive and
cannot be assigned or transferred to third
party.

Article 23: Categories of permits to
access genetic resources or associated
traditional knowledge
Permits to access genetic resources or
associated traditional knowledge are
classified in the following categories:
1° permit issued
purpose;
commercial
2° permit issued for non-commercial
purpose.
An Order of the Minister determines
requirements and modalities to apply for
access to genetic resources or associated
traditional knowledge.
Article 24: Publication of list of permits
to access to genetic resources
The Authority in charge of biological
diversity, in consultation with concerned
organs, publishes an annual list of permits
for accessing genetic resources and
associated traditional knowledge.
for

Article 25: Prior informed consent and
mutually agreed terms
Any access to genetic resources or
associated traditional knowledge are
carried out with prior informed consent of
the provider under negotiated mutually
agreed terms between the provider and the
user.
A ministerial order determines the
modalities and requirements for obtaining
prior informed consent, negotiation and
conclusion of mutually agreed terms.
Article 26: Benefit sharing principle
A fair and equitable sharing of benefits
arising from the utilization of genetic
resources and associated traditional
knowledge is subject to mutually agreed
terms signed between the user and
provider.
A Ministerial Order determines the
modalities for benefit sharing arising from

utilization of genetic resources
associated traditional knowledge.
and
Article 27: Import or export of genetic
resources
Import or export of any genetic resource is
subject to a permit.
A Ministerial Order determines modalities
and requirements for issuance of the permit
referred to in this Article.
Article 28: Gene bank
A Prime Minister’s Order establishes
gene bank for preserving and conserving
the genetic content of fauna and flora and
determines modalities for its management
and use.

Section 2 : Conservation of wildlife and
their habitats
Article 29: Carrying out research in
protected area
Research in a protected area may be carried
out by the Authority or any other
authorized person.
The Authority provides guidelines for
conducting research in a protected area.
Article 30: Possession, introduction into
the country or application of drugs on
wild animals or plants
Possession, introduction into the country or
application of drugs on wild animal or
plant requires a permit from the Authority.
Article 31: Reporting a trophy or
specimen of wild plant species
Any person who finds a trophy or
specimen of protected wild plant species
listed on Annex I, Annex II and Annex III

of this Law reports and hands it over to the
authorised officer or to the nearest
administrative authority.
Article 32: Conservation of wildlife
species and their habitats
For the purpose of conservation of wildlife
species and their habitats, the following
acts are prohibited:
1° taking or destroying an egg or
nest of any wild animal;
2° capturing, stressing or removing
animal species from their habitat,
harming
them,
transporting,
hawking, utilising, possessing,
selling or purchasing them;
3° destroying, cutting, mutilating,
collecting or removing any wild
plant species, picking fruits,
transporting, hawking, utilising,
possessing, selling and purchasing,
or seedlings or of wild plant
species;

4° destroying, altering or degrading
natural habitats of wild animal and
plant species;
5° erecting infrastructure in protected
areas.
However, the acts cited in items 2, 3, 4 and
5 may be carried out upon a permit of the
Authority.
CHAPTER
V:
COEXISTENCE
BETWEEN HUMAN AND WILDLIFE
Article 33: Mutual co-existence
The Authority issues guidelines for mutual
coexistence between wildlife and their
surrounding or adjacent communities.
Article 34: Collaboration between the
Authority and stakeholders
The Authority, in collaboration with
stakeholders, promotes community-based
conservation of wildlife. It may also
support stakeholders in conservation of
wildlife.

Article 35: Wounding or killing an
animal
Wounding or killing an animal is
prohibited, unless it is authorized. An
animal may also be killed in case of self-
defence or defence of another person. An
authorised officer may equally kill an
animal by humane dispatch where the
animal is found to be in severe pain.
The burden of proof of wounding or killing
an animal in self-defence, defence of
another person or in the process of humane
dispatch lies with the person who wounds
or kills it.
A person who kills or wounds an animal as
mentioned in Paragraph One of this Article
reports the incident to the nearest wildlife
office or Police station, in a period not
exceeding twenty-four (24) hours.

Article 36: Management of a feral
animal or a problem animal
A person who encounters a feral animal or
a problem animal informs the nearest
authority for intervention.
The Authority may permit to eliminate or
translocate a feral or problem animal after
conformity and impact assessment.
Where the authorized person mentioned in
Paragraph 2 of this Article kills a feral or
problem animal, he or she reports the
incident to the nearest wildlife office or
Police station, in a period not exceeding
twenty-four (24) hours.

Article 37: Compensation for damages
caused by wildlife
Compensation for damages caused by
wildlife is governed by relevant laws.
Article 38: Management of vermin
The Authority is responsible for
establishing a list of vermin and how to
manage them.
Where listed vermin are of value, the
Authority advises the local community on
its appropriate management.
CHAPTER
VI:
RIGHT
TO
OWNERSHIP OF WILDLIFE AND
OTHER RELATED RIGHTS
Article 39: Ownership right on wildlife
Ownership right on wildlife is vested in the
State.

However, an individual may be granted
right of use, in accordance with provisions
of this Law.
A Ministerial Order determines modalities
of granting ownership right on wildlife.
Article 40: State trophies
The following are State trophies:
1° a trophy with no owner;
2° a dead animal, its parts or
derivatives;
3° an animal killed in self-defence;
4° an animal killed by an authorised
officer.
Provisions of Paragraph One of this Article
do not apply where a trophy is kept by
person for historical purposes as may be
authorised by the Authority.

A Ministerial Order establishes modalities
for the management of trophies.
Article 41: Trade in specimens of species
Trading,
utilising,
possessing
or
transferring specimens of species requires
a permit.
In case of species in Annexes I and II of
this Law, the permit referred to under
Paragraph One of this Article is granted
where:
1° specimen of species is not harmful
to life of species;
2° specimen of species is possessed or
acquired lawfully;
3° specimen of species is expected to
be processed and transported in
manner that does not expose it to
death,
wound,
harm
or
mistreatment;

4° introduction into the country of
specimen
of
species
was
authorised.
A Ministerial Order determines the ambit
and duration of the permit referred to in
this Article.
Article 42: Wildlife user rights
The Authority may issue or withdraw
wildlife use permit on the following
activities:
1° game ranching;
2° live capture;
3° off-take of an animal for research;
4° cropping;
5° culling;
6° sport hunting;

7° wildlife-based tourism;
8° commercial
filming;
9° education and research;
10° cultural, socio-economic
religious related activities.
photography
and
and
The Authority issues guidelines on
carrying out activities referred to in
Paragraph One of this Article.
Article 43: Transfer of wildlife user
rights
With approval from the Authority, the
holder of a wildlife user permit may
transfer any or all of his or her user rights
granted to him or her to a qualified agent.
The Authority may decline an application
for assignment of a permit if such
assignment deviates from the main
purposes set out in the permit.

The transferee of the rights under the
permit is responsible for acts and
omissions he or she commits.
Article 44: Revocation of wildlife user
rights
The Authority may revoke wildlife user
rights granted to the beneficiary if:
1° the permit holder is in breach of the
agreed terms;
2° revocation of rights is necessary for
purposes of protecting and conserving
wildlife;
3° the holder so requests.
Where the Authority decides to withdraw
the wildlife user rights, it serves a notice of
thirty (30) days, stating the reasons of
revocation of the user rights.

Article 45: Appeal and seizing the court
Where the permit holder whose wildlife
user right has been revoked believes that
the decision was not appropriate, he or she
appeals to the Authority within fifteen (15)
days from reception of the revocation
notice.
The Authority notifies the permit holder
whose wildlife user right has been revoked
of its decision within fifteen (15) days from
the date of reception of the appeal.
Where the permit holder whose wildlife
user right has been revoked is aggrieved by
the decision of the Authority, he or she
may, within thirty (30) days, from the date
of notification, seize a competent court.
Article 46: Control of commercial
activities
During the control of activities related to
trading in derivatives of specimen of
species, the Authority may seize or
confiscate specimens of species illegally
possessed.

For offences provided for in this Law, the
authorised officer may seize specimens of
species or objects used to commit an
offence.
The court may order that the cost of
disposing of any specimens of species or
objects used in the commission of an
offence be borne by the person convicted.
The court may also order that any permit or
such other authorisation given in
accordance with the provisions of this
Law, and to which the offence relates, be
cancelled.
Where a person is convicted of an offence
under this Law, or when a specimen is
seized without identification of its owner,
any specimen and equipment by means of
which the offence was committed, are
seized by the State.
The authorized Officer, without delay
notifies the Authority of the seized or

confiscated specimens and provides all
relevant information, documents and data
on the case.
A Ministerial Order establishes modalities
of seizure and management of confiscated
specimens of species.
Article 47: Transportation of specimens
Transportation of specimen of species
listed in Annexes I, II and III of this Law
within country requires a permit.
A Ministerial Order determines the
requirements for transportation of
specimens of species listed in Annexes I, II
and III of this Law.
Article 48: Specimens born and bred in
captivity or artificially propagated
A person who wishes for profit, to breed
animals in captivity and artificially
propagated plants of any species listed in
Annex I of this Law must register with the

Authority. The Authority issues a permit
for breeding animals in captivity or to
artificially propagate plants listed in Annex
I of this Law.
Specimens of animal species listed in
Annex I of this Law born and bred in
captivity or of a plant species listed in
Annex I of this Law, artificially propagated
are treated as specimens of species listed in
Annex II of this Law.
Article 49: Transit or transhipment
Transit or transhipment of specimen of
species listed in Annexes I, II and III of this
Law requires a permit.
The Authority puts in place instructions
determining the ambit, tenure and
requirements for granting the permit.

CHAPTER VII: ADMINISTRATIVE
FAULTS AND SANCTIONS
Article 50: Violation of access to genetic
resources and associated traditional
knowledge requirements
A natural person who:
1° conducts studies on genetic
resources or associated traditional
knowledge, accesses or export
them without a relevant permit;
2° violates the terms of the permit;
3° provides false information in an
application for a permit or in
concluding agreements pertaining
to access to genetic resources or
associated traditional knowledge;
commits a fault.

He or she is liable to an administrative fine
of not less than two million Rwandan
francs (FRW 2,000,000) and not more than
five million Rwandan francs (FRW
5,000,000).
Apart from sanctions provided in
Paragraph One of this Article, the
Authority:
1° seizes any genetic resources on
which the fault was committed as
well as the equipment used to
commit it;
2° nullifies the issued permit.
Article 51: Degrading a protected area
A natural person who throws, discharges or
pours waste in a protected area in a way to
cause damage, commits a fault. He or she
is liable to an administrative fine of not less
than three hundred thousand Rwandan
francs (FRW 300,000) and not more than
five hundred thousand Rwandan francs
(FRW 500,000).

Without prejudice to the provisions of
Paragraphs One of this Article, the
Authority may direct the wrongdoer to
contribute to wildlife related activity as
compensation, restoration or restitution
depending on the extent of the damage.
Article 52: Causing to enter or grazing
domestic animal in a protected area
A natural person who causes to enter or
grazes a domestic animal in a protected
area without authorization commits a fault.
He or she is liable to a fine of not less than
one hundred thousand Rwandan francs
(FRW 100,000) but not more than three
million Rwandan francs (FRW 3,000,000).
Article 53: Trespass in a protected area
A natural person who trespasses in
protected area commits a fault. He or she is
liable to an administrative fine of not less
than one hundred thousand Rwandan
francs (100,000 FRW) and not more than
three hundred thousand Rwandan francs
(300,000 FRW).

Article 54: Payment of administrative
fine
A Ministerial Order in charge of finance
determines the modalities for payment and
deposit of the administrative fines
provided for in this Law.
Article 55: Administrative faults and
sanctions against an entity with legal
personality
Without prejudice to other legal
provisions, the fine provided in this Law is
multiplied by five (5) in case the fault
referred to under Articles 50, 51, 52 and 53
of this law, is committed by a company,
cooperative organisation, a national non-

non-governmental

A natural person who poaches, injures,

Article 84: Induction training for an
authorised officer and power to use
arms
Modalities for acquiring, possessing,
carrying, keeping and receiving an arm by
an authorised officer, are determined by
relevant Laws.

officer undergoes training relating to
wildlife security and use of arms.

organisation of training relating to wildlife
security and use of arms.

firearm, in accordance with relevant laws.
