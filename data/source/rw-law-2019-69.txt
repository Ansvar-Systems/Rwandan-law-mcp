ADOPTS:
CHAPTER
PROVISIONS
ONE:
GENERAL
Article One: Purpose of this Law
This Law governs the Akagera National Park.
It also determines its boundaries, its surface
area and its buffer zone.
Article 2: Definitions
In this Law, the following terms have the
following meanings:
1° buffer zone: an area situated between
the park boundaries and the land
reserved for other activities, equal to
one hundred metres (100m) from the
boundaries of the western part of the
park, that is created with the purpose
of protecting the park;
2° park: a natural vegetation area
reserved for environment and
biodiversity protection, tourism,
research and other related activities;

3° competent authority: the organ in
charge of national parks.
CHAPTER II: AKAGERA NATIONAL
PARK AND BUFFER ZONE
Article 3: Location and surface area of the
Akagera National Park
The Akagera National Park is located in
Kayonza, Gatsibo and Nyagatare Districts, in
the Eastern Province.
The Akagera National Park has surface area
of one hundred eight thousand three hundred
sixty-three and seven tenths hectares
(108,363.7 ha).
Article 4: Buffer zone
The buffer zone has a total surface area of one
thousand one hundred fifty-three and three
tenths hectares (1,153.3 ha).

Article 5: Boundaries of the Akagera
National Park and those of the buffer zone
Boundaries of the Akagera National Park are
described in Annex I of this Law.
Boundaries of the buffer zone are described in
Annex II of this Law.
Article 6: Map of the Akagera National
Park
The map of the Akagera National Park and its
buffer zone is in Annex III of this Law.
Article 7: Management of the Akagera
National Park and the buffer zone
The Akagera National Park is managed by the
competent authority.
Without prejudice to the provisions of
Paragraph One of this Article, the buffer zone
of the Akagera National Park is managed in
accordance with laws governing forests, land
and environment.

CHAPTER III: FINAL PROVISIONS
Article 8: Drafting, consideration and
adoption of this Law
This Law was drafted, considered and
adopted in Ikinyarwanda.
Article 9: Saving and repealing provision
Except Article One of Law nº 33/2010 of
24/09/2010 establishing the Akagera National
Park with respect to its provision relating to
the establishment of the Akagera National
Park, other provisions of that Law and all
prior provisions of other Laws contrary to this
Law are repealed.
Article 10: Commencement
This Law comes into force on the date of its
publication in the Official Gazette of the
Republic of Rwanda.

THE PARLIAMENT:
The Chamber of Deputies, in its session of
September 2019;
Pursuant to the Constitution of the Republic
Rwanda of 2003 revised in 2015, especially
Articles 29, 64, 69, 70, 88, 90, 91, 106, 120 and

Having reviewed Law nº 68/2018
30/08/2018 determining offences and penalties
in general;

ADOPTS:
Article one: Reduction of penalties in case
mitigating circumstances approved by
judge
Article 60 of Law nº 68/2018 of 30/08/2018
determining offences and penalties in general
amended as follows:
“Article 60: Reduction of penalties in case
mitigating circumstances approved by
judge
If there are mitigating circumstances, penalties
may be reduced as follows:
1 º
subject to the provisions of Articles
and 133 of this Law, life imprisonment
may be reduced but it cannot be less
than twenty-five (25) years;
2 º
a fixed-term imprisonment or a fine may
be reduced but it cannot be less than the
minimum sentence provided for the
offence committed.”

Article 2: Definition of amnesty
Article 68 of Law nº 68/2018 of 30/08/2018
determining offences and penalties in general
amended as follows:
“Amnesty is a pardon granted in the general
interest and for offences that were committed
a given time.”
Article 3: Punishment of the crime
genocide
Article 92 of Law nº 68/2018 of 30/08/2018
determining offences and penalties in general
amended as follows:
“Any person who commits any of the acts
referred to under Article 91 of this Law
commits an offence. Upon conviction, he or she
is liable to the penalty of life imprisonment that
cannot be mitigated by any circumstances.
However, if the convict was a child at the time
of commission of the offence, he or she
punished in accordance with the provisions
Article 54 of this Law.”

Article 4: Child defilement
Article 133 of Law nº 68/2018 of 30/08/2018
determining offences and penalties in general
amended as follows:
“Any person who commits any of the sex
related acts listed below on a child, commits
offence:
1 º
insertion of a sexual organ into the
sexual organ, anus or mouth of the
child;
2 º
insertion of any organ of the human
body into a sexual organ or anus of
child;
3 º
performing any other act on the body
a child for the purpose of bodily
pleasure.
Upon conviction, he or she is liable
imprisonment for a term of not less than twenty
(20) years and not more than twenty-five (25)
years.
If an adult person commits child defilement
a child under fourteen (14) years, the penalty
life imprisonment that cannot be mitigated
any circumstances.

If an adult person commits child defilement
a child of fourteen (14) years of age or older has
resulted into an incurable illness or disability,
the penalty is life imprisonment.
If an adult person commits child defilement
which is followed by cohabitation as husband
and wife, the penalty is life imprisonment that
cannot be mitigated by any circumstances.
If a child aged fourteen (14) years but who
not yet eighteen (18) years commits child
defilement on a child under fourteen (14) years,
he/she is punished in accordance with the
provisions of Article 54 of this Law.
If a child aged at least fourteen (14) years
commits child defilement on a child aged
least fourteen (14) years by use of force, threats,
trickery or who does so on grounds
vulnerability of the victim, he/she is punished
in accordance with the provisions of Article
of this Law.”
Article 5: Adultery, its prosecution and
punishment
Article 136 of the Law nº 68/2018

30/08/2018 determining offences and penalties
in general is amended as follows:
“Any spouse who has sexual intercourse with
person other than his or her spouse, commits
offence. Upon conviction, he or she is liable
imprisonment for a term of not less than six (6)
months and not more than one (1) year.
Prosecution of adultery can only be initiated
upon complaint of the offended spouse. In that
case, prosecution is initiated against the accused
spouse and the co-offender.
The offended spouse may, at any stage of the
procedure, request for termination of the
proceedings when he or she changes his or her
mind and drops the complaint. The dropping
the proceedings or the execution of the
judgment has effects on the co-offender.”
Article 6: Prosecution of the offence
concubinage and desertion of the marital
home
Article 140 of the Law nº 68/2018
30/08/2018 determining offences and penalties
in general is amended as follows:
“Prosecution of the offence of concubinage and

desertion of the marital home can only
initiated upon complaint of the offended
spouse.
The offended spouse may, at any stage of the
procedure, request for termination of the
proceedings when he or she changes his or her
mind and drops the complaint. The dropping
the proceedings or the execution of the
judgment has effects on the co-offender.”
Article 7: Public defamation of religious
rituals
Article 154 of the Law nº 68/2018
30/08/2018 determining offences and penalties
in general is repealed.
Article 8: Abuse, injure or kill domestic
animals
Article 190 of the Law nº 68/2018
30/08/2018 determining offences and penalties
in general is amended as follows:
“Any person who, maliciously, abuses
transports livestock or domestic animals in
way that compromises their health, commits
offence. Upon conviction, he or she is liable
imprisonment for a term of not less than eight
(8) days and not more than two (2) months,

fine of not less than fifty thousand Rwandan
francs (FRW 50,000) and not more than one
hundred thousand Rwandan francs (FRW
100,000) and a community service in a period
not exceeding fifteen (15) days or only one
these penalties.
If the malicious abuse or transportation
livestock or domestic animals causes serious
injury or death to the livestock or domestic
animals, the penalty is an imprisonment for
term of not less than two (2) months and not
more than (6) months.
Any person who, maliciously kills or seriously
hurts livestock or domestic animals belonging
to him/her or to another person, commits
offence. Upon conviction, he/she is liable
imprisonment for a term of not less than five (5)
years and not more than seven (7) years with
fine of not less than five hundred thousand
Rwandan francs (FRW 500,000) and not more
than one million Rwandan francs (FRW
1,000,000) “

Article 11: Carrying out acts related to the
use of narcotic drugs or psychotropic
substances
Article 263 of the Law nº 68/2018
30/08/2018 determining offences and penalties
is amended as follows:
“Any person who, in any way, is caught with,
eats, drinks, injects himself/herself, inhales
one who anoints oneself with narcotic drugs
psychotropic substances, commits an offence.
Any person convicted of any of the acts
mentioned in Paragraph One of this Article
liable to imprisonment for a term of not less
than one (1) year and not more than two (2)
years or subject to a penalty of community
service.
Any person who, unlawfully produces,
transforms, transports, stores, gives to another

or who sells in the country narcotic drugs
psychotropic substances commits an offence.
Upon conviction, he/she is liable to:
1 º
life imprisonment and a fine of more
than twenty million Rwandan francs
(20,000,000 FRW) and not more than
thirty million Rwandan francs (FRW
30,000,000) in regard to severe narcotic
drugs;
2 º
imprisonment for a term of not less than
twenty (20) years and not more than
twenty five (25) years and a fine of not
less than fifteen million Rwandan francs
(FRW 15,000,000) and not more than
twenty million Rwandan francs (FRW
20,000,000) in regard to severe narcotic
drugs;
3 º
imprisonment for a term of not less than
seven (7) years and not more than ten
(10) years and a fine of not less than five
million Rwandan francs (FRW
5,000,000) and less than ten million
Rwandan francs (FRW 10,000,000)
regard to simple narcotics.
If acts mentioned in Paragraph 3 of this Article
are committed against a child or if they are

committed at the international level, the penalty
is a life imprisonment and a fine of more than
thirty million Rwandan francs (FRW
30,000,000) and not more than fifty million
Rwandan francs (FRW 50,000,000).
An Order of the Minister in charge of health
establishes a list of narcotic drugs that
constitute each category.”
