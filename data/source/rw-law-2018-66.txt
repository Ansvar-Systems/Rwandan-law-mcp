Article One: Purpose of this Law
This Law regulates labour in Rwanda.

Article
4:
Conducive
working
environment for the employee
The conducive working environment for
employee is regulated by this Law.
However, conducive working conditions
for an employee which are stipulated
individual employment contract, rules
procedure of an enterprise or collective
agreement are allowed.
Article 5: Minimum age for admission
employment
The minimum age for admission
employment is sixteen (16) years.

However, a child aged between thirteen
(13) and fifteen (15) years is allowed
perform only light works in the context
apprenticeship.
An Order of the Minister in charge of labour
establishes the list of light works for the
child provided for under Paragraph 2 of this
Article.
Article 6: Prohibited forms of work for
the child
It is prohibited to subject a child below the
age of eighteen (18) years to any of the
following forms of work:
1 º forms of work which are physically
harmful to the child;
2 º work underground, under water,
dangerous heights or in confined
spaces;
3 º work with dangerous machinery,
equipment and tools, or which
involves the manual handling
transport of heavy loads;
4 º work in an environment which
exposes the child to temperatures,

noise levels or vibrations damaging
to his/her health;
5 º work for long hours or during the
night or work performed in confined
spaces.
An Order of the Minister in charge of labour
determines the nature of prohibited forms
work for a child.
Article 7: Prohibition of forced labour
It is prohibited to impose forced labour,
permit the imposition thereof or accept,
whether directly or indirectly, that a person
be subjected to forced labour.
However, the following forms of work are
not considered forced labour:
1 º any work performed in accordance
with the Laws governing mandatory
military service;
2 º any work performed as part of
training aimed at civic and patriotic
education;
3 º any work imposed following
decision of a court and which

executed under the control of
competent public authority;
4 º any work imposed in case of the
state of siege, emergency
disasters.
Article 8:
harassment
Prohibition
of
sexual
Sexual harassment in any form against
supervisee is prohibited.
It is prohibited to dismiss an employee
having reported or testified on sexual
harassment
committed
by
his/her
supervisor.
If there is tangible evidence that
employee has resigned due to sexual
harassment committed against him/her
his/her supervisor, his/ her resignation
considered as unfair dismissal.
Article
9:
Protection
discrimination at workplace
against
An employer must give employees equal
opportunities at the workplace.

An employer is prohibited from
discriminating employees on basis of ethnic
origin, family or ancestry, clan, skin colour
or race, sex, region, economic categories,
religion or faith, opinion, fortune, cultural
difference, language, physical or mental
disability or any other form
discrimination.
Every employer must pay employees equal
salary for work of equal value without
discrimination of any kind.
Article 10: Right to freedom of opinion
and association
Employees and their representatives have
the right to express their opinions with
regard to
working conditions, work
execution and organization.
Employees have the right to form or join
trade union.
Employers have the right to form or join
employers’ associations.

CHAPTER
II:
EMPLOYMENT
CONTRACT, APPRENTICESHIP AND
INTERNSHIP CONTRACTS
Section One: Employment contract
Article 11: Conclusion of an employment
contract
An employment contract is concluded
based on the mutual consent of the
employee and employer.
Employment contracts between
employee and more than one employer are
acceptable if they are not compromising
one another.
An employment contract can be for a fixed
term or indefinite.
The employment contract can be written
unwritten.
An Order of the Minister in charge of labour
determines the core elements of written
employment contract.

However, the duration of unwritten
employment contract cannot exceed ninety
(90) consecutive days.
A proof of an employment contract can
established by any means.
Article 12: Employment of a foreign
employee
The employment relationship between the
employer and foreign employee is governed
by this Law unless otherwise provided
by international treaties ratified by Rwanda.
A foreign employee who works in Rwanda
is given a written employment contract.
Matters relating to work permit for
foreign employee who works in Rwanda are
determined by the Rwandan immigration
and emigration Laws.
The Minister in charge of labour, after
consultation with immigration and
emigration service, issues an Order
regulating employment for foreign
employees in Rwanda.

Article 13: Probation period
The probation period cannot exceed three
(3) months.
However, after the written evaluation of the
employee’s performance notified to the
employee, the employer can for valid
reasons related to the nature of work,
employee’s performance and conduct,
decides that an employee retakes the
probation for a period not exceeding three
(3) months.
If the probation period comes to an end and
proves to be conclusive, the employee
immediately offered employment and
notified in writing by the employer.
If the probation period proves that
employee is not competent based on written
performance evaluation and notified to the
employee, the employer terminates the
employment contract without notice. Apart
from the salary for which the employee has
worked for, such termination does not entail
the payment of terminal benefits.
An employee re-employed by the same
employer for the same job position is not
subject to the probation period.

Article 14: Modification of employment
contract
The employment contract can be modified
in the course of its execution at the request
of either party and upon mutual consent.
Article 15: Modification
enterprise’s status
of
the
In case of modification of the enterprise’s
legal status, employment contracts
progress on the date of the modification
remain valid between the new employer and
the existing employees of the enterprise.
However, the termination of such contracts
may only take place under the conditions
provided for by this Law.
Article 16: Transfer of employment
contract
An employment contract cannot
transferred from one employer to another
without the employee’s consent.
Article 17: Moving an employee
another position
The employee must occupy the position
or she applied for.

An employer, due to the enterprise’s
interests, can transfer an employee to
position different from the one he or she
applied for but which is at the same level
that one he or she applied for without
reducing his/her salary and other benefits.
An employer cannot transfer an employee
to another level of employment which can
result in reducing his/her level and salary
without the employee’s consent.
Article 18: Suspension of employment
contract
The suspension of employment contract
occurs when termination thereof does not
take place, but parties suspend the
performance of all or part of their
obligations.
Each party’s obligations which remain
during suspension must be provided
under this Law or agreed upon by both
parties.
The suspension of employment contract
occurs due to any of the following grounds:

1 º in case of strike or lockout carried
out in accordance with the
provisions of this Law;
2 º disciplinary layoff of an employee
for a period of eight (8) working
days;
3 º an employee is sentenced to
imprisonment not exceeding six
months or is provisionally detained
for a period not exceeding six
months;
4 º suspension of the enterprise’s
activities as a result of economic
technical difficulties;
5 º suspension
for
administrative
investigation purposes;
6 º suspension of the enterprise’s
activities due to force majeure.
Other grounds for the suspension
employment contract are set out in the
employment contract, in the rules
procedure of the enterprise and collective
agreement.

Article 19: Occupational accident
disease
An employee who has an occupational
accident or disease while his/her employer
has contributed for him/ her in a social
security body in Rwanda, he/she is entitled
to compensation in accordance with Laws
governing social security in Rwanda.
An employee having an occupational
accident or disease while the employer has
not contributed for him/her in a social
security body in Rwanda, receives from the
employer compensation equivalent to the
social benefits he/she would have received
from a social security body in Rwanda if the
employer had contributed for him/her,
including medical and related expenses.
An employee cannot be dismissed as
result of occupational accident unless
recognized doctor declares him/her unfit
resume service in the employment he/she
held prior to the accident.
If it appears that the employee is fit to work
again, the employer offers him/her another
employment suitable to his/her ability.
case of absence of such an employment, the

contract is terminated with the employee
being granted benefits provided for by Law.
Article 20: Suspension of employment
contract
due
to
administrative
investigation by an employer
When an employer is conducting
administrative investigation against
employee while such investigation can
result in suspension, the employer can
suspend the employee in writing for
period not exceeding thirty (30) days
without payment but the employee’s salary
is calculated and retained.
If, after the investigation, the employee’s
innocence is proven, the employer
reinstates the employee and pays him/her
all his/her salary that was retained
him/her.
Article 21: Suspension of employment
contract due to economic or technical
reasons
In case of suspension of employment
contract due to economic or technical
reasons, an employee cannot be laid off one
or more times for a period longer than
ninety (90) days during the same period
one (1) year.

Upon expiry of the period provided
under Paragraph One of this Article, the
employee is considered dismissed, and
receives terminal benefits provided for
this Law.
The employer may, after informing
employees’
representatives
in
the
enterprise, proceed with individual
collective dismissal due to the enterprise’s
internal reorganization or restructuring due
to economic reason or technological
transfer with the aim of preserving the
enterprise’s competitiveness and informs
writing the competent labour inspector.
In case an employer proceeds with
individual or collective dismissal on the
grounds provided for under Paragraph 3
this Article, he/she places employees on the
list of those to be dismissed based
performance, professional qualification,
experience in the enterprise and legally
recognized dependents of each employee.

Article 22: Right to be reinstated
employment after being dismissed for
economic or technical reasons
A worker dismissed for economic
technical reasons and whose dismissal does
not last more than six (6) months is entitled
to be reinstated in employment without
competition when he meets the profile
required for the position to which the
employer seeks to fill.
Article 23: Subcontract
The subcontract occurs when the employer
assigns the performance of part of work
services to another employer.
The subcontract provides for guarantee
the payment of salaries of and social
security contributions for employees and
other obligations of the employer towards
the employee and work conditions.
The subcontract between an employee and
an employer is governed by this Law.

Section 2: Termination of employment
contract and dismissal of employee
Article 24: Termination of employment
contract with notice
Subject to the provisions of the collective
agreement, rules of procedure
employment contract, the period of notice
given by the employer or employee must
as follows:
1 º at least fifteen (15) days, in case
employee has served for less than
one (1) year;
2 º at least thirty (30) days, in case
employee has served for more than
one (1) year.
The notice must be given in writing and
state reasons for termination
employment contract.
The
employer
cannot
terminate
employment contract in the following
circumstances:
1 º in case the employee is
suspension
period
of
the
employment contract;

2 º in case an employee is on a leave.
During the period of a notice given by
employer, the employee is allowed to
absent at work for one (1) day per week
look for a new job.
No notice can be given during the probation
period.
Article 25: Compensation notice
Subject to the provisions of collective
agreement, rules of procedure
employment contract, any contract
termination without notice or without
having fully observed the notice period
results in the party responsible
termination paying the other party the
compensation provided for by this Law.
Article 26: Termination of employment
contract for gross misconduct
The employer terminates employment
contract without notice in case
employee’s gross misconduct.
If employment contract is terminated
gross misconduct, the employer must notify

the employee within forty-eight (48) hours
of the occurrence of evidence of the gross
misconduct specifying the grounds
termination.
An Order of the Minister in charge of labour
establishes the list of gross misconduct.
Article 27: Termination of indefinite
term employment contract
An indefinite term employment contract
can be terminated by either party
legitimate reasons.
Article 28: Expiry and termination of
fixed-term employment contract
A fixed-term employment contract comes
to an end at the expiry of the duration
therein.
However, a fixed-term employment
contract can be terminated before the
duration set therein by mutual consent
the parties.
A party wishing to terminate a fixed-term
employment contract before the expiry

the duration set therein must
legitimate reasons for termination.
have
If a fixed-term contract is not renewed
writing while the employee continues
work, the employee is remunerated based
on worked days.
Article 29: Notification of the notice
period
The notice must be notified in writing to the
concerned party, specifying the grounds
contract termination. During the notice
period, the employer and the employee
must respect the terms agreed upon between
them.
The notice is not required if so mutually
agreed between parties.
Article 30: Damages for termination
employment contract
Unlawful termination of employment
contract gives rise to the payment
damages.
Damages paid to the employee victim
unfair dismissal cannot go below his/her
three (3) month salary, nor exceed his/her
six (6) month salary.

However, if the employee has more than ten
(10) years of experience with the same
employer, damages cannot exceed his/her
nine (9)-month net salary.
Damages provided for under Paragraph 3
this Article are also paid to employees’
representatives, occupational health and
safety committee members and trade union
representatives’ victims of unfair dismissal
as a result of the discharge of their
responsibility to represent employees.

An internship contract is a contract

An employee is entitled to an annual leave.

An employer must provide an employee

Except for provisions of the contract that

leave equals to one and a half (1½) working

An employed child aged sixteen (16) and

An employee benefits from one

An employee on annual leave continues

The annual leave schedule is drawn up

The annual leave schedule approved by the

months period, unless it is so agreed

An employer can allow an employee to
on his/her annual leave in two (2) phases.
Article 48: Interruption of annual leave
Due to work interests, an employer can
require in writing an employee who is
leave to return to work.
If an employer requires an employee

In case of employment contract termination

Provisions of Paragraph One of this Article

The right to request for an annual leave

leave.

An employee has the right to circumstantial

An employer can grant an employee

An employee has the right to official public

An employee who works on official public

When a sick leave exceeds fifteen (15) days

(6) months.

CHAPTER VIII: LABOUR DISPUTES

Article 102: Amicable settlement

The employees’ representatives amicably
settle individual labour disputes between
employers and employees.
If employees’ representatives fail to settle

the area where enterprise is located.

not been followed.

Collective labour disputes arising in the

Collective disputes extending beyond

charge of labour.
An Order of the Minister in charge of labour
determines the organisation, functioning
labour inspection and procedure for labour
disputes settlement.

A claim arising from work lapses after two
(2) years starting from the date of the birth
of the dispute giving rise to the claim.
The period referred to in Paragraph One
this Article is interrupted if:
1 º a party to the dispute is requested
writing to settle such a dispute;
2 º an employee or employer has
referred the dispute to the
employees’ representatives, labour
inspector or court.

committee has exceeded fifteen (15)
working days without issuing its decision
the conciliation resolution on collective
dispute or the court award being
enforceable have not been implemented.
In any case, a strike or lockout must
preceded by a notice of four (4) working

Article 106: Decision on illegality of
strike or lockout
The competent court is the only competent
organ to decide whether the strike
lockout is illegal.
Article 107: Employment relationship
between the employer and employee
after lawful strike or lockout
Lawful strike or lockout must not affect the
employment relationship between the
employer and employee.
An employer or an employee is not allowed
to terminate employment contract as
result of lawful strike or lockout.
Article 108: Consequences of illegal
strike
Employees on illegal strike or employees’
organisations involved in such an illegal

strike, upon conviction, are faced with one
or all of the following consequences:
1 º compensation for the damages
caused by them or for which they
are responsible;
2 º loss of remuneration in days
illegal strike;
Article 109: Consequences of illegal
lockout
An employer who illegally declares
lockout or employers’ organisations
involved in such an illegal lockout, upon
conviction, are faced with one or all of the
following consequences:
1 º to pay employees for days they were
forced out of work;
2 º to be subject to withdrawal for
(6) months the right to bid
tenders;
3 º to have his/her name deleted from
the register of companies

4 o to have his/her business suspended
temporarily or definitely
Article 110: Essential services during the
strike or lockout
The strike or lockout must not interrupt the
continuity of essential services.
An Order of the Minister in charge of labour
determines essential services that should
not be interrupted during the strike
lockout and the terms and conditions
exercising the right to strike in these
services.
CHAPTER X: LABOUR ORGANS
Article 111: Labour Administration
The Labour Administration is responsible

implementation of national policy and
legislation governing employment.

Article 112: Powers of the Director

The Director of Labour Administration has
the same powers as the Labour Inspector

Article 113: Labour Inspectorate
The Labour Inspectorate is responsible
monitoring compliance with this Law,

as well as awareness and providing advice

Article 114: Employees’ representatives

CHAPTER XII: OFFENCES AND

AND RELATED SANCTIONS
SECTION PREMIERE: OFFENCES
AND THEIR PENALTIES
Article 117: Offences and penalties
relating to prohibited work for the child
An employer who personally subjects
child to any of the prohibited forms of work
for the child referred to under Article 6
this Law, commits an offence. Upon
conviction, he/she is liable to imprisonment
for a term of not less than two (2) years and
not more than five (5) years and a fine
not less than five hundred thousand
Rwandan francs (FRW 500,000) and not
more than five million Rwandan francs
(FRW 5,000,000) or one of these penalties.
If the perpetrator of an offence referred
under Paragraph One of this Article is
company, institution, public or private

organisation and association, it is only
liable to a fine referred to under Paragraph
One of this Article which is doubled.
Article 118: Offences and penalties
relating to illegal strike or lockout
Without prejudice to provisions of Articles
108 and 109 of this Law, if the employer
a natural person who does a lockout or
employees go on strike illegally, they
commit an offence. Upon conviction, they
are liable to imprisonment for a term of not
less than six (6) months and a fine of not
less than five hundred thousand Rwandan
francs (FRW 500,000) and not more than
five million Rwandan francs (FRW
5,000,000) or one of these penalties.
If the perpetrator of an offence referred
under this Article is a company, institution,
public or private organisation and
association, it is only liable to a fine
referred to under Paragraph One of this
Article which is doubled.

Article 119: Offences and penalties
relating to occupational health and safety
If an employer is a natural person or
employee who, through clumsiness,
carelessness,
inattention,
negligence,
failure to observe the rules or any other lack
of precaution, causes danger commits
offence.
If the perpetrator of an offence referred
under Paragraph One of this Article
convicted, he/she is liable to imprisonment
for a term of not less than six (6) months
and not more than two (2) years and a fine
of not less than five hundred thousand
Rwandan francs (FRW 500,000) and not
more than two million Rwandan francs
(FRW 2,000,000) or one of these penalties.
If the perpetrator of an offence referred
under Paragraph One of this Article has
caused death and is convicted, he/she
liable to imprisonment for a term ranging
from two (2) years to five (5) years and
fine ranging from two million Rwandan
francs (FRW 2,000,000) to five million
Rwandan francs (FRW 5,000,000) or one
these penalties.
If the perpetrator of an offence referred
under this Article is a company, institution,

public or private organisation and
association, it is only liable to a fine
referred to under this Article which
doubled.
SECTION
2:
ADMINISTRATIVE
MISCONDUCT AND ITS SANCTIONS
Article 120: Obstructing the functioning
of the labour inspectorate
An employer who refuses to allow a labour
inspector to enter an enterprise, refuses
provide information with him/her, fails
report to him/her summon or implement
recommendations from a labour inspector,
commits an administrative misconduct.
He/she is liable to an administrative fine
not less than one hundred thousand
Rwandan francs (FRW 100,000) and not
more than two million Rwandan francs
(FRW 2,000,000).
Modalities for implementing sanctions
referred to under Paragraph One of this
Article are determined by an Order of the
Minister in charge of labour referred
under Article 103 of this Law.

Article 121: Temporary closure of
enterprise
Without prejudice to provisions of Articles
117, 118, 119 and 120, for the purpose
preserving national interests, the Minister
in charge of labour can decide a temporary
closure of an enterprise.

Article 122: Payment of salaries for
employees executing public or private
tenders
Without prejudice to provisions of Laws
governing public procurement, a successful
bidder of a public or private tender pays
salaries of employees employed.
A successful bidder who sub-contracts
part of the tender to a third party
responsible for payments of employees’
salaries in case the sub-contractor has not
paid employees’ salaries.
A successful bidder who is awarded
tender is not entitled to payments without
showing to the procuring entity the proof

that he/she has paid the debt related
salaries of employees employed.
In case a successful bidder fails to pay the
employees’ salaries, the procuring entity
retains the amount equivalent
employees’ salaries, until the successful
bidder proves that he/she has paid the
employees.
However, if the payment is not effected
the successful bidder in a period of forty-
five (45) days, the procuring entity pays the
concerned
employees
the
salaries
equivalent to the amount retained.
Article 123: Ongoing employment
contracts at the time of commencement
of this Law
Employment contracts that were concluded
before commencement of this Law remain
valid.
However, any clause of an employment
contract contrary to this Law must be
conformity with this Law within twelve
(12) months from the commencement
this Law.
